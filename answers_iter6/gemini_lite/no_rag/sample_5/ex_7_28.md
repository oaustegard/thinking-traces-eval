The problem describes a system with a potential $U(q) = \frac{m^2 q^2}{2} - \frac{g q^3}{6}$. This is the potential for a Euclidean cubic anharmonic oscillator. We are asked to calculate the 1-point function $G_1(t) = \langle q(t) \rangle$ and the connected 2-point function $G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = \langle q(t_1) q(t_2) \rangle_c$ in the Euclidean path integral formulation.

The expectation value of an observable $O(q)$ in the Euclidean path integral is given by:
$\langle O(q) \rangle = \frac{1}{Z} \int \mathcal{D}q \, O(q) e^{-S[q]}$
where $S[q] = \int dt \left( \frac{1}{2} (\partial_t q)^2 + U(q) \right)$ is the Euclidean action, and $Z = \int \mathcal{D}q \, e^{-S[q]}$ is the partition function.

We are interested in the behavior of these correlation functions in the presence of the anharmonic term, characterized by the coupling constant $g$. We will use perturbation theory around the harmonic oscillator part of the potential.

Let $U_0(q) = \frac{m^2 q^2}{2}$ be the harmonic part and $U_1(q) = -\frac{g q^3}{6}$ be the anharmonic perturbation.
The action can be written as $S[q] = S_0[q] + S_1[q]$, where $S_0[q] = \int dt \left( \frac{1}{2} (\partial_t q)^2 + \frac{m^2 q^2}{2} \right)$ and $S_1[q] = \int dt \left( -\frac{g q^3}{6} \right)$.

The expectation value can be expanded as a power series in $g$:
$\langle O(q) \rangle = \langle O(q) \rangle_0 + g \langle O(q) \rangle_1 + g^2 \langle O(q) \rangle_2 + \dots$
where $\langle \dots \rangle_0$ denotes the expectation value in the absence of the anharmonic term (i.e., for the harmonic oscillator).

The harmonic oscillator correlators are well-known. For instance, the 2-point function is $\langle q(t_1) q(t_2) \rangle_0 = G_2^0(t_1, t_2) = \frac{1}{m^2} D(t_1 - t_2)$, where $D(t)$ is the Euclidean propagator.
