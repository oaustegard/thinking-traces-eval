## Reasoning and Solution

**Part 1: Computing the 1-loop contribution to $\log(Z/Z_0)$**

The partition function is $Z = \int e^{-S(x)/\hbar} dx$.
$Z_0$ is the partition function for $g=0$: $S_0(x) = \tfrac{x^2}{2}$.
$Z_0 = \int e^{-x^2/(2\hbar)} dx$. This integral is formally $\sqrt{2\pi \hbar}$.

The action for the main problem is $S(x) = \frac{\mu^2}{2} x^2 - \frac{g}{6} x^3$, where $\mu^2 = 1 - 2g$.
$Z = \int e^{-(\frac{\mu^2}{2} x^2 - \frac{g}{6} x^3)/\hbar} dx$.

We are interested in the 1-loop contribution to $\log(Z/Z_0)$. This means we want the terms in $\log Z$ that are of order $\hbar^1$, after subtracting the $\log Z_0$ term.
$\log Z = \log \int e^{-\frac{1}{\hbar}S(x)} dx$.
The 1-loop contribution to $\log Z$ comes from vacuum diagrams. These are bubble diagrams where internal lines are propagators and vertices are interaction vertices.

The term $\log(Z/Z_0)$ isolates the contributions from the interaction terms. In our case, the interaction term is $-\frac{g}{6}x^3$. The $-gx^2$ term is part of the "free" part of the action when computing $Z/Z_0$, effectively changing the free propagator.

Let's use the formula for the 1-loop contribution to $\log Z$ from vacuum diagrams, which is $\frac{\hbar}{2} \text{Tr} \log(\text{Propagator Matrix})$. In zero dimensions, this becomes $\frac{\hbar}{2} \log(\text{Propagator Value})$.
The "free" action, including the modified quadratic term, is $S_{\text{free}}(x) = \frac{\mu^2}{2} x^2 = \frac{1-2g}{2} x^2$.
The free propagator $G_0$ is the inverse of the quadratic term's coefficient (times $\hbar$). In zero dimensions, this is $1/( (1-2g)/\hbar ) = \hbar/(1-2g)$.
The 1-loop contribution to $\log Z$ from vacuum diagrams is:
$\frac{1}{\hbar} \log Z_{\text{1-loop}} = \frac{1}{2} \log(\text{value of free propagator}) \times (\text{number of loops})$.
Here, the 'number of loops' is implicitly handled by the structure. The 1-loop contribution to $\log Z$ is given by the sum of all 1-loop vacuum diagrams.
The only 1-loop vacuum diagram with a $x^3$ vertex is a tadpole diagram.

The action is $S(x) = \frac{\mu^2}{2} x^2 - \frac{g}{6} x^3$.
The free propagator $G_0 = \frac{1}{\mu^2} = \frac{1}{1-2g}$.
The vertex factor for the $x^3$ term is $g$ (from $e^{gx^3/(6\hbar)}$, with $1/3!$ cancelling $3!$).
The 1-loop tadpole diagram involves one vertex and one loop. The loop is formed by contracting two legs of the vertex, with the third leg being external in the context of calculating $\log Z$. However, for vacuum diagrams, all legs are internal and contract.
The diagram consists of a single 3-valent vertex where two legs are contracted to form a loop (propagator $G_0$) and the third leg is also contracted with itself (self-contraction of a point, which is 1 in zero dimensions).

The contribution of a 1-loop tadpole diagram is:
$(-g) \times \frac{1}{\text{symmetry factor}} \times G_0 \times (\text{self-contraction})$.
The symmetry factor for a tadpole diagram with a single vertex and one loop is 2.
The self-contraction of a point is 1.
So, the 1-loop contribution to $\log Z$ is:
$\frac{1}{\hbar} \log Z_{\text{1-loop}} = \frac{1}{2} \times (-g) \times \frac{1}{2} \times G_0 = -\frac{g}{4} G_0$.
(The $1/2$ factor comes from the standard formula for 1-loop contribution $\frac{\hbar}{2} \log \det(\dots)^{-1}$).

Substituting $G_0 = \frac{1}{1-2g}$:
$\frac{1}{\hbar} \log Z_{\text{1-loop}} = -\frac{g}{4(1-2g)}$.

Now, we need $\log(Z/Z_0)$.
$Z = \int e^{-S(x)/\hbar} dx = \int e^{-(\frac{\mu^2}{2} x^2 - \frac{g}{6} x^3)/\hbar} dx$.
$Z_0 = \int e^{-\frac{1}{2\hbar} x^2} dx$.

We can write $Z = Z_0 \times \frac{\int e^{-(\frac{\mu^2}{2} x^2 - \frac{g}{6} x^3)/\hbar} dx}{\int e^{-\frac{1}{2\hbar} x^2} dx}$.
The ratio is $\frac{Z}{Z_0} = \frac{\int e^{-\frac{1}{\hbar}(\frac{1-2g}{2} x^2 - \frac{g}{6} x^3)} dx}{\int e^{-\frac{1}{2\hbar} x^2} dx}$.

Let $x = \sqrt{\hbar} y$.
$Z_0 = \int e^{-y^2/2} \sqrt{\hbar} dy = \sqrt{\hbar} \sqrt{2\pi}$.
$Z = \int e^{-\frac{1}{\hbar}(\frac{1-2g}{2} \hbar y^2 - \frac{g}{6} \hbar^{3/2} y^3)} \sqrt{\hbar} dy = \sqrt{\hbar} \int e^{-\frac{1-2g}{2} y^2 + \frac{g}{6} \hbar^{1/2} y^3} dy$.

$\frac{Z}{Z_0} = \frac{\int e^{-\frac{1-2g}{2} y^2 + \frac{g}{6} \hbar^{1/2} y^3} dy}{\sqrt{2\pi}}$.
Expanding the exponential $e^{A} \approx 1+A$ for $A = \frac{g}{6} \hbar^{1/2} y^3$:
$\frac{Z}{Z_0} \approx \frac{\int e^{-\frac{1-2g}{2} y^2} (1 + \frac{g}{6} \hbar^{1/2} y^3) dy}{\sqrt{2\pi}}$
$= \frac{\int e^{-\frac{1-2g}{2} y^2} dy}{\sqrt{2\pi}} + \frac{g}{6} \hbar^{1/2} \frac{\int y^3 e^{-\frac{1-2g}{2} y^2} dy}{\sqrt{2\pi}}$.

The first term is $1$ (since $\int e^{-ay^2/2} dy = \sqrt{2\pi/a}$, so $\int e^{-ay^2/2}/\sqrt{2\pi} = \sqrt{1/a}$). Here $a = 1-2g$, so $\sqrt{1/(1-2g)}$.
The second term: $\int y^3 e^{-ay^2/2} dy = 0$ because $y^3 e^{-ay^2/2}$ is an odd function.
This means the first-order correction in $g$ is zero. We need to go to higher order in $\hbar$.

The 1-loop contribution to $\log Z$ is $\frac{1}{2} \log(\text{propagator})$.
The propagator is $1/(1-2g)$.
So, the 1-loop contribution to $\log Z$ is $\frac{1}{2} \log\left(\frac{1}{1-2g}\right)$.

The question asks for $\log(Z/Z_0)$.
$\log(Z/Z_0) = \log Z - \log Z_0$.
$Z_0 = \int e^{-x^2/(2\hbar)} dx$. $\log Z_0 = \log(\sqrt{2\pi\hbar})$. This is a constant term and does not depend on $g$.
So, the $g$-dependent part of $\log Z$ is the 1-loop contribution.
The 1-loop contribution to $\log Z$ comes from the 1-loop vacuum diagrams.
The action is $S(x) = \frac{1-2g}{2}x^2 - \frac{g}{6}x^3$.
The free propagator is $G_0 = \frac{1}{1-2g}$.
The vertex is $g$.
The 1-loop tadpole diagram has value: $(-g) \times \frac{1}{2} \times G_0 = -\frac{g}{2(1-2g)}$.
This is the contribution to $S_{\text{eff}}^{(1)}$ at zero external legs.
The 1-loop contribution to $\log Z$ is $\frac{1}{\hbar}$ times the sum of 1-loop vacuum diagrams.
The value of the 1-loop tadpole diagram is $\frac{1}{\hbar} \times \frac{-g}{2(1-2g)}$.

This seems to imply that the 1-loop contribution to $\log Z$ is $\frac{1}{\hbar} \times (-\frac{g}{2(1-2g)})$.
However, the standard formula for $\log Z$ from vacuum diagrams is $\frac{1}{2} \text{Tr} \log(\dots)$.
In zero dimensions, this is $\frac{1}{2} \log(G_0)$.
$\frac{1}{\hbar} \log Z_{\text{1-loop}} = \frac{1}{2} \log \left(\frac{1}{1-2g}\right)$.

Let's verify this. The relation $S_{\text{eff}} = S + \hbar S_1 + \dots$ and $W = \hbar \log Z$ implies $S_{\text{eff}} = -W$.
$W(J) = \int dx e^{-S(x)/\hbar} (Jx)$. At $J=0$, $W(0) = \hbar \log Z$.
$S_{\text{eff}}(x_c) = J x_c - W(J)$. Setting $J=0$ means $x_c = 0$, so $S_{\text{eff}}(0) = -W(0)$.
The 1-loop contribution to $S_{\text{eff}}(0)$ is the sum of 1-loop vacuum diagrams.
$S_1(0) = \frac{1}{2} \log(\text{propagator})$.
For $S(x) = \frac{\mu^2}{2} x^2 - \frac{g}{6} x^3$: propagator is $1/\mu^2 = 1/(1-2g)$.
$S_1(0) = \frac{1}{2} \log \left(\frac{1}{1-2g}\right)$.
Since $W(0) = \hbar \log Z$, the 1-loop contribution to $\log Z$ is $\frac{1}{\hbar} S_1(0) = \frac{1}{2\hbar} \log\left(\frac{1}{1-2g}\right)$.

So, the 1-loop contribution to $\log(Z/Z_0)$ is $\frac{1}{2\hbar} \log\left(\frac{1}{1-2g}\right)$.
This is because $\log Z_0$ is a constant with respect to $g$ (it's $\log(\sqrt{2\pi\hbar})$), so $\log(Z/Z_0) = \log Z - \log Z_0$ means the $g$-dependent part is just the $g$-dependent part of $\log Z$.
