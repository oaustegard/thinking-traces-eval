## Solution to the Main Problem

**Part 1: 1-loop contribution to $\log(Z/Z_0)$**

The partition function is $Z = \int e^{-S(x)/\hbar} dx$. $Z_0$ is the partition function for $g=0$.
$S(x) = \tfrac{x^2}{2} - g x^2 - \tfrac{g x^3}{6}$.
Let $\hbar=1$ for simplicity in this calculation.

The 1-loop contribution to $\log Z$ is given by $\tfrac{1}{2} \log \det(\text{Hessian})$, where the Hessian is evaluated at the classical saddle point. For $S(x) = \tfrac{x^2}{2} - g(x^2 + \tfrac{x^3}{6})$, the classical equation of motion is $S'(x) = x - 2gx - \tfrac{g x^2}{2} = 0$.
The vacuum solution ($x=0$) is $0=0$. So we evaluate the Hessian at $x=0$.

The Hessian of $S(x)$ is $S''(x) = \frac{d^2S}{dx^2} = 1 - 2g - g x$.
Evaluating at $x=0$: $S''(0) = 1 - 2g$.

The 1-loop contribution to $\log Z$ is:
$$ \frac{1}{2} \log \det(S''(0)) = \frac{1}{2} \log(1 - 2g). $$
This is the 1-loop contribution to $\log Z$. Since $Z_0$ corresponds to the free theory ($g=0$), where $S_0(x) = x^2/2$, $Z_0 = \int e^{-x^2/2} dx = \sqrt{2\pi}$. The 1-loop contribution to $\log(Z/Z_0)$ is the same as the 1-loop contribution to $\log Z$, because $Z_0$ is a constant and does not have any $g$-dependent 1-loop corrections.

Thus, the 1-loop contribution to $\log(Z/Z_0)$ is $\frac{1}{2} \log(1 - 2g)$.

This can be expanded as a series:
$$ \frac{1}{2} \log(1 - 2g) = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{(-2g)^k}{k} = \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^k 2^{k-1} g^k}{k}. $$
