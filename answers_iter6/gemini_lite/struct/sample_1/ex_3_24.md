**Part 1: 1-Loop Contribution to $\log(Z/Z_0)$**

The partition function is given by $Z = \int dx \, e^{-S(x)/\hbar}$.
The free partition function is $Z_0 = \int dx \, e^{-S_0(x)/\hbar}$, where $S_0(x) = \frac{x^2}{2}$.
The generating functional for connected diagrams is $W[J] = \log Z[J] = \log \int dx \, e^{-(S(x) - Jx)/\hbar}$.
The 1-loop contribution to $\log Z$ is given by the sum of all 1-loop diagrams. This is often calculated using the formula:
$\log Z = \log Z_0 + \log \left( \int dx \, e^{-(S_0(x) + S_{\text{int}}(x))/\hbar} \right)$
$\log Z = \log Z_0 + \log \left( \int dx \, e^{-S_0(x)/\hbar} e^{-S_{\text{int}}(x)/\hbar} \right)$
$\log Z = \log Z_0 + \log \left( Z_0 \int dx \, e^{-S_0(x)/\hbar} \frac{e^{-S_{\text{int}}(x)/\hbar}}{e^{-S_0(x)/\hbar}} \right)$
$\log Z = \log Z_0 + \log Z_0 + \log \left\langle e^{-S_{\text{int}}(x)/\hbar} \right\rangle_0$
where $\langle \cdot \rangle_0$ denotes the average with respect to the free theory measure $e^{-S_0(x)/\hbar}$.

Using the Taylor expansion of the exponential:
$\log Z = \log Z_0 + \log \left( 1 + \left\langle -\frac{S_{\text{int}}(x)}{\hbar} \right\rangle_0 + \frac{1}{2!} \left\langle \left(-\frac{S_{\text{int}}(x)}{\hbar}\right)^2 \right\rangle_0 + \dots \right)$

The 1-loop contribution to $\log(Z/Z_0)$ is the term linear in $g$:
$\log(Z/Z_0) \approx \left\langle -\frac{S_{\text{int}}(x)}{\hbar} \right\rangle_0$

The interaction part of the action is $S_{\text{int}}(x) = g(x^2 + \frac{x^3}{6})$.
The free action is $S_0(x) = \frac{x^2}{2}$.
The free propagator is found by considering the equation of motion for the free field or by the second derivative of $S_0(x)$. $S_0''(x) = 1$.
In the context of a single field $x$, the "propagator" can be thought of as related to $\frac{1}{S_0''(x)}$. For a quantum field theory, this would be $G_0(p) \sim \frac{1}{p^2 + m^2}$ or similar. Here, we are in a very simple one-dimensional integral case. The expectation value $\langle x^n \rangle_0$ can be calculated.

Let's consider the average of $x^2$ and $x^3$ under the measure $e^{-x^2/(2\hbar)}$.
The generating function for moments is $\int dx \, e^{-x^2/(2\hbar)} e^{Jx} = \sqrt{2\pi\hbar} e^{J^2\hbar/2}$.
Differentiating with respect to $J$:
$\langle x \rangle_0 = 0$
$\langle x^2 \rangle_0 = \hbar$
$\langle x^3 \rangle_0 = 0$
$\langle x^4 \rangle_0 = 3\hbar^2$

So, the 1-loop contribution is:
$\left\langle -\frac{g}{\hbar}(x^2 + \frac{x^3}{6}) \right\rangle_0 = -\frac{g}{\hbar} (\langle x^2 \rangle_0 + \frac{1}{6}\langle x^3 \rangle_0)$
$= -\frac{g}{\hbar} (\hbar + \frac{1}{6} \cdot 0) = -g$

This is the contribution to $\log(Z/Z_0)$.
