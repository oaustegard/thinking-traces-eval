**Part (b): Reasoning and Solution for the Main Problem.**

The Euclidean action is $S[q] = \int dt \bigl(\tfrac12 \dot q^2 + \tfrac{m^2}{2} q^2 - \tfrac{g}{6} q^3\bigr)$.
The interaction part is $S_{\text{int}}[q] = \int dt (-\tfrac{g}{6} q^3)$.
The free propagator is $G_0(t_1, t_2) = \langle q(t_1) q(t_2)\rangle_0 = \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|}$.
The Feynman rules for connected functions $G_N^c(t_1, \dots, t_N) = \frac{\delta^N \ln Z[J]}{\delta J(t_1)\dots\delta J(t_N)}|_{J=0}$:
*   Each internal line (propagator) carries $G_0(t_i, t_j)$.
*   Each 3-valent vertex carries a factor of $g$ and an integration over its time coordinate $t_v$. (The $1/3!$ from the action cancels the $3!$ ways to connect legs to the vertex.)

---

**(i) Calculate the leading term of the 1-point function $G_1(t) = \langle q(t) \rangle$ with respect to $g$.**

The 1-point function $G_1(t)$ is the sum of all connected Feynman diagrams with one external leg.
The leading term is of order $g^1$.

*   **Order $g^0$:** No vertices; no connected diagram with one external leg exists. So $G_1^{(0)}(t) = 0$.
*   **Order $g^1$:** One vertex. The vertex is 3-valent. One leg is external (to $t$). The remaining two legs must connect to form a closed loop.
    *   **Diagram:** A 3-valent vertex at $t_v$. One leg connects to the external point $t$. The other two legs form a loop, connected to the vertex at $t_v$.
    *   **Vertex factor:** $g$.
    *   **Propagators:** One external leg (implicitly $1$) and one internal loop $G_0(t_v, t_v)$.
    *   **Symmetry factor:** The two internal legs forming the loop are indistinguishable, leading to a symmetry factor of $S=2$ for the loop.
    *   **Calculation:**
        $G_1^{(1)}(t) = \frac{g}{S} \int dt_v \, \delta(t - t_v) \, G_0(t_v, t_v)$.
        $G_1^{(1)}(t) = \frac{g}{2} \, G_0(t, t)$.
        Using $G_0(t, t) = \frac{1}{2m}$:
        $G_1^{(1)}(t) = \frac{g}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{2m} = \frac{g}{4m}$.

Thus, the leading term of the 1-point function is:
$$\boxed{\;G_1(t) = \frac{g}{4m} + O(g^2).\;}$$

**(ii) Calculate the connected 2-point function $G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = \langle q(t_1) q(t_2) \rangle_c$ modulo $g^3$ (i.e., up to and including order $g^2$).**

The connected 2-point function $G_2^c(t_1, t_2)$ is the sum of all connected Feynman diagrams with two external legs (to $t_1$ and $t_2$).

*   **Order $g^0$:** No vertices.
    *   **Diagram:** A single free propagator connecting $t_1$ and $t_2$.
    *   **Calculation:**
        $G_2^{c, (0)}(t_1, t_2) = G_0(t_1, t_2) = \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|}$.

*   **Order $g^1$:** One vertex. The vertex is 3-valent. Two legs are external (to $t_1, t_2$). The remaining one leg must form a closed loop.
    *   **Diagram:** A 3-valent vertex at $t_v$. One leg to $t_1$, one leg to $t_2$, and the third leg forms a loop at $t_v$.
    *   **Vertex factor:** $g$.
    *   **Propagators:** Two external legs (implicitly $1$) and one internal loop $G_0(t_v, t_v)$.
    *   **Symmetry factor:** $S=1$ (the external legs are distinct, and the internal loop has no further symmetry elements relative to the rest of the diagram).
    *   **Calculation:**
        $G_2^{c, (1)}(t_1, t_2) = g \int dt_v \, \delta(t_1 - t_v) \, \delta(t_2 - t_v) \, G_0(t_v, t_v)$.
        $G_2^{c, (1)}(t_1, t_2) = g \, G_0(t_1, t_1) \, \delta(t_1 - t_2)$.
        Using $G_0(t, t) = \frac{1}{2m}$:
        $G_2^{c, (1)}(t_1, t_2) = \frac{g}{2m} \, \delta(t_1 - t_2)$.

*   **Order $g^2$:** Two vertices.
    We need to identify all connected diagrams with two vertices and two external legs.
    1.  **Double-bubble diagram:** Two 3-valent vertices ($V_A, V_B$). One external leg to $t_1$ from $V_A$, one external leg to $t_2$ from $V_B$. The remaining two legs from $V_A$ connect to $V_B$, and the remaining two legs from $V_B$ connect to $V_A$, forming two parallel propagators between $V_A$ and $V_B$.
        *   **Vertex factors:** $g^2$.
        *   **Propagators:** Two external legs and two internal propagators $G_0(t_A, t_B)$ between the vertices.
        *   **Symmetry factor:** The two internal propagators connecting $V_A$ and $V_B$ are indistinguishable, leading to $S=2$.
        *   **Calculation:**
            $G_{2, \text{double-bubble}}^{c, (2)}(t_1, t_2) = \frac{g^2}{2} \int dt_A dt_B \, \delta(t_1 - t_A) \, [G_0(t_A, t_B)]^2 \, \delta(t_B - t_2)$.
            $= \frac{g^2}{2} [G_0(t_1, t_2)]^2 = \frac{g^2}{2} \left(\frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|}\right)^2$.
            $= \frac{g^2}{8m^2} e^{-2m|t_1 - t_2|}$.

    2.  **Two-tadpole-chain diagram:** Two 3-valent vertices ($V_A, V_B$). One external leg to $t_1$ from $V_A$, one external leg to $t_2$ from $V_B$. One propagator connects $V_A$ and $V_B$. The third leg of $V_A$ forms a tadpole at $V_A$, and the third leg of $V_B$ forms a tadpole at $V_B$.
        *   **Vertex factors:** $g^2$.
        *   **Propagators:** Two external legs, one internal propagator $G_0(t_A, t_B)$, one tadpole $G_0(t_A, t_A)$, and another tadpole $G_0(t_B, t_B)$.
        *   **Symmetry factor:** $S=1$ (all internal lines are distinguishable, and vertices $V_A, V_B$ are distinct due to external legs).
        *   **Calculation:**
            $G_{2, \text{two-tadpole-chain}}^{c, (2)}(t_1, t_2) = g^2 \int dt_A dt_B \, \delta(t_1 - t_A) \, G_0(t_A, t_A) \, G_0(t_A, t_B) \, G_0(t_B, t_B) \, \delta(t_B - t_2)$.
            $= g^2 \, G_0(t_1, t_1) \, G_0(t_1, t_2) \, G_0(t_2, t_2)$.
            $= g^2 \left(\frac{1}{2m}\right) \left(\frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|}\right) \left(\frac{1}{2m}\right)$.
            $= \frac{g^2}{8m^3} e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|}$.

    3.  **Propeller-with-tadpole diagram:** One 3-valent vertex ($V_A$) connected to both external legs ($t_1, t_2$). The third leg of $V_A$ connects to a second 3-valent vertex ($V_B$). $V_B$ has a tadpole from two of its legs, and its third leg connects back to $V_A$.
        *   **Vertex factors:** $g^2$.
        *   **Propagators:** Two external legs from $V_A$, two propagators connecting $V_A$ and $V_B$ ($G_0(t_A,t_B)$), and one tadpole $G_0(t_B,t_B)$ at $V_B$.
        *   **Symmetry factor:** $S=1$.
        *   **Calculation:**
            $G_{2, \text{propeller-tadpole}}^{c, (2)}(t_1, t_2) = g^2 \int dt_A dt_B \, \delta(t_1 - t_A) \, \delta(t_2 - t_A) \, G_0(t_A, t_B) \, G_0(t_B, t_B) \, G_0(t_B, t_A)$.
            $= g^2 \, \delta(t_1 - t_2) \int dt_B \, G_0(t_1, t_B) \, G_0(t_B, t_B) \, G_0(t_B, t_1)$.
            Using $G_0(t_B, t_B) = \frac{1}{2m}$ and $\int dt_B G_0(t_1, t_B) G_0(t_B, t_1) = \frac{1}{4m^3}$.
            $= g^2 \, \delta(t_1 - t_2) \left(\frac{1}{2m}\right) \left(\frac{1}{4m^3}\right)$.
            $= \frac{g^2}{8m^4} \delta(t_1 - t_2)$.

Combining all terms up to $O(g^2)$:

$$G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|} \;+\; \frac{g}{2m} \delta(t_1 - t_2) \;+\; \frac{g^2}{8m^2} e^{-2m|t_1 - t_2|} \;+\; \frac{g^2}{8m^3} e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|} \;+\; \frac{g^2}{8m^4} \delta(t_1 - t_2) \;+\; O(g^3).$$

The final answer is:
$$\boxed{\;G_1(t) = \frac{g}{4m} + O(g^2).\;}$$
$$\boxed{\;G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|} + \frac{g}{2m} \delta(t_1 - t_2) + \frac{g^2}{8m^2} e^{-2m|t_1 - t_2|} + \frac{g^2}{8m^3} e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|} + \frac{g^2}{8m^4} \delta(t_1 - t_2) + O(g^3).\;}$$
