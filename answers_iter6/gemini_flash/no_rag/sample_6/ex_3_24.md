The problem asks for two things:
1.  Compute the 1-loop contribution to $\log(Z/Z_0)$ for $S(x) = x^2/2 - g(x^2 + x^3/6)$.
2.  Use this result to compute the number of labeled $n$-vertex 1-loop graphs with 1-valent and 3-valent vertices only.

Let's address each part carefully.

**Part 1: 1-loop contribution to $\log(Z/Z_0)$**

The partition function is $Z = \int e^{-S(x)/\hbar} dx$. The free partition function $Z_0 = \int e^{-S_0(x)/\hbar} dx$ with $S_0(x) = x^2/2$.
We can write $S(x) = S_0(x) - S_{int}(x)$, where $S_{int}(x) = g(x^2 + x^3/6)$.
Then $\log(Z/Z_0) = \log\left(\frac{1}{Z_0} \int e^{-S_0(x)/\hbar} e^{S_{int}(x)/\hbar} dx\right)$.
This can be expanded using the cumulant expansion for $\log \langle e^A \rangle_0 = \sum_{k=1}^\infty \frac{1}{k!} \langle A^k \rangle_{0,c}$, where $A = S_{int}(x)/\hbar$.
$A = g(x^2/\hbar + x^3/(6\hbar))$.
The average $\langle \cdot \rangle_0$ is taken with respect to the Gaussian measure $e^{-x^2/(2\hbar)}/Z_0$.
The moments of this Gaussian are $\langle x^{2k} \rangle_0 = (2k-1)!! \hbar^k$ and $\langle x^{2k+1} \rangle_0 = 0$.
Specifically, $\langle x^2 \rangle_0 = \hbar$, $\langle x^4 \rangle_0 = 3\hbar^2$, $\langle x^6 \rangle_0 = 15\hbar^3$.

Let's compute the terms in the cumulant expansion up to $O(g^2)$:
$\log(Z/Z_0) = \langle A \rangle_0 + \frac{1}{2}(\langle A^2 \rangle_0 - \langle A \rangle_0^2) + O(g^3)$.

1.  **First order in $g$**:
    $\langle A \rangle_0 = g\left(\frac{\langle x^2 \rangle_0}{\hbar} + \frac{\langle x^3 \rangle_0}{6\hbar}\right) = g\left(\frac{\hbar}{\hbar} + 0\right) = g$.

2.  **Second order in $g$**:
    We need $\langle A^2 \rangle_0 - \langle A \rangle_0^2$.
    $\langle A \rangle_0^2 = g^2$.
    $\langle A^2 \rangle_0 = g^2 \left\langle \left(\frac{x^2}{\hbar} + \frac{x^3}{6\hbar}\right)^2 \right\rangle_0$
    $= g^2 \left\langle \frac{x^4}{\hbar^2} + \frac{x^5}{3\hbar^2} + \frac{x^6}{36\hbar^2} \right\rangle_0$
    $= g^2 \left(\frac{\langle x^4 \rangle_0}{\hbar^2} + \frac{\langle x^5 \rangle_0}{3\hbar^2} + \frac{\langle x^6 \rangle_0}{36\hbar^2}\right)$
    $= g^2 \left(\frac{3\hbar^2}{\hbar^2} + 0 + \frac{15\hbar^3}{36\hbar^2}\right) = g^2 \left(3 + \frac{15\hbar}{36}\right) = g^2 \left(3 + \frac{5\hbar}{12}\right)$.

    So, $\frac{1}{2}(\langle A^2 \rangle_0 - \langle A \rangle_0^2) = \frac{1}{2}\left[g^2 \left(3 + \frac{5\hbar}{12}\right) - g^2\right]$
    $= \frac{1}{2} g^2 \left(2 + \frac{5\hbar}{12}\right) = g^2 + \frac{5g^2\hbar}{24}$.

Combining these terms, we get:
$\log(Z/Z_0) = g + g^2 + \frac{5g^2\hbar}{24} + O(g^3)$.

The term "1-loop contribution" typically refers to contributions proportional to $\hbar^1$ in the effective action or $\log Z$. However, in the context of Feynman diagrams, it refers to diagrams with exactly one loop ($L=E-V+1=1$). Let's use the latter interpretation, as it's more relevant for graph enumeration.

The interaction terms are $S_{int}(x) = g x^2 + g x^3/6$. This means we have 2-valent vertices (from $g x^2$) and 3-valent vertices (from $g x^3/6$). The terms in $\log(Z/Z_0)$ correspond to sums of connected vacuum diagrams.
*   The term $g$ comes from a single 2-valent vertex whose two legs are connected, forming a loop. This is a graph with $V=1$ vertex and $E=1$ edge. $L = E-V+1 = 1-1+1 = 1$. This is a 1-loop graph.
*   The term $g^2$ comes from two 2-valent vertices. The connected part $\langle (g x^2/\hbar)^2 \rangle_{0,c}$ is $g^2 (3-1) = 2g^2$. The factor of $1/2$ from the cumulant formula gives $g^2$. This graph has $V=2$ vertices (both 2-valent) and $E=2$ edges (a double edge between them). $L = E-V+1 = 2-2+1=1$. This is also a 1-loop graph.
*   The term $\frac{5g^2\hbar}{24}$ comes from two 3-valent vertices. This graph has $V=2$ vertices (both 3-valent) and $E=3$ edges (three edges connecting them). $L = E-V+1 = 3-2+1=2$. This is a 2-loop graph.

Therefore, the 1-loop contribution (sum of diagrams with $L=1$) to $\log(Z/Z_0)$ is $g+g^2$.

**Part 2: Number of labeled n-vertex 1-loop graphs**

The problem specifies "1-valent and 3-valent vertices only". This contradicts the interaction terms in the action ($g x^2$ and $g x^3/6$), which correspond to 2-valent and 3-valent vertices. Given that the problem asks to "use this [Part 1] to compute", we must assume a typo in the graph description and that it refers to **2-valent and 3-valent vertices only**, consistent with the action.

We are looking for connected vacuum graphs with exactly one loop ($L=1$).
For any connected graph, the sum of degrees of its vertices is $2E$.
For a 1-loop graph, $L=E-V+1=1$, which implies $E=V$.
Thus, the sum of degrees is $2V$.
If a graph contains $V_2$ 2-valent vertices and $V_3$ 3-valent vertices, then $V = V_2 + V_3$.
The sum of degrees is $2V_2 + 3V_3$.
So, $2V_2 + 3V_3 = 2(V_2 + V_3) = 2V_2 + 2V_3$.
This equation implies $3V_3 = 2V_3$, which means $V_3=0$.
Therefore, a 1-loop vacuum graph can only consist of 2-valent vertices. The 3-valent vertices cannot form part of a 1-loop vacuum graph.

This simplifies the problem: we only need to consider $n$-vertex 1-loop graphs with 2-valent vertices.
The only connected graph with $n$ vertices, all 2-valent, and $E=n$ edges is an $n$-cycle graph, $C_n$.

From Part 1, the 1-loop contribution to $\log(Z/Z_0)$ is $g+g^2$.
The coefficients of $g^n$ in this expansion (after accounting for $\hbar$ factors) represent the sum of contributions from all distinct labeled $n$-vertex 1-loop graphs. The calculation of the cumulants already includes the combinatorial factors for connecting legs, which corresponds to the number of ways to form a labeled graph.

*   **For $n=1$**: The coefficient of $g^1$ is 1.
    This corresponds to a graph with one 2-valent vertex. The only such graph is a self-loop (a $C_1$ graph).
    There is only **1** way to label a single vertex. So, for $n=1$, there is 1 labeled 1-loop graph.

*   **For $n=2$**: The coefficient of $g^2$ is 1.
    This contribution arises from $\frac{1}{2\hbar^2} (g^2 \langle x^4 \rangle_0 - g^2 \langle x^2 \rangle_0^2) = \frac{1}{2\hbar^2} (g^2 (3\hbar^2) - g^2 (\hbar^2)) = \frac{1}{2\hbar^2} (2g^2\hbar^2) = g^2$.
    The factor of 2 in $2\hbar^2$ (from $\langle x^4 \rangle_0 - \langle x^2 \rangle_0^2 = 2\hbar^2$) directly represents the number of ways to connect two labeled 2-valent vertices ($v_1, v_2$) to form a connected graph (a $C_2$ graph, or a "double edge"). If $v_1$ has legs $(x_{1a}, x_{1b})$ and $v_2$ has legs $(x_{2a}, x_{2b})$, the two ways are $(x_{1a}-x_{2a}, x_{1b}-x_{2b})$ and $(x_{1a}-x_{2b}, x_{1b}-x_{2a})$.
    So, for $n=2$, there are **2** labeled 1-loop graphs.

*   **For $n \ge 3$**: The 1-loop contribution $g+g^2$ has no terms of order $g^n$ for $n \ge 3$.
    This means that higher cycles like $C_3$, $C_4$, etc., made of only $x^2$ interactions, do not contribute in this 0D field theory. (Specifically, $\langle x^{2n} \rangle_c = 0$ for $n \ge 3$ when only quadratic interactions are considered).
    So, for $n \ge 3$, there are **0** labeled 1-loop graphs.

To check for small numbers of vertices:
*   $n=1$: A single vertex labeled '1'. Its two legs are connected to form a loop. This is 1 graph.
*   $n=2$: Two vertices labeled '1' and '2'. Each has two legs. We need to connect them with two edges to form a loop.
    Let vertex 1 have legs $a,b$ and vertex 2 have legs $c,d$.
    Connection 1: $a-c, b-d$.
    Connection 2: $a-d, b-c$.
    These are two distinct labeled graphs.
*   $n=3$: Three vertices labeled '1', '2', '3'. Each has two legs. We need to connect them to form a triangle. This would be a $C_3$ graph. However, the calculation showed that such a graph does not contribute (coefficient of $g^3$ is 0).

Final Answer:
The 1-loop contribution to $\log(Z/Z_0)$ is $g+g^2$.
The number of labeled $n$-vertex 1-loop graphs (with 2-valent and 3-valent vertices, which reduces to 2-valent only for 1-loop graphs) is:
*   For $n=1$: 1 graph
*   For $n=2$: 2 graphs
*   For $n \ge 3$: 0 graphs

The final answer is $\boxed{
\text{1-loop contribution to log(Z/Z_0): } g+g^2 \\
\text{Number of labeled n-vertex 1-loop graphs:} \\
\quad n=1: 1 \\
\quad n=2: 2 \\
\quad n \ge 3: 0
}$
