The problem asks for two things:
1.  Compute the 1-loop contribution to $\log(Z/Z_0)$ for the given action $S(x)$.
2.  Use this result to compute the number of labeled n-vertex 1-loop graphs with 1-valent and 3-valent vertices.

**Part 1: 1-loop contribution to $\log(Z/Z_0)$**

The partition function is given by $Z = \int e^{-S(x)/\hbar} dx$, and the free partition function by $Z_0 = \int e^{-S_0(x)/\hbar} dx$, where $S_0(x)$ is $S(x)$ at $g=0$.
From $S(x) = x^2/2 - g(x^2 + x^3/6)$, the free action is $S_0(x) = x^2/2$.
The interaction action is $S_{int}(x) = -g(x^2 + x^3/6)$.

First, let's compute $Z_0$:
$Z_0 = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-x^2/(2\hbar)} dx = \sqrt{2\pi\hbar}$.

Next, we expand $Z$ around $Z_0$:
$Z = \int e^{-S_0(x)/\hbar} e^{-S_{int}(x)/\hbar} dx = Z_0 \left\langle e^{-S_{int}(x)/\hbar} \right\rangle_0$,
where $\langle \cdot \rangle_0$ denotes the expectation value with respect to the free action $S_0(x)$.
$S_{int}(x)/\hbar = -g(x^2 + x^3/6)/\hbar = (g/\hbar)x^2 + (g/(6\hbar))x^3$.

We use the cumulant expansion for $\log(Z/Z_0)$:
$\log(Z/Z_0) = \left\langle -S_{int}/\hbar \right\rangle_0 + \frac{1}{2} \left( \left\langle (-S_{int}/\hbar)^2 \right\rangle_0 - \left\langle -S_{int}/\hbar \right\rangle_0^2 \right) + O(g^3)$.

We need the Gaussian expectation values $\langle x^n \rangle_0 = \frac{1}{Z_0} \int x^n e^{-x^2/(2\hbar)} dx$:
$\langle x^n \rangle_0 = 0$ for odd $n$.
$\langle x^2 \rangle_0 = \hbar$.
$\langle x^4 \rangle_0 = 3\hbar^2$.
$\langle x^6 \rangle_0 = 15\hbar^3$.

Let's compute the terms:
1.  $\left\langle -S_{int}/\hbar \right\rangle_0 = \left\langle (g/\hbar)x^2 + (g/(6\hbar))x^3 \right\rangle_0$
    $= (g/\hbar)\langle x^2 \rangle_0 + (g/(6\hbar))\langle x^3 \rangle_0 = (g/\hbar)\hbar + 0 = g$.
    This term is independent of $\hbar$, so it's a tree-level (0-loop in QFT sense) contribution.

2.  $\left\langle (-S_{int}/\hbar)^2 \right\rangle_0 = \left\langle ((g/\hbar)x^2 + (g/(6\hbar))x^3)^2 \right\rangle_0$
    $= \left\langle (g/\hbar)^2 x^4 + 2(g/\hbar)(g/(6\hbar))x^5 + (g/(6\hbar))^2 x^6 \right\rangle_0$
    $= (g^2/\hbar^2)\langle x^4 \rangle_0 + (g^2/(3\hbar^2))\langle x^5 \rangle_0 + (g^2/(36\hbar^2))\langle x^6 \rangle_0$
    $= (g^2/\hbar^2)(3\hbar^2) + 0 + (g^2/(36\hbar^2))(15\hbar^3)$
    $= 3g^2 + \frac{15}{36}g^2\hbar = 3g^2 + \frac{5}{12}g^2\hbar$.

3.  $\left\langle -S_{int}/\hbar \right\rangle_0^2 = g^2$.

Now, substitute these into the cumulant expansion for $\log(Z/Z_0)$:
$\log(Z/Z_0) = g + \frac{1}{2} \left( (3g^2 + \frac{5}{12}g^2\hbar) - g^2 \right) + O(g^3, \hbar^2)$
$\log(Z/Z_0) = g + \frac{1}{2} \left( 2g^2 + \frac{5}{12}g^2\hbar \right) + O(g^3, \hbar^2)$
$\log(Z/Z_0) = g + g^2 + \frac{5}{24}g^2\hbar + O(g^3, \hbar^2)$.

In 0D QFT, the loop expansion corresponds to powers of $\hbar$. The tree-level contribution is independent of $\hbar$ (i.e., $\hbar^0$), and the 1-loop contribution is proportional to $\hbar^1$.
Therefore, the 1-loop contribution to $\log(Z/Z_0)$ is $\frac{5}{24}g^2\hbar$.

**Part 2: Number of labeled n-vertex 1-loop graphs**

The problem asks for "n-vertex 1-loop graphs with 1-valent and 3-valent vertices only".
The "1-loop" in this context refers to the $\hbar^1$ term we just calculated.
The term $\frac{5}{24}g^2\hbar$ originates from the $(g/(6\hbar))x^3$ part of $-S_{int}/\hbar$. This corresponds to 3-valent vertices. The "1-valent" description in the problem statement does not contribute to this $\hbar^1$ term (as shown in thought process, 1-valent interactions would lead to $\hbar^0$ terms). Thus, we consider only 3-valent vertices.

The term $\frac{5}{24}g^2\hbar$ arises from the expansion of $\left\langle (-S_{int}/\hbar)^2 \right\rangle_0$, specifically from the $\left\langle ((g/(6\hbar))x^3)^2 \right\rangle_0$ part, which is $\frac{1}{2!} (g/(6\hbar))^2 \langle x^6 \rangle_0$.
This corresponds to a diagram with two 3-valent vertices (so $n=2$).
The number of labeled graphs is obtained by considering the number of distinct Wick contractions that lead to this connected diagram.
For the term $\langle x^6 \rangle_0$, there are $5!! = 1 \times 3 \times 5 = 15$ ways to pair the six $x$ variables.
These 15 pairings correspond to distinct ways of connecting the legs of two 3-valent vertices (let's call them A and B). Each pairing represents a unique labeled graph.

Let's enumerate these 15 labeled graphs explicitly for $n=2$ (two 3-valent vertices A and B):
Each vertex has 3 "legs" or ports. We need to connect these $3+3=6$ legs in pairs using 3 propagators.

1.  **Graphs with no self-loops (all edges connect A to B):**
    Vertex A has legs $(A_1, A_2, A_3)$, and vertex B has legs $(B_1, B_2, B_3)$.
    We connect $A_1$ to any of B's legs (3 choices).
    Then $A_2$ to any of the remaining 2 legs of B (2 choices).
    Then $A_3$ to the last remaining leg of B (1 choice).
    This gives $3 \times 2 \times 1 = 3! = 6$ distinct ways to connect the legs. These 6 graphs have 2 labeled vertices and 3 edges between them.

2.  **Graphs with self-loops (each vertex has a self-loop, and one edge connects A to B):**
    For vertex A: Choose 2 legs out of 3 to form a self-loop ($\binom{3}{2}=3$ ways). The remaining leg must connect to B.
    For vertex B: Choose 2 legs out of 3 to form a self-loop ($\binom{3}{2}=3$ ways). The remaining leg must connect to A.
    The remaining single leg from A connects to the remaining single leg from B (1 way).
    This gives $3 \times 3 \times 1 = 9$ distinct ways to form such graphs. These 9 graphs have 2 labeled vertices, one edge between them, and a self-loop on each vertex.

Total number of labeled graphs = $6 + 9 = 15$.

The final answer is $\boxed{\text{1-loop contribution to log(Z/Z_0) is } \frac{5}{24}g^2\hbar. \text{The number of labeled 2-vertex 1-loop graphs is 15.}}$
