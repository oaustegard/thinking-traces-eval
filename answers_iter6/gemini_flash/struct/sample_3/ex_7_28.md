The problem asks us to calculate the leading term of the 1-point function and the connected 2-point function up to order $g^2$ for a Euclidean cubic anharmonic oscillator.

**Relevant Examples and Differences:**

*   **Example 1** (Quantum particle with $U(q) = \tfrac{m^2 q^2}{2} + \tfrac{g q^4}{24}$) is highly relevant. It demonstrates the methodology for calculating correlation functions using Feynman diagrams in Euclidean quantum field theory, including setting up the action, free propagator, vertex rules, drawing diagrams, calculating symmetry factors, and performing time-domain integrals.
*   **Key Differences:**
    1.  **Interaction Term:** Example 1 has a $q^4$ interaction, leading to a 4-valent vertex. The main problem has a $q^3$ interaction, leading to a 3-valent vertex. This changes the Feynman rules and the types of diagrams.
    2.  **Quantity Calculated:** Example 1 calculates the 1-particle irreducible (1PI) 2-point function $\Sigma$. The main problem asks for the 1-point function $G_1(t) = \langle q(t) \rangle$ and the *connected* 2-point function $G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = \langle q(t_1) q(t_2) \rangle_c$.
    3.  **Order of Calculation:** Example 1 calculates $\Sigma$ up to $O(g^2)$. The main problem asks for $G_1$ to leading order in $g$ and $G_2^c$ up to $O(g^2)$.

**Step 1: Establish Euclidean Action and Feynman Rules**

The Euclidean action is $S[q] = \int dt \left(\frac12 \dot q^2 + \frac{m^2}{2} q^2 - \frac{g}{6} q^3\right)$.
The free part of the action is $S_0[q] = \int dt \left(\frac12 \dot q^2 + \frac{m^2}{2} q^2\right)$.
The interaction part is $S_I[q] = \int dt \left(-\frac{g}{6} q^3\right)$.

**Feynman Rules:**
*   **Free Propagator:** $G_0(t_1, t_2) = \langle q(t_1) q(t_2) \rangle_0 = \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|}$.
*   **Vertex:** Each $q^3$ vertex carries a factor of $+g$. (From the expansion $e^{-S_I} = 1 - S_I + \dots = 1 - \int dt (-\frac{g}{6} q^3) + \dots = 1 + \int dt \frac{g}{6} q^3 + \dots$. The $1/3!$ is absorbed by Wick's theorem counting factors).
*   **Integration:** Integrate over all internal time variables.
*   **Loop Factor:** For a closed loop of identical propagators, include a $1/2$ symmetry factor. (This is usually handled by Wick contractions, as shown below).

**Useful Integrals:**
Let $L = G_0(t_v, t_v) = \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_v-t_v|} = \frac{1}{2m}$.
1.  $\int dt_v G_0(t, t_v) = \frac{1}{2m} \int dt_v e^{-m|t-t_v|} = \frac{1}{2m} \left( \int_{-\infty}^t e^{-m(t-t_v)} dt_v + \int_t^\infty e^{-m(t_v-t)} dt_v \right) = \frac{1}{2m} \left( \frac{1}{m} + \frac{1}{m} \right) = \frac{1}{m^2}$.
2.  $I_2(t_1, t_2) = \int dt_v G_0(t_1, t_v) G_0(t_v, t_2) = \frac{1}{4m^2} (1+m|t_1-t_2|)e^{-m|t_1-t_2|}$.
3.  $I_3(t_1, t_2) = \int dt_v dt_w G_0(t_1, t_v) G_0(t_v, t_w) G_0(t_w, t_2) = \frac{1}{8m^3} (1+m|t_1-t_2|+\frac{1}{3}m^2(t_1-t_2)^2)e^{-m|t_1-t_2|}$.

**Part (i): Calculate $G_1(t) = \langle q(t) \rangle$ to leading order in $g$.**

$G_1(t)$ is the sum of all connected diagrams with one external leg at time $t$.
*   **Order $g^0$:** No vertices. $\langle q(t) \rangle_0 = 0$.
*   **Order $g^1$:** There is one connected diagram with one vertex (3-valent) and one external leg. The remaining two legs must form a loop (a "tadpole").
    *   Diagram: An external leg from $t$ connects to a vertex at $t_v$. Two legs from $t_v$ connect to each other forming a loop.
    *   From the expansion $\langle q(t) \rangle = \frac{\langle q(t) e^{-S_I} \rangle_0}{\langle e^{-S_I} \rangle_0} \approx \langle q(t) (1 + \int dt_v \frac{g}{6} q^3(t_v)) \rangle_0$.
    *   The $O(g^1)$ term is $\frac{g}{6} \int dt_v \langle q(t) q(t_v) q(t_v) q(t_v) \rangle_0$.
    *   Number of Wick contractions: $q(t)$ contracts with one $q(t_v)$ (3 ways). The remaining two $q(t_v)$ contract with each other (1 way). Total $3 \times 1 = 3$ ways.
    *   $G_1^{(1)}(t) = \frac{g}{6} \times 3 \times \int dt_v G_0(t, t_v) G_0(t_v, t_v)$.
    *   $G_1^{(1)}(t) = \frac{g}{2} \int dt_v G_0(t, t_v) L$.
    *   Using the useful integrals: $G_1^{(1)}(t) = \frac{g}{2} \times \frac{1}{m^2} \times \frac{1}{2m} = \frac{g}{4m^3}$.
    *   This is the leading (first non-zero) term.

**Answer for (i):** $\boxed{G_1(t) = \frac{g}{4m^3} + O(g^2)}$

---

**Part (ii): Calculate $G_2^c(t_1, t_2)$ modulo $g^3$ (i.e. up to and including order $g^2$).**

$G_2^c(t_1, t_2)$ is the sum of all connected diagrams with two external legs at $t_1$ and $t_2$.

*   **Order $g^0$:** The free propagator.
    *   $G_2^{c,(0)}(t_1, t_2) = G_0(t_1, t_2) = \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_1-t_2|}$.

*   **Order $g^1$:** One vertex.
    *   Diagram: A vertex at $t_v$ connects to $t_1$ and $t_2$. The third leg of the vertex forms a loop. This is a self-energy insertion.
    *   From the expansion $\langle q(t_1) q(t_2) \rangle_c \approx \langle q(t_1) q(t_2) (1 + \int dt_v \frac{g}{6} q^3(t_v)) \rangle_0^c$.
    *   The $O(g^1)$ term is $\frac{g}{6} \int dt_v \langle q(t_1) q(t_2) q(t_v) q(t_v) q(t_v) \rangle_0^c$.
    *   Number of Wick contractions: $q(t_1)$ to one $q(t_v)$ (3 ways). $q(t_2)$ to another $q(t_v)$ (2 ways). The remaining $q(t_v)$ to itself (1 way). Total $3 \times 2 \times 1 = 6$ ways.
    *   $G_2^{c,(1)}(t_1, t_2) = \frac{g}{6} \times 6 \times \int dt_v G_0(t_1, t_v) G_0(t_v, t_2) G_0(t_v, t_v)$.
    *   $G_2^{c,(1)}(t_1, t_2) = g L \int dt_v G_0(t_1, t_v) G_0(t_v, t_2) = g L I_2(t_1, t_2)$.
    *   $G_2^{c,(1)}(t_1, t_2) = g \times \frac{1}{2m} \times \frac{1}{4m^2} (1+m|t_1-t_2|)e^{-m|t_1-t_2|}$.
    *   $G_2^{c,(1)}(t_1, t_2) = \frac{g}{8m^3} (1+m|t_1-t_2|)e^{-m|t_1-t_2|}$.

*   **Order $g^2$:** Two vertices.
    *   The $O(g^2)$ term is $\frac{1}{2!} \left(\frac{g}{6}\right)^2 \int dt_a dt_b \langle q(t_1) q(t_2) q^3(t_a) q^3(t_b) \rangle_0^c = \frac{g^2}{72} \int dt_a dt_b \langle q(t_1) q(t_2) q^3(t_a) q^3(t_b) \rangle_0^c$.
    *   We need to identify all connected diagrams with two external legs and two 3-valent vertices. For each, we count the number of Wick contractions.
    *   **Diagram 1: "Tadpole-ladder"**
        *   Two vertices $V_a, V_b$ are connected by one propagator $G_0(t_a, t_b)$. Each vertex also has a loop $G_0(t_a, t_a)$ and $G_0(t_b, t_b)$. The external legs connect to $V_a$ and $V_b$.
        *   Number of contractions: $q(t_1)$ to $q(t_a)$ (3 ways). $q(t_2)$ to $q(t_b)$ (3 ways). One remaining $q(t_a)$ to one $q(t_b)$ (2x2 ways). The last $q(t_a)$ to itself (1 way). The last $q(t_b)$ to itself (1 way). Total $3 \times 3 \times 2 \times 2 \times 1 \times 1 = 36$ ways.
        *   Contribution: $\frac{g^2}{72} \times 36 \times \int dt_a dt_b G_0(t_1, t_a) G_0(t_a, t_a) G_0(t_a, t_b) G_0(t_b, t_b) G_0(t_b, t_2)$.
        *   $= \frac{g^2}{2} L^2 \int dt_a dt_b G_0(t_1, t_a) G_0(t_a, t_b) G_0(t_b, t_2) = \frac{g^2}{2} L^2 I_3(t_1, t_2)$.
        *   $= \frac{g^2}{2} \left(\frac{1}{2m}\right)^2 \frac{1}{8m^3} (1+m|t_1-t_2|+\frac{1}{3}m^2(t_1-t_2)^2)e^{-m|t_1-t_2|}$.
        *   $= \frac{g^2}{64m^5} (1+m|t_1-t_2|+\frac{1}{3}m^2(t_1-t_2)^2)e^{-m|t_1-t_2|}$.

    *   **Diagram 2: "Two-rung ladder"**
        *   Two vertices $V_a, V_b$ are connected by *two* propagators $G_0(t_a, t_b)^2$. The external legs connect to $V_a$ and $V_b$.
        *   Number of contractions: $q(t_1)$ to $q(t_a)$ (3 ways). $q(t_2)$ to $q(t_b)$ (3 ways). The remaining two $q(t_a)$ connect to the remaining two $q(t_b)$ (2x2 ways for the first pair, 1x1 for the second). Total $3 \times 3 \times 2 \times 2 \times 1 \times 1 = 36$ ways.
        *   Contribution: $\frac{g^2}{72} \times 36 \times \int dt_a dt_b G_0(t_1, t_a) [G_0(t_a, t_b)]^2 G_0(t_b, t_2)$.
        *   Let $G_0^{(2)}(t) = [G_0(t)]^2 = \frac{1}{4m^2} e^{-2m|t|}$. This can be written as $G_0^{(2)}(t) = \frac{1}{m} G_0'(t)$, where $G_0'(t) = \frac{1}{4m} e^{-2m|t|}$ is a free propagator with mass $2m$.
        *   The integral is $\frac{g^2}{2m} \int dt_a dt_b G_0(t_1, t_a) G_0'(t_a, t_b) G_0(t_b, t_2)$.
        *   This is $\frac{g^2}{2m}$ times the triple convolution $G_0 * G_0' * G_0$. In momentum space, this is $\frac{g^2}{2m} \tilde{G}_0(p) \tilde{G}_0'(p) \tilde{G}_0(p) = \frac{g^2}{2m} \frac{1}{(p^2+m^2)} \frac{1}{(p^2+(2m)^2)} \frac{1}{(p^2+m^2)} = \frac{g^2}{2m} \frac{1}{(p^2+m^2)^2 (p^2+4m^2)}$.
        *   Using partial fraction decomposition and inverse Fourier transforms:
            $\mathcal{F}^{-1}\left[ \frac{1}{(p^2+m^2)^2 (p^2+4m^2)} \right] = \frac{1}{72m^6} \left[ (3m|t|-1)e^{-m|t|} + 2 e^{-2m|t|} \right]$.
        *   So, the contribution is $\frac{g^2}{2m} \times \frac{1}{72m^6} \left[ (3m|t_1-t_2|-1)e^{-m|t_1-t_2|} + 2 e^{-2m|t_1-t_2|} \right]$.
        *   $= \frac{g^2}{144m^7} \left[ (3m|t_1-t_2|-1)e^{-m|t_1-t_2|} + 2 e^{-2m|t_1-t_2|} \right]$.

    *   **Diagram 3: "Self-energy with tadpole insertion"**
        *   A vertex $V_a$ connects to $t_1$ and $t_2$. The third leg of $V_a$ connects to $V_b$. The vertex $V_b$ forms a loop with its two remaining legs.
        *   Number of contractions: $q(t_1)$ to $q(t_a)$ (3 ways). $q(t_2)$ to $q(t_a)$ (2 ways). The remaining $q(t_a)$ to one $q(t_b)$ (1x3 ways). The remaining two $q(t_b)$ form a loop (2x1 ways). Total $3 \times 2 \times 1 \times 3 \times 2 \times 1 = 36$ ways.
        *   Contribution: $\frac{g^2}{72} \times 36 \times \int dt_a dt_b G_0(t_1, t_a) G_0(t_a, t_2) G_0(t_a, t_b) G_0(t_b, t_b)$.
        *   $= \frac{g^2}{2} L \int dt_a G_0(t_1, t_a) G_0(t_a, t_2) \left( \int dt_b G_0(t_a, t_b) \right)$.
        *   Using $\int dt_b G_0(t_a, t_b) = \frac{1}{m^2}$:
        *   $= \frac{g^2}{2} L \frac{1}{m^2} I_2(t_1, t_2) = \frac{g^2}{2} \frac{1}{2m} \frac{1}{m^2} I_2(t_1, t_2)$.
        *   $= \frac{g^2}{4m^3} \frac{1}{4m^2} (1+m|t_1-t_2|)e^{-m|t_1-t_2|}$.
        *   $= \frac{g^2}{16m^5} (1+m|t_1-t_2|)e^{-m|t_1-t_2|}$.

**Answer for (ii):**
$\boxed{G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_1-t_2|} + \frac{g}{8m^3} (1+m|t_1-t_2|)e^{-m|t_1-t_2|} \\ \quad + \frac{g^2}{64m^5} (1+m|t_1-t_2|+\frac{1}{3}m^2(t_1-t_2)^2)e^{-m|t_1-t_2|} \\ \quad + \frac{g^2}{144m^7} \left[ (3m|t_1-t_2|-1)e^{-m|t_1-t_2|} + 2 e^{-2m|t_1-t_2|} \right] \\ \quad + \frac{g^2}{16m^5} (1+m|t_1-t_2|)e^{-m|t_1-t_2|} + O(g^3)}$
