The problem asks for two quantities in a Euclidean cubic anharmonic oscillator theory with potential $U(q) = \tfrac{m^2 q^2}{2} - \tfrac{g q^3}{6}$. The Euclidean action is $S[q] = \int dt \bigl(\tfrac12 \dot q^2 + \tfrac{m^2}{2} q^2 - \tfrac{g}{6} q^3\bigr)$. The interaction part is $S_{int}[q] = \int dt (-\tfrac{g}{6}) q^3(t)$.

**Relevant Examples and Differences:**
Example 1 is highly relevant. It demonstrates the calculation of Feynman diagrams for an anharmonic oscillator in Euclidean space, including establishing the free propagator, Feynman rules, and calculating contributions order by order in the coupling constant.
The key differences are:
1.  **Interaction Term:** Example 1 has a $q^4$ interaction, leading to a vertex factor of $-g$. Our problem has a $q^3$ interaction. The coefficient of $q^3/3!$ in $S_{int}$ is $-g/6$. Thus, the Feynman rule for a $q^3$ vertex is a factor of $+g$.
2.  **Quantities Calculated:** Example 1 calculates the 1-particle irreducible (1PI) 2-point function $\Sigma(t_1, t_2)$. This problem asks for the 1-point function $\langle q(t) \rangle$ and the *connected* 2-point function $\langle q(t_1) q(t_2) \rangle_c$. The 1-point function is non-zero due to the odd-power interaction.

**Step 1: Establish Feynman Rules and Free Propagator**
The free Euclidean action is $S_0[q] = \int dt \bigl(\tfrac12 \dot q^2 + \tfrac{m^2}{2} q^2\bigr)$.
The free propagator (Green's function) is $G_0(t_1, t_2) = \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|}$.
The interaction term is $S_{int} = \int dt \left(-\frac{g}{6}\right) q^3(t)$.
The Feynman rule for a $q^3$ vertex is a factor of $+g$.

**Part (i): Calculate the leading term of the 1-point function $G_1(t) := \langle q(t) \rangle$ with respect to $g$.**

The 1-point function $\langle q(t) \rangle$ is given by the sum of all connected diagrams with one external leg. The lowest order non-zero contribution comes from $O(g^1)$.

**Step 1.1: Draw the leading order diagram**
At $O(g^1)$, we have one external leg $q(t)$ and one $q^3$ vertex at time $t'$. One leg from the vertex connects to $q(t)$. The remaining two legs from the vertex must connect to each other to form a loop (a tadpole diagram).

**Step 1.2: Calculate the diagram's contribution**
The diagram consists of:
*   One vertex with factor $g$.
*   One propagator $G_0(t, t')$ connecting the external leg to the vertex.
*   One internal propagator $G_0(t', t')$ forming the loop.
*   We integrate over the internal vertex time $t'$.
*   The symmetry factor for this diagram (one external leg, one internal loop, from a $q^3$ vertex) is $1/2! = 1/2$ because the two internal legs forming the loop are indistinguishable.

So, $G_1(t) = \int dt' \left(g \cdot G_0(t, t') \cdot G_0(t', t') \cdot \frac{1}{2}\right) + O(g^2)$.
$G_1(t) = \frac{g}{2} \int dt' G_0(t, t') G_0(t', t')$.

**Step 1.3: Evaluate the integral**
We use $G_0(t_1, t_2) = \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|}$.
$G_0(t', t') = \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|0|} = \frac{1}{2m}$. This is the "tadpole value".
The integral $\int dt' G_0(t, t')$:
$\int_{-\infty}^\infty \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t - t'|} dt' = \frac{1}{2m} \left( \int_{-\infty}^t e^{-m(t - t')} dt' + \int_t^\infty e^{-m(t' - t)} dt' \right)$
$= \frac{1}{2m} \left( \frac{1}{m} + \frac{1}{m} \right) = \frac{1}{m^2}$.

Substituting these values:
$G_1(t) = \frac{g}{2} \left( \frac{1}{2m} \right) \left( \frac{1}{m^2} \right) = \frac{g}{4m^3}$.
The 1-point function is a constant, as expected for a time-independent potential.

**Part (ii): Calculate the connected 2-point function $G_2^c(t_1, t_2) := \langle q(t_1) q(t_2) \rangle_c$ modulo $g^3$.**

The connected 2-point function is the sum of all connected diagrams with two external legs (at $t_1$ and $t_2$).

**Step 2.1: $O(g^0)$ contribution**
The leading term is the free propagator:
$G_2^c(t_1, t_2)|_{O(g^0)} = G_0(t_1, t_2) = \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|}$.

**Step 2.2: $O(g^1)$ contribution**
At $O(g^1)$, there is one vertex at $t'$. Two legs connect to the external fields $q(t_1)$ and $q(t_2)$. The third leg must form a loop (tadpole).
*   One vertex with factor $g$.
*   Propagators $G_0(t_1, t')$, $G_0(t_2, t')$, $G_0(t', t')$.
*   We integrate over $t'$.
*   The symmetry factor for this diagram (two external legs, one internal loop, from a $q^3$ vertex) is $1$. The external legs are distinct, and the internal loop is fixed.

So, $G_2^c(t_1, t_2)|_{O(g^1)} = g \int dt' G_0(t_1, t') G_0(t_2, t') G_0(t', t')$.
Using $G_0(t', t') = \frac{1}{2m}$:
$= \frac{g}{2m} \int dt' G_0(t_1, t') G_0(t_2, t')$.
The integral $\int dt' G_0(t_1, t') G_0(t_2, t')$:
$\int_{-\infty}^\infty \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_1 - t'|} \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_2 - t'|} dt' = \frac{1}{4m^2} \int_{-\infty}^\infty e^{-m(|t_1 - t'| + |t_2 - t'|)} dt'$.
This integral evaluates to $\frac{1}{4m^2} e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|} \left( \frac{1}{m} + |t_1 - t_2| \right)$.
So, $G_2^c(t_1, t_2)|_{O(g^1)} = \frac{g}{2m} \frac{1}{4m^2} e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|} \left( \frac{1}{m} + |t_1 - t_2| \right)$
$= \frac{g}{8m^3} e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|} \left( \frac{1}{m} + |t_1 - t_2| \right)$.

**Step 2.3: $O(g^2)$ contributions**
There are two types of connected diagrams with two external legs and two vertices.
Let $\tau = |t_1 - t_2|$.

**Diagram 1: Two tadpoles linked by a propagator**
This diagram has $t_1$ connected to $V_1(t_a)$, which is connected to $V_2(t_b)$, which is connected to $t_2$. Both $V_1$ and $V_2$ have a tadpole loop.
*   Two vertices, each with factor $g$, so $g^2$.
*   Propagators: $G_0(t_1, t_a)$, $G_0(t_a, t_b)$, $G_0(t_b, t_2)$, $G_0(t_a, t_a)$, $G_0(t_b, t_b)$.
*   Integrate over $t_a, t_b$.
*   Symmetry factor $S_D = 1$ (no non-trivial automorphisms).

So, $D_1 = g^2 \int dt_a dt_b G_0(t_1, t_a) G_0(t_a, t_b) G_0(t_b, t_2) G_0(t_a, t_a) G_0(t_b, t_b)$.
Using $G_0(t_a, t_a) = G_0(t_b, t_b) = \frac{1}{2m}$:
$D_1 = g^2 \left(\frac{1}{2m}\right)^2 \int dt_a dt_b G_0(t_1, t_a) G_0(t_a, t_b) G_0(t_b, t_2)$.
The integral is a convolution of three propagators, which in momentum space is $\int \frac{dp}{2\pi} e^{ip(t_1-t_2)} G_0(p)^3$.
The real-space result for this is $\frac{e^{-m|\tau|}}{16m^5} (2m^2\tau^2 + 6m\tau + 3)$.
So, $D_1 = g^2 \frac{1}{4m^2} \frac{e^{-m\tau}}{16m^5} (2m^2\tau^2 + 6m\tau + 3)$
$= \frac{g^2}{64m^7} e^{-m\tau} (2m^2\tau^2 + 6m\tau + 3)$.

**Diagram 2: "Bubble" diagram**
This diagram has $t_1$ connected to $V_1(t_a)$, which is connected to $V_2(t_b)$ by two internal propagators. $V_2$ is connected to $t_2$.
*   Two vertices, each with factor $g$, so $g^2$.
*   Propagators: $G_0(t_1, t_a)$, $G_0(t_b, t_2)$, and two $G_0(t_a, t_b)$ propagators.
*   Integrate over $t_a, t_b$.
*   Symmetry factor $S_D = 2$ because the two internal lines connecting $V_1$ and $V_2$ are indistinguishable.

So, $D_2 = \frac{g^2}{2} \int dt_a dt_b G_0(t_1, t_a) [G_0(t_a, t_b)]^2 G_0(t_b, t_2)$.
In momentum space, this corresponds to $\frac{g^2}{2} \int \frac{dp}{2\pi} e^{ip\tau} G_0(p) \left( \int \frac{dq}{2\pi} G_0(q) G_0(p-q) \right) G_0(p)$.
Let $H(t) = [G_0(t)]^2 = \frac{1}{4m^2} e^{-2m|t|}$. Its Fourier transform is $H(p) = \frac{1}{m(p^2+4m^2)}$.
The integral in momentum space is $\int \frac{dp}{2\pi} e^{ip\tau} G_0(p) H(p) G_0(p) = \int \frac{dp}{2\pi} e^{ip\tau} \frac{1}{(p^2+m^2)} \frac{1}{m(p^2+4m^2)} \frac{1}{(p^2+m^2)}$.
The inverse Fourier transform of $\frac{1}{(p^2+m^2)^2 (p^2+4m^2)}$ is $\frac{1}{m} \left[ \frac{7}{54m^5} e^{-m\tau} - \frac{1}{12m^5} e^{-m\tau} (1+m\tau) + \frac{1}{36m^5} e^{-2m\tau} \right]$.
So, $D_2 = \frac{g^2}{2} \frac{1}{m} \left[ \frac{7}{54m^5} e^{-m\tau} - \frac{1}{12m^5} e^{-m\tau} (1+m\tau) + \frac{1}{36m^5} e^{-2m\tau} \right]$
$= \frac{g^2}{2m^6} \left[ \left( \frac{7}{54} - \frac{1}{12}(1+m\tau) \right) e^{-m\tau} + \frac{1}{36} e^{-2m\tau} \right]$
$= \frac{g^2}{2m^6} \left[ \left( \frac{14 - 9(1+m\tau)}{108} \right) e^{-m\tau} + \frac{1}{36} e^{-2m\tau} \right]$
$= \frac{g^2}{216m^6} (5 - 9m\tau) e^{-m\tau} + \frac{g^2}{72m^6} e^{-2m\tau}$.

**Step 2.4: Combine all terms for $G_2^c(t_1, t_2)$**
$G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = G_0(t_1, t_2) + G_2^c(t_1, t_2)|_{O(g^1)} + D_1 + D_2 + O(g^3)$.
Let $\tau = |t_1 - t_2|$.
$G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m\tau}$
$+ \frac{g}{8m^3} e^{-m\tau} \left( \frac{1}{m} + \tau \right)$
$+ \frac{g^2}{64m^7} e^{-m\tau} (2m^2\tau^2 + 6m\tau + 3)$
$+ \frac{g^2}{216m^6} e^{-m\tau} (5 - 9m\tau)$
$+ \frac{g^2}{72m^6} e^{-2m\tau} + O(g^3)$.

The final answer is $\boxed{
\begin{aligned}
\text{(i) } \langle q(t) \rangle &= \frac{g}{4m^3} + O(g^2) \\
\text{(ii) } \langle q(t_1) q(t_2) \rangle_c &= \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_1-t_2|} \\
&+ \frac{g}{8m^3} e^{-m|t_1-t_2|} \left( \frac{1}{m} + |t_1-t_2| \right) \\
&+ \frac{g^2}{64m^7} e^{-m|t_1-t_2|} (2m^2|t_1-t_2|^2 + 6m|t_1-t_2| + 3) \\
&+ \frac{g^2}{216m^6} e^{-m|t_1-t_2|} (5 - 9m|t_1-t_2|) \\
&+ \frac{g^2}{72m^6} e^{-2m|t_1-t_2|} + O(g^3)
\end{aligned}
}$
