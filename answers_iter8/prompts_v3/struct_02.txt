Example 1: **Approach:** Numerical Example

**Problem:** Determine the percentage of chocolates that are vanilla flavored and not squashed, given that 10% are cocoa flavored and 90% of the non-cocoa flavored chocolates are squashed.

**Step 1 — Choose a Total:** Assume a total of 100 chocolates.

**Step 2 — Calculate Cocoa Chocolates:** Determine the number of cocoa flavored chocolates: 10% of 100 = 10.

**Step 3 — Calculate Vanilla Chocolates:** Compute the number of vanilla flavored chocolates: 100 - 10 = 90.

**Step 4 — Calculate Squashed Vanilla Chocolates:** Determine the number of squashed vanilla chocolates: 90% of 90 = 81.

**Step 5 — Calculate Not Squashed Vanilla Chocolates:** Compute the number of not squashed vanilla chocolates: 90 - 81 = 9.

**Step 6 — Calculate Percentage:** Calculate the percentage of chocolates that are vanilla and not squashed: (9 / 100) * 100% = 9%.

**Step 7 — State the Answer:** The percentage is 9%.

**Answer:** $\boxed{9\%}$
