Example 3: **Approach:** Product and Sum Relationships

**Problem:** Find the sum of the integers in the third row of a $3 \times 3$ grid containing the integers from $1$ to $9$ exactly once, given that the product of the first row is 60 and the product of the second row is 96.

**Step 1 — Compute the Total Product:**
Calculate the product of all the numbers from $1$ to $9$, which is $9! = 362880$.

**Step 2 — Determine the Product of the Third Row:**
Divide the product of all integers ($9!$) by the product of the first row ($60$) and the second row ($96$): $362880 / (60 \times 96) = 362880 / 5760 = 63$.

**Step 3 — Find the Factors of the Third Row Product:**
Determine the distinct integers from $1$ to $9$ that multiply to $63$. The prime factorization of $63$ is $3^2 \times 7$. Thus, the numbers are $1, 7, 9$ because $1 \times 7 \times 9 = 63$.

**Step 4 — Calculate the Sum:**
Add the integers in the third row: $1 + 7 + 9 = 17$.
