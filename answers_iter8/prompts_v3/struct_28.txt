Example 3: **Approach:** Coordinate Geometry and Area Decomposition

**Problem:** Find the area of quadrilateral $ADBC$ given that $A$, $B$, and $C$ are vertices of a right triangle with $\angle A = 30^\circ$, $\angle B = 60^\circ$, $\angle C = 90^\circ$, and $BC = 1$.  $A$ is at the origin, $B$ is on the positive x-axis, and $D$ lies on the angle bisector of $\angle C$.

**Step 1 — Determine Vertex Coordinates:** Assign coordinates: $A=(0,0)$. Since $\angle A = 30^\circ$, $\angle B = 60^\circ$, and $BC=1$, and B is on x-axis: $B = (\sqrt{3},0)$ and $C=(\sqrt{3},1)$.

**Step 2 — Calculate Angle Bisector Equation:**  The angle bisector of $\angle C$ makes an angle of $60^\circ$ with the positive x-axis. Thus, its slope is $\sqrt{3}$, and it passes through $C(\sqrt{3}, 1)$.  Equation: $y - 1 = \sqrt{3}(x - \sqrt{3})$ or $y = \sqrt{3}x - 2$.

**Step 3 — Find D's Coordinates:** Since $D$ is the intersection of the angle bisector with the y-axis, set $x=0$ in the bisector equation. This gives $y = -2$. Thus, $D = (0, -2)$.

**Step 4 — Decompose the Quadrilateral into Triangles:** Divide the quadrilateral $ADBC$ into $\triangle ABC$ and $\triangle ABD$ using the common side $AB$.

**Step 5 — Calculate Area of $\triangle ABC$:** Use base and height for right triangle: Area($\triangle ABC$) = $\frac{1}{2} \cdot AB \cdot BC = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \sqrt{3} \cdot 1 = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$.

**Step 6 — Calculate Area of $\triangle ABD$:** Use the base AB and height, the y-coordinate of D, which is $2$.  Area($\triangle ABD$) = $\frac{1}{2} \cdot AB \cdot |y_D| = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \sqrt{3} \cdot 2 = \sqrt{3}$.

**Step 7 — Sum the Areas:** Area($ADBC$) = Area($\triangle ABC$) + Area($\triangle ABD$) = $\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} + \sqrt{3} = \frac{3\sqrt{3}}{2}$.

**Answer:** $\boxed{\frac{3\sqrt{3}}{2}}$
