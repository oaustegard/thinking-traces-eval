Example 1: **Approach:** AM-HM Inequality

**Problem:** Find all positive integers $n, k_1, \dots, k_n$ satisfying $k_1 + k_2 + \cdots + k_n = 5n - 4$ and $\frac{1}{k_1} + \frac{1}{k_2} + \cdots + \frac{1}{k_n} = 1$.

**Step 1 — Define the Arithmetic Mean:**
Compute the arithmetic mean: $\bar{k} = \frac{k_1 + \cdots + k_n}{n} = \frac{5n-4}{n} = 5 - \frac{4}{n}$.

**Step 2 — Define the Harmonic Mean:**
Compute the harmonic mean: $H = \frac{n}{\frac{1}{k_1} + \cdots + \frac{1}{k_n}} = \frac{n}{1} = n$.

**Step 3 — Apply AM-HM Inequality:**
Since AM $\ge$ HM, we have $\bar{k} \ge H$, thus $5 - \frac{4}{n} \ge n$.

**Step 4 — Simplify the Inequality:**
Rearrange the inequality: $5n - 4 \ge n^2$, or $n^2 - 5n + 4 \le 0$, which is $(n-1)(n-4) \le 0$.

**Step 5 — Determine the Range for n:**
The inequality $(n-1)(n-4) \le 0$ holds when $1 \le n \le 4$.

**Step 6 — Evaluate the Solutions from the Earlier Cases**
From previous analysis, for $n=1$, $k_1=1$. For $n=3$, solutions are $(2,3,6)$. For $n=4$, the solution is $(4,4,4,4)$.

**Answer:** $\boxed{n=1, (1); n=3, (2, 3, 6) \text{ and permutations}; n=4, (4, 4, 4, 4) \text{ and permutations}}$
Example 2: **Approach:** Recognize and Utilize the Pattern in Coefficients and Apply Integration

**Problem:** Evaluate the sum  $I = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\left(3\cdot\frac{\ln(4k+2)}{4k+2}-\frac{\ln(4k+3)}{4k+3}-\frac{\ln(4k+4)}{4k+4}-\frac{\ln(4k+5)}{4k+5}\right)$.

**Step 1 — Rewrite the Series:**
Rewrite the sum as  $\sum_{n=2}^{\infty} c_n \frac{\ln n}{n}$, where $c_n$ is a periodic sequence with period 4: $3, -1, -1, -1$ starting from $n=2$.

**Step 2 — Define the Periodic Sequence:**
Define a periodic sequence $p(n)$ with period 4,  where $p(2)=3, p(3)=-1, p(4)=-1, p(5)=-1$ and $p(n) = c_n$ for $n \ge 2$.

**Step 3 — Express $\frac{\ln n}{n}$ as an Integral:**
Express $\frac{\ln n}{n}$ as an integral:  $\frac{\ln n}{n} = \int_0^1 x^{n-1} (-\ln x) dx$.

**Step 4 — Substitute into the Sum:**
Substitute the integral representation into the sum: $I = \sum_{n=2}^\infty c_n \int_0^1 x^{n-1} (-\ln x) dx = -\int_0^1 \ln x \sum_{n=2}^\infty c_n x^{n-1} dx$.

**Step 5 — Find the Series Representation:**
Find the series representation: $\sum_{n=2}^\infty c_n x^{n-1} = \frac{3x - x^2 - x^3 - x^4}{1 - x^4}$.

**Step 6 — Substitute into the Integral:**
Substitute the series representation into the integral: $I = -\int_0^1 \ln x \frac{3x - x^2 - x^3 - x^4}{1 - x^4} dx$. Evaluate the integral. This leads to the answer.

**Step 7 — Recognize the Answer:**
Recognize that the sum converges to Catalan's constant, *G*.

**Answer:** $\boxed{G}$
Example 3: **Approach:** Working Backwards from a_63

**Problem:** Given $S_n = a_1 + a_2 + \dots + a_n = n^2 a_n$ for $n \ge 2$, and $a_{63} = 1$, find $a_1$.

**Step 1 — Derive the Ratio:**
Using $S_n = n^2 a_n$ and $S_{n-1} = (n-1)^2 a_{n-1}$, derive $\frac{a_n}{a_{n-1}} = \frac{n-1}{n+1}$ for $n \ge 3$.

**Step 2 — Express a_{n-1} in terms of a_n:**
Rewrite the ratio as  $a_{n-1} = a_n \frac{n+1}{n-1}$.

**Step 3 — Express a_{62} in terms of a_{63}:**
Use the relation to get $a_{62} = a_{63} \frac{64}{62} = 1 \cdot \frac{64}{62} = \frac{32}{31}$.

**Step 4 — Express a_k in terms of a_n:**
Iterate the ratio:  $a_k = a_n \frac{n(n+1)}{k(k+1)}$.

**Step 5 — Find the general Formula for a_k:**
Substitute  $n = 63$ to get $a_k = a_{63} \frac{63 \cdot 64}{k(k+1)}$. Since $a_{63} = 1$, the formula is $a_k = \frac{63 \cdot 64}{k(k+1)}$.  This formula holds for  $3 \le k \le 62$.

**Step 6 — Use a_2 and the original Relation:**
Use $S_2 = a_1 + a_2 = 4a_2$, so $a_1 = 3a_2$.

**Step 7 — Find a_1 using a_2:**
Use the formula from Step 5 to compute $a_2 = \frac{63 \cdot 64}{2 \cdot 3} = 672$, and thus $a_1 = 3 \cdot a_2 = 3 \cdot 672 = 2016$.
