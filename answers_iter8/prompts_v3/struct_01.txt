Example 1: **Approach:** Trigonometry and Right Triangles

**Problem:** Find the area of rectangle $ABCD$, given that $\angle C$ is trisected by $\overline{CF}$ and $\overline{CE}$, with $E$ on $\overline{AB}$ and $F$ on $\overline{AD}$. Also, $BE=6$ and $AF=2$.

**Step 1 ‚Äî Recognize Angle Trisectors and Right Triangles:**
Identify that $\angle BCE = \angle DCF = 30^\circ$ and triangles $\triangle BCE$ and $\triangle DCF$ are right triangles.

**Step 2 ‚Äî Calculate $BC$ using $\triangle BCE$:**
Apply $\tan(\angle BCE) = \frac{BE}{BC}$, so $\tan(30^\circ) = \frac{6}{BC}$, which yields $BC = 6\sqrt{3}$.

**Step 3 ‚Äî Express $AD$:**
Since $ABCD$ is a rectangle, $AD = BC = 6\sqrt{3}$.

**Step 4 ‚Äî Calculate $FD$:**
Since $F$ is on $AD$, $AD = AF + FD$. Hence, $FD = AD - AF = 6\sqrt{3} - 2$.

**Step 5 ‚Äî Calculate $DC$ using $\triangle DCF$:**
Apply $\tan(\angle DCF) = \frac{DF}{DC}$, so $\tan(30^\circ) = \frac{6\sqrt{3} - 2}{DC}$, which yields $DC = \sqrt{3}(6\sqrt{3} - 2) = 18 - 2\sqrt{3}$.

**Step 6 ‚Äî Calculate the Area:**
Area of rectangle $= BC \cdot DC = (6\sqrt{3})(18 - 2\sqrt{3}) = 108\sqrt{3} - 36$.

**Step 7 ‚Äî State the Final Answer:**
The area of the rectangle is $108\sqrt{3} - 36$.

**Answer:** $\boxed{108\sqrt{3} - 36}$
