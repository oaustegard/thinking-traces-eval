Example 1: **Approach:** Finding the Target Number of Chairs and Subtracting

**Problem:** Determine the number of chairs to remove from $110$ chairs so that the remaining chairs can be arranged in complete rows of $11$, accommodate at least $70$ students, and minimize the number of empty seats.

**Step 1 — Identify the Constraint:**
Recognize that the remaining number of chairs, $N$, must be a multiple of $11$ and greater than or equal to $70$.

**Step 2 — Find the Target Number of Chairs:**
Determine the smallest multiple of $11$ that is $\ge 70$.

**Step 3 — Divide to Find the Integer Multiple:**
Divide 70 by 11 to get $70/11 = 6.36...$

**Step 4 — Round Up to find the number of rows:**
Since $N = 11 \times k$,  where $k$ is the number of rows, round $6.36$ up to the nearest integer, 7.

**Step 5 — Calculate the Target Number of Chairs:**
Compute $7 \times 11 = 77$ chairs. This is the minimum number of chairs needed.

**Step 6 — Compute the Number of Chairs to Remove:**
Subtract the target number of chairs from the initial number of chairs: $110 - 77$.

**Step 7 — Calculate the Result:**
Perform the subtraction: $110 - 77 = 33$.

**Answer:** $\boxed{33}$
Example 2: **Approach:** Direct Calculation

**Problem:** A classroom has 11 rows of chairs, with 11 chairs in each row. The chairs in each row are numbered from 1 to 11. How many chairs have odd numbers?

**Step 1 — Identify the Chair Numbering within a Row:** Observe that chairs in each row are numbered from 1 to 11.

**Step 2 — Count Odd Numbers in a Single Row:** Identify the odd numbers within the range 1 to 11: 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11. Count them; there are 6.

**Step 3 — Consider All Rows:** Recognize that this pattern (6 odd-numbered chairs per row) applies to all 11 rows.

**Step 4 — Calculate the Total Count:** Multiply the number of odd-numbered chairs per row by the number of rows: 6 chairs/row * 11 rows.

**Step 5 — Compute the Final Result:** Calculate 6 * 11 = 66.

**Answer:** $\boxed{66}$
Example 3: **Approach:** Ratio and Proportion with Deduction

**Problem:** Two-thirds of the people in a room are seated in three-fourths of the chairs. There are 6 empty chairs. How many people are in the room?

**Step 1 — Deduce Total Chairs from Empty Chairs:** The empty chairs are $\frac{1}{4}$ of the total chairs, so the total number of chairs is $6 \times 4 = 24$.

**Step 2 — Calculate Occupied Chairs:** Since $\frac{3}{4}$ of the chairs are occupied, the number of occupied chairs is $\frac{3}{4} \times 24 = 18$.

**Step 3 — Relate Occupied Chairs to Seated People:** The number of seated people equals the number of occupied chairs, which is 18.

**Step 4 — Relate Seated People to Total People:** 18 people represent $\frac{2}{3}$ of the total people.

**Step 5 — Calculate Total People:**  If $\frac{2}{3}$ of the people is 18, then the total number of people is $18 \times \frac{3}{2} = 27$.

**Step 6 — State the Answer:** The total number of people is 27.

**Answer:** $\boxed{27}$
