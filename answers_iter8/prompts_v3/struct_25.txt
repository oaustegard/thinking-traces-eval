**Step 2 — Establish Constraints:** State the constraints on $x, y$, and $s$: $0 \leq x \leq 24$, $0 \leq y \leq 24$, $1 \leq s$, $x + s \leq 24$, and $y + s \leq 24$.

**Step 4 — Count Squares for a Fixed Side Length:** For a fixed side length $s$, count the possible $(x, y)$ pairs. $x$ can range from $0$ to $24-s$, and $y$ can range from $0$ to $24-s$.

**Step 5 — Determine Number of Squares for Given Side Length:** The number of choices for $x$ and $y$ independently is $(24 - s + 1) = 25 - s$. The number of squares for the given side length $s$ is $(25 - s) \times (25 - s) = (25 - s)^2$.

**Step 6 — Sum Over Possible Side Lengths:** The side length $s$ can range from $1$ to $24$. So, sum the number of squares for each valid side length: $\sum_{s=1}^{24} (25 - s)^2$.

**Step 7 — Calculate the Sum:**  Rewrite the sum: $\sum_{s=1}^{24} (25 - s)^2 = \sum_{k=1}^{24} k^2 = \frac{24(24+1)(2 \cdot 24 + 1)}{6} = \frac{24 \cdot 25 \cdot 49}{6} = 4900$.

**Answer:** $\boxed{4900}$
