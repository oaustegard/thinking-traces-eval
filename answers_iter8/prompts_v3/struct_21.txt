Example 1: **Approach:** Finding the Smallest Element

**Problem:** Given an infinite set $B$ of positive integers such that for any $a, b \in B$ with $a > b$, $\frac{a-b}{(a,b)} \in B$, prove that $B$ must contain all positive integers.

**Step 1 — Define the Smallest Element:**
Let $m$ be the smallest element in $B$.

**Step 2 — Consider an Element Larger Than the Smallest:**
Choose an element $a \in B$ such that $a > m$.

**Step 3 — Apply the Given Property:**
Since $a, m \in B$ and $a > m$, then $\frac{a-m}{(a,m)} \in B$.

**Step 4 — Leverage the Minimality Condition:**
Since $m$ is the smallest element in $B$, and $\frac{a-m}{(a,m)} \in B$, it must be the case that $m \le \frac{a-m}{(a,m)}$, which implies $m(a, m) \le a - m$.

**Step 5 — Analyze the Greatest Common Divisor:**
Consider two cases: either $(a,m)=1$ or $(a,m) > 1$.

**Step 6 — Consider $(a, m)=1$:**
If $(a, m) = 1$, the inequality $m(a, m) \le a - m$ simplifies to $m \le a-m$, which gives $a \ge 2m$. Also then, $\frac{a-m}{(a,m)} = a-m \in B$. Then, $\frac{(a-m)-m}{(a-m, m)} = \frac{a-2m}{1}= a-2m \in B$, and so forth until we get to $1 \in B$.

**Step 7 — Conclude:**
Since $1 \in B$, and for any $a \in B$, $\frac{a-1}{(a,1)} = a-1 \in B$, we can repeatedly subtract 1 from elements of $B$ to arrive at the fact that all positive integers are in $B$.

**Answer:** $\boxed{B \text{ contains all positive integers}}$
Example 2: **Approach:** Geometric Probability with Area

**Problem:** A machine picks a real number uniformly at random from $[0, 2022]$. Andrew randomly chooses a real number from $[2020, 2022]$. The probability that Andrew’s number is less than the machine’s number is $\frac{m}{n}$ where $m$ and $n$ are relatively prime positive integers. Find $m + n$.

**Step 1 — Define the Sample Space:**
Let $M$ be the machine's number, $M \in [0, 2022]$, and $A$ be Andrew's number, $A \in [2020, 2022]$.

**Step 2 — Find the Probability Density Functions:**
The probability density function for $M$ is $f_M(m) = \frac{1}{2022}$ for $0 \le m \le 2022$. The probability density function for $A$ is $f_A(a) = \frac{1}{2}$ for $2020 \le a \le 2022$.

**Step 3 — Set Up the Integration Region:**
Determine the condition for $A < M$. The integration region will be defined by $2020 \le a \le 2022$, and $a < m \le 2022$.

**Step 4 — Set up the Integral:**
The probability is given by $\int_{2020}^{2022} \int_{a}^{2022} f_A(a) f_M(m) dm da$. The integral is $P(A < M) = \int_{2020}^{2022} \int_{a}^{2022} \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{2022} dm da$.

**Step 5 — Evaluate the Inner Integral:**
$\int_{a}^{2022} \frac{1}{4044} dm = \frac{1}{4044}[m]_{a}^{2022} = \frac{1}{4044}(2022 - a)$.

**Step 6 — Evaluate the Outer Integral:**
$\int_{2020}^{2022} \frac{1}{4044}(2022-a) da = \frac{1}{4044} \int_{2020}^{2022} (2022-a) da = \frac{1}{4044}[2022a - \frac{a^2}{2}]_{2020}^{2022}$.

**Step 7 — Simplify and Find the Answer:**
$\frac{1}{4044} [ (2022^2 - \frac{2022^2}{2}) - (2022 \cdot 2020 - \frac{2020^2}{2}) ] = \frac{1}{4044} \cdot [ \frac{2022^2 - 2020^2}{2} - 2020 (2022-2020)] = \frac{2}{4044} = \frac{1}{2022}$.  Then $m = 1$, $n = 2022$, and $m + n = 1 + 2022 = 2023$.

**Answer:** $\boxed{2023}$
Example 3: **Approach:** Direct Counting

**Problem:** Find the probability that a number chosen randomly from the integers 1 to 25 is a multiple of 2 or 5. Express the answer as a simplified fraction.

**Step 1 — Identify the Sample Space:**
The sample space is the set of integers from 1 to 25, with a total of 25 possible outcomes.

**Step 2 — List Favorable Outcomes:**
List the numbers between 1 and 25 that are multiples of 2 or 5: 2, 4, 5, 6, 8, 10, 12, 14, 15, 16, 18, 20, 22, 24, 25.

**Step 3 — Count Favorable Outcomes:**
Count the numbers in the list from Step 2. There are 15 favorable outcomes.

**Step 4 — Calculate the Probability:**
Divide the number of favorable outcomes by the total number of outcomes: $\frac{15}{25}$.

**Step 5 — Simplify the Fraction:**
Simplify the fraction $\frac{15}{25}$ to $\frac{3}{5}$.

**Answer:** $\boxed{\frac{3}{5}}$
