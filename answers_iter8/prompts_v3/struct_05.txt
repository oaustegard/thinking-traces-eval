Example 1: **Approach:** Approach using Radius of Inscribed Circle

**Problem:** Find the area of an isosceles trapezoid circumscribed around a circle, given that the longer base is 16 and one base angle is arcsin(0.8).

**Step 1 ŌĆö Define Variables and Relationships:**
Let $r$ be the radius of the inscribed circle, so $h = 2r$. Also, $AB=16$, and $\sin \theta = 0.8$.

**Step 2 ŌĆö Utilize Trigonometric Ratios and AE:**
Since $\tan \theta = \frac{h}{AE} = \frac{2r}{AE}$ and $\tan \theta = \frac{4}{3}$, find $AE = \frac{3h}{4} = \frac{3(2r)}{4} = \frac{3r}{2}$.  Also, $AE = \frac{AB-CD}{2}$.

**Step 3 ŌĆö Express CD in terms of r:**
Equate the expressions for $AE$: $\frac{3r}{2} = \frac{16-CD}{2}$.  Then $3r = 16-CD$, which simplifies to $CD = 16-3r$.

**Step 4 ŌĆö Use Tangential Trapezoid Property and Relate AD and r:**
The property of tangential trapezoid yields $AB+CD = 2AD$, so $16+(16-3r)=2AD$.  Also, $\sin \theta = \frac{h}{AD}$, therefore $0.8 = \frac{2r}{AD}$, or $AD = \frac{2r}{0.8} = \frac{5r}{2}$.

**Step 5 ŌĆö Solve for r:**
Substitute AD into the equation obtained in Step 4:  $32-3r = 2 \cdot \frac{5r}{2}=5r$.  This simplifies to $32 = 8r$, and thus $r=4$.

**Step 6 ŌĆö Determine Height, CD, and calculate the Area:**
If $r=4$, then $h = 2r = 8$, and $CD = 16 - 3(4) = 4$. Then, compute Area: $\frac{1}{2}(16+4)(8) = 80$.

**Answer:** $\boxed{80}$
Example 2: **Approach:** Geometric Solution

**Problem:** Find the area of an isosceles trapezoid with bases of length 7 and 17, and legs of length 13.

**Step 1 ŌĆö Draw the Diagram and Label:** Draw an isosceles trapezoid ABCD with AB = 7 and CD = 17 and AD = BC = 13. Drop perpendiculars from A and B to CD, meeting CD at E and F respectively.

**Step 2 ŌĆö Recognize the Rectangle and Congruent Triangles:** Observe that ABEF is a rectangle, so EF = AB = 7. Recognize that triangles ADE and BCF are congruent.

**Step 3 ŌĆö Compute the Projection Lengths:** Since DE + EF + FC = 17, and DE = FC, calculate DE and FC as (17 - 7)/2 = 5.

**Step 4 ŌĆö Apply the Pythagorean Theorem:** In right triangle ADE, apply the Pythagorean theorem: $AE^2 + DE^2 = AD^2$. Substitute the known values.

**Step 5 ŌĆö Calculate the Height:** Solve for the height AE (which is also the height of the trapezoid, h): $h^2 + 5^2 = 13^2$,  $h^2 = 144$, $h = 12$.

**Step 6 ŌĆö Apply the Area Formula:** Use the formula for the area of a trapezoid:  $A = (1/2)(b_1 + b_2)h$. Substitute the known values.

**Step 7 ŌĆö Calculate the Area:**  Compute the area:  $A = (1/2)(7 + 17)(12) = 144$.

**Answer:** $\boxed{144}$
