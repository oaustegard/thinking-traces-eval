**Step 3 — Rewrite the problem:** Recognize that $|EM| + |MB| = |EM| + |MB'|$ where $B'$ is the reflection of $B$.

**Step 4 — Determine the range of values:** The minimum value of $|EM|+|MB'|$ is $|EB'| = \sqrt{(2-0)^2 + (4-0)^2} = \sqrt{20}$. The maximum value is $|EA|+|AB'| = |EA|+|AD|=2+4=6$ when $M=A$.

Example 3: **Approach:** Algebraic Simplification and Calculus

**Problem:** Given a point A at the origin and points B, C, and D forming a tetrahedron such that AB, AC, and AD are mutually orthogonal, CD = $\sqrt{2}$, find the minimum value of $BC^6 + BD^6 - AC^6 - AD^6$.

**Step 1 — Define Coordinates and Express Distances:**
Define B=(3,0,0), C=(0,y,0), D=(0,0,z). CD = $\sqrt{2} \implies y^2 + z^2 = 2$.

**Step 2 — Write the Expression in Terms of y and z:**
$BC^2 = 9+y^2$, $BD^2 = 9+z^2$, $AC^2 = y^2$, $AD^2 = z^2$.  The expression to minimize is $(9+y^2)^3+(9+z^2)^3-y^6-z^6$.

**Step 3 — Substitute and Simplify:**
Let $u = y^2$ and $v = z^2$. Then $u+v=2$, and the expression becomes $(9+u)^3+(9+v)^3-u^3-v^3$. Since $v=2-u$, substitute to get $f(u) = (9+u)^3+(11-u)^3-u^3-(2-u)^3$.

**Step 4 — Differentiate the Expression:**
Compute $f'(u) = 3(9+u)^2 - 3(11-u)^2 - 3u^2 + 3(2-u)^2$.

**Step 5 — Simplify and Solve for Critical Points:**
Simplify $f'(u) = 3(-36+36u)$. Set $f'(u) = 0$ to get $u=1$.

**Step 6 — Evaluate at the Critical Point:**
With $u=1$, the original expression becomes $(9+1)^3+(9+1)^3-1^3-1^3 = 1000+1000-1-1=1998$.

**Step 7 — State the Solution:**
The minimum value is 1998.

**Answer:** $\boxed{1998}$
