# Trace: Euclidean Cubic Anharmonic Oscillator

**Problem.** Let $U(q) := \tfrac{m^2 q^2}{2} - \tfrac{g q^3}{6}$ (Euclidean cubic anharmonic oscillator).
(i) Calculate the leading term of the 1-point function $G_1(t) := \langle q(t) \rangle$ with respect to $g$.
(ii) Calculate the connected 2-point function $G_2^c(t_1, t_2) := \langle q(t_1) q(t_2) \rangle_c$ modulo $g^3$ (i.e., up to and including order $g^2$).

## Reasoning trace

**Setup.** The Euclidean action is $S[q] = \int dt \bigl(\tfrac12 \dot q^2 + \tfrac{m^2}{2} q^2 - \tfrac{g}{6} q^3\bigr)$.
The interaction term is $S_{\text{int}}[q] = -\int dt \frac{g}{6} q^3$.
The term in the path integral exponent is $-S_{\text{int}}/\hbar = \int dt \frac{g}{6} q^3$.
Feynman rules:
*   Free propagator: $G_0(t_1, t_2) = \langle q(t_1) q(t_2)\rangle_0 = \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|}$.
*   Each 3-valent vertex (from $q^3$) carries a weight $g$. (The $1/3!$ in the action cancels the $3!$ ways to label the legs of a vertex).
*   For a given diagram, multiply by $1/S$, where $S$ is the symmetry factor of the diagram.

We will use the following standard convolution integrals for the free propagator $G_0(t) = \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t|}$:
*   $I_2(T) = \int dt' G_0(T-t') G_0(t') = \frac{1}{4m^3} (1+m|T|) e^{-m|T|}$.
*   $I_3(T) = \int dt' G_0(T-t') I_2(t') = \frac{1}{8m^5} (1+m|T|+\frac{1}{3}m^2 T^2) e^{-m|T|}$.

---

### Part (i): Leading term of $G_1(t) = \langle q(t) \rangle$ with respect to $g$.

The 1-point function is the sum of all connected diagrams with one external leg.

**Order $g^0$.** In the free theory ($g=0$), $\langle q(t) \rangle_0 = 0$ as the potential is symmetric ($q \to -q$).

**Order $g^1$.** Diagrams with one vertex. The 3-valent vertex has one external leg (at time $t$). The remaining two half-edges must contract with each other to form a loop.
*   **Diagram:** A single 3-valent vertex with one external leg at $t$ and a self-loop.
    ```
    t -- V
         |
         o
    ```
*   **Vertex factor:** $g$.
*   **Propagators:** $G_0(t_v, t_v)$ for the loop (where $t_v$ is the vertex time, which becomes $t$ due to the external leg).
*   **Symmetry factor:** The self-loop has a $\mathbb{Z}_2$ symmetry (interchanging its two ends), so $S=2$.
*   **Contribution:** $G_1^{(1)}(t) = \frac{g}{S} G_0(t,t) = \frac{g}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{2m} = \frac{g}{4m}$.

This is the leading term. It is a constant, reflecting a shift in the equilibrium position of the oscillator due to the cubic term.

---

### Part (ii): Connected 2-point function $G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = \langle q(t_1) q(t_2) \rangle_c$ modulo $g^3$.

This is the sum of all connected diagrams with two external legs.

**Order $g^0$.**
*   **Diagram:** A single propagator connecting $t_1$ and $t_2$.
*   **Contribution:** $G_2^{c,(0)}(t_1, t_2) = G_0(t_1, t_2) = \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|}$.

**Order $g^1$.** Diagrams with one vertex. The 3-valent vertex has two external legs (at $t_1, t_2$). The remaining half-edge must form a loop.
*   **Diagram:** A single 3-valent vertex, with one leg to $t_1$, one leg to $t_2$, and a self-loop.
    ```
    t1 -- V -- t2
         |
         o
    ```
*   **Vertex factor:** $g$.
*   **Propagators:** $G_0(t_1, t_v)$, $G_0(t_v, t_2)$, and $G_0(t_v, t_v)$ for the loop.
*   **Symmetry factor:** The self-loop has no additional symmetry when attached to an external line, so $S=1$.
*   **Contribution:** $G_2^{c,(1)}(t_1, t_2) = g \int dt_v G_0(t_1, t_v) G_0(t_v, t_2) G_0(t_v, t_v)$.
    Using $G_0(t_v, t_v) = \frac{1}{2m}$ and $I_2(T)$ definition:
    $G_2^{c,(1)}(t_1, t_2) = g \cdot \frac{1}{2m} \int dt_v G_0(t_1, t_v) G_0(t_v, t_2) = \frac{g}{2m} I_2(t_1-t_2)$.
    $G_2^{c,(1)}(t_1, t_2) = \frac{g}{2m} \cdot \frac{1}{4m^3} (1+m|t_1-t_2|) e^{-m|t_1-t_2|} = \frac{g}{8m^4} (1+m|t_1-t_2|) e^{-m|t_1-t_2|}$.

**Order $g^2$.** Diagrams with two vertices. Each vertex is 3-valent, so there are 6 half-edges in total. Two are external (to $t_1, t_2$), leaving 4 internal half-edges.

1.  **"Double tadpole" diagram:** Two vertices, each with a self-loop, connected by a single propagator.
    ```
    t1 -- V1 -- V2 -- t2
         |    |
         o    o
    ```
*   **Vertex factors:** $g^2$.
*   **Propagators:** $G_0(t_1, t_v)$, $G_0(t_v, t_v)$, $G_0(t_v, t_{v'})$, $G_0(t_{v'}, t_{v'})$, $G_0(t_{v'}, t_2)$.
*   **Symmetry factor:** Each self-loop has $S=2$. Since there are two independent self-loops, the total symmetry factor is $S=2 \cdot 2 = 4$.
*   **Contribution:** $G_2^{c,(2a)}(t_1, t_2) = \frac{g^2}{4} \int dt_v dt_{v'} G_0(t_1, t_v) G_0(t_v, t_v) G_0(t_v, t_{v'}) G_0(t_{v'}, t_{v'}) G_0(t_{v'}, t_2)$.
    Using $G_0(t_v, t_v) = \frac{1}{2m}$ for both loops:
    $G_2^{c,(2a)}(t_1, t_2) = \frac{g^2}{4} \left(\frac{1}{2m}\right)^2 \int dt_v dt_{v'} G_0(t_1, t_v) G_0(t_v, t_{v'}) G_0(t_{v'}, t_2) = \frac{g^2}{16m^2} I_3(t_1-t_2)$.
    $G_2^{c,(2a)}(t_1, t_2) = \frac{g^2}{16m^2} \cdot \frac{1}{8m^5} (1+m|t_1-t_2|+\frac{1}{3}m^2(t_1-t_2)^2) e^{-m|t_1-t_2|} = \frac{g^2}{128m^7} (1+m|t_1-t_2|+\frac{1}{3}m^2(t_1-t_2)^2) e^{-m|t_1-t_2|}$.

2.  **"Eye" (or "double-propagator") diagram:** Two vertices connected by *two* propagators, with external legs at $t_1, t_2$.
    ```
    t1 --- V1 ===== V2 --- t2
    ```
    (Double line indicates two propagators between $V_1$ and $V_2$)
*   **Vertex factors:** $g^2$.
*   **Propagators:** $G_0(t_1, t_v)$, $G_0(t_{v'}, t_2)$, and two copies of $G_0(t_v, t_{v'})$ connecting the vertices.
*   **Symmetry factor:** The two internal propagators between $V_1$ and $V_2$ are indistinguishable. So $S=2$.
*   **Contribution:** $G_2^{c,(2b)}(t_1, t_2) = \frac{g^2}{2} \int dt_v dt_{v'} G_0(t_1, t_v) [G_0(t_v, t_{v'})]^2 G_0(t_{v'}, t_2)$.
    The term $[G_0(t_v, t_{v'})]^2 = \left(\frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_v-t_{v'}|}\right)^2 = \frac{1}{4m^2} e^{-2m|t_v-t_{v'}|}$.
    Let $K(T) = e^{-2m|T|}$. Its Fourier transform is $\mathcal{F}[K(\omega)] = \frac{4m}{\omega^2+(2m)^2}$.
    The integral is $\frac{g^2}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{4m^2} \int dt_v dt_{v'} G_0(t_1, t_v) e^{-2m|t_v-t_{v'}|} G_0(t_{v'}, t_2)$.
    In frequency space, this is $\frac{g^2}{8m^2} G_0(\omega) \mathcal{F}[K(\omega)] G_0(\omega) = \frac{g^2}{8m^2} \frac{1}{\omega^2+m^2} \frac{4m}{\omega^2+4m^2} \frac{1}{\omega^2+m^2}$.
    $= \frac{g^2}{8m^2} \frac{4m}{(\omega^2+m^2)^2 (\omega^2+4m^2)}$.
    Performing the inverse Fourier transform (using partial fractions, as done in the thought process):
    $\mathcal{F}^{-1}\left[ \frac{4m}{(\omega^2+m^2)^2 (\omega^2+4m^2)} \right](T) = \frac{1}{9m^4} \left( (1+3m|T|) e^{-m|T|} + e^{-2m|T|} \right)$.
    So, $G_2^{c,(2b)}(t_1, t_2) = \frac{g^2}{8m^2} \cdot \frac{1}{9m^4} \left( (1+3m|t_1-t_2|) e^{-m|t_1-t_2|} + e^{-2m|t_1-t_2|} \right)$.
    $G_2^{c,(2b)}(t_1, t_2) = \frac{g^2}{72m^6} \left( (1+3m|t_1-t_2|) e^{-m|t_1-t_2|} + e^{-2m|t_1-t_2|} \right)$.

---

## Final answer

(i) The leading term of the 1-point function $G_1(t) = \langle q(t) \rangle$ is:
$$\boxed{\;G_1(t) = \frac{g}{4m} + O(g^2).\;}$$

(ii) The connected 2-point function $G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = \langle q(t_1) q(t_2) \rangle_c$ modulo $g^3$ is:
$$\boxed{\begin{aligned}
G_2^c(t_1, t_2) &= \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|} \\
&+ \frac{g}{8m^4} (1+m|t_1-t_2|) e^{-m|t_1-t_2|} \\
&+ \frac{g^2}{128m^7} (1+m|t_1-t_2|+\frac{1}{3}m^2(t_1-t_2)^2) e^{-m|t_1-t_2|} \\
&+ \frac{g^2}{72m^6} \left( (1+3m|t_1-t_2|) e^{-m|t_1-t_2|} + e^{-2m|t_1-t_2|} \right) + O(g^3).
\end{aligned}}$$
