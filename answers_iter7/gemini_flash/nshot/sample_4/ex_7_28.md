## Reasoning trace

The system is a quantum particle with potential $U(q) = \tfrac{m^2 q^2}{2} - \tfrac{g q^3}{6}$. The Euclidean action is $S[q] = \int dt \left(\tfrac12 \dot q^2 + \tfrac{m^2}{2} q^2 - \tfrac{g}{6} q^3\right)$.
The free propagator is $G_0(t_1, t_2) = \langle q(t_1) q(t_2)\rangle_0 = \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|}$.
The interaction term is $S_{\text{int}} = -\int dt \tfrac{g}{6} q^3$. In Feynman rules, each $q^3$ vertex carries a weight $g$ (the $1/3!$ from the action cancels the $3!$ leg permutations for unlabeled legs).

**(i) Leading term of the 1-point function $G_1(t) = \langle q(t) \rangle$ with respect to $g$.**

The 1-point function $\langle q(t) \rangle$ is the sum of all connected Feynman diagrams with one external leg.
- **Order $g^0$ (free theory):** $\langle q(t) \rangle_0 = 0$ due to the $q \leftrightarrow -q$ symmetry of the free harmonic oscillator.
- **Order $g^1$:** We need a diagram with one 3-valent vertex and one external leg at time $t$. The other two half-edges of the vertex must contract with each other to form a loop.
    - **Diagram:** A single 3-valent vertex at time $t_v$. One leg connects to the external point $t$. The other two legs form a self-loop.
    - **Vertex factor:** $g$.
    - **Propagators:** $G_0(t, t_v)$ for the external leg, and $G_0(t_v, t_v)$ for the self-loop.
    - **Symmetry factor:** The diagram has no non-trivial symmetries (the external leg is unique, the loop is unique). So, $S=1$.
    - **Value:** $g \int dt_v \, G_0(t, t_v) \, G_0(t_v, t_v)$.
    - We know $G_0(t_v, t_v) = \frac{1}{2m}$.
    - For the integral $\int dt_v \, G_0(t, t_v)$, in the zero-frequency limit (which corresponds to a constant shift in $\langle q(t) \rangle$), this integral evaluates to $G_0(\omega=0) = \frac{1}{m^2}$. (The integral $\int_{-\infty}^\infty d\tau e^{-m|\tau|}$ diverges, but in the context of constant background fields, the relevant factor is $G_0(0)$).
    - So, $\langle q(t) \rangle^{(1)} = g \cdot \frac{1}{2m} \cdot \frac{1}{m^2} = \frac{g}{2m^3}$.
    This is the leading order term for $\langle q(t) \rangle$.

**(ii) Connected 2-point function $G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = \langle q(t_1) q(t_2) \rangle_c$ modulo $g^3$.**

The connected 2-point function is related to the 1-particle irreducible (1PI) 2-point function (self-energy) $\Sigma$ via Dyson's equation:
$G_2^c = G_0 + G_0 \star \Sigma \star G_0 + G_0 \star \Sigma \star G_0 \star \Sigma \star G_0 + \dots$.
We need to calculate this up to $O(g^2)$. This means we need terms:
$G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = G_0(t_1, t_2) + (G_0 \star \Sigma^{(1)} \star G_0)(t_1, t_2) + (G_0 \star \Sigma^{(2)} \star G_0)(t_1, t_2) + (G_0 \star \Sigma^{(1)} \star G_0 \star \Sigma^{(1)} \star G_0)(t_1, t_2) + O(g^3)$.
Here, $\Sigma^{(k)}$ denotes the 1PI 2-point function at order $g^k$. The external legs of $\Sigma$ are amputated (no external propagators).

**Order $g^0$:** $G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = G_0(t_1, t_2)$. $\Sigma^{(0)}=0$.

**Order $g^1$ for $\Sigma$ (1-loop):**
- **Diagram:** A single 3-valent vertex at $t_v$. Two half-edges are connected to the amputated external legs at $t_1, t_2$. The third half-edge forms a self-loop.
- **Vertex factor:** $g$.
- **Propagator:** $G_0(t_v, t_v)$ for the self-loop.
- **Value:** $g \int dt_v \, \delta(t_1 - t_v) \, \delta(t_2 - t_v) \, G_0(t_v, t_v)$.
- **Symmetry factor:** $S=1$.
- $\Sigma^{(1)}(t_1, t_2) = g \, G_0(t_1, t_1) \, \delta(t_1 - t_2) = \frac{g}{2m} \, \delta(t_1 - t_2)$.
This is a local (in time) self-energy correction.

**Order $g^2$ for $\Sigma$ (2-loop):**
- We need 1PI diagrams with two 3-valent vertices and two amputated external legs.
- **Diagram:** Two 3-valent vertices at $t_v, t_{v'}$. Each vertex connects to one external leg ($t_1$ to $t_v$, $t_2$ to $t_{v'}$). The remaining two half-edges from $t_v$ connect to the remaining two half-edges from $t_{v'}$, forming two parallel propagators between the vertices. This is a "double-bubble" diagram.
- **Vertex factors:** $g \cdot g = g^2$.
- **Propagators:** $[G_0(t_v, t_{v'})]^2$ for the two internal lines.
- **Value:** $g^2 \int dt_v dt_{v'} \, \delta(t_1 - t_v) \, [G_0(t_v, t_{v'})]^2 \, \delta(t_{v'} - t_2) = g^2 \, [G_0(t_1, t_2)]^2$.
- **Symmetry factor:** The two propagators connecting the two vertices are identical and interchangeable. So $S=2$.
- $\Sigma^{(2)}(t_1, t_2) = \frac{g^2}{2} \, [G_0(t_1, t_2)]^2$.
This is a non-local self-energy correction.

**Combining terms for $G_2^c(t_1, t_2)$:**
Let $G_0(t_1, t_2)$ denote $\frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_1-t_2|}$. Let $G_0^{(k)}(t_1, t_2)$ denote $\frac{1}{2k} e^{-k|t_1-t_2|}$. So $G_0(t_1, t_2) = G_0^{(m)}(t_1, t_2)$.
The convolution $(A \star B)(t_1, t_2) = \int d\tau A(t_1, \tau) B(\tau, t_2)$.

- **Order $g^0$:**
    $G_0(t_1, t_2)$.

- **Order $g^1$:**
    $(G_0 \star \Sigma^{(1)} \star G_0)(t_1, t_2) = \int dt_a dt_b \, G_0(t_1, t_a) \, \left(\frac{g}{2m} \delta(t_a - t_b)\right) \, G_0(t_b, t_2)$
    $= \frac{g}{2m} \int dt_a \, G_0(t_1, t_a) \, G_0(t_a, t_2) = \frac{g}{2m} (G_0 \star G_0)(t_1, t_2)$.

- **Order $g^2$ (two terms):**
    1. $(G_0 \star \Sigma^{(2)} \star G_0)(t_1, t_2) = \int dt_a dt_b \, G_0(t_1, t_a) \, \left(\frac{g^2}{2} [G_0(t_a, t_b)]^2\right) \, G_0(t_b, t_2)$.
        Note that $[G_0(t_a, t_b)]^2 = \left(\frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_a-t_b|}\right)^2 = \frac{1}{4m^2} e^{-2m|t_a-t_b|} = \frac{1}{2m} G_0^{(2m)}(t_a, t_b)$.
        So this term is $\frac{g^2}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{2m} (G_0 \star G_0^{(2m)} \star G_0)(t_1, t_2) = \frac{g^2}{4m} (G_0 \star G_0^{(2m)} \star G_0)(t_1, t_2)$.
    2. $(G_0 \star \Sigma^{(1)} \star G_0 \star \Sigma^{(1)} \star G_0)(t_1, t_2)$.
        Let $A(t_1, t_2) = (G_0 \star \Sigma^{(1)} \star G_0)(t_1, t_2) = \frac{g}{2m} (G_0 \star G_0)(t_1, t_2)$.
        Then this term is $(A \star \Sigma^{(1)} \star G_0)(t_1, t_2) = \int dt_a dt_b \, A(t_1, t_a) \, \left(\frac{g}{2m} \delta(t_a - t_b)\right) \, G_0(t_b, t_2)$
        $= \frac{g}{2m} \int dt_a \, A(t_1, t_a) \, G_0(t_a, t_2) = \frac{g}{2m} \left( \frac{g}{2m} (G_0 \star G_0) \star G_0 \right)(t_1, t_2) = \frac{g^2}{4m^2} (G_0 \star G_0 \star G_0)(t_1, t_2)$.

## Final answer

**(i) Leading term of the 1-point function $G_1(t) = \langle q(t) \rangle$:**

$$\boxed{\;\langle q(t) \rangle = \frac{g}{2m^3} + O(g^2).\;}$$

**(ii) Connected 2-point function $G_2^c(t_1, t_2)$ modulo $g^3$:**

Let $G_0(t_1, t_2) = \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|}$ be the free propagator.
Let $G_0^{(k)}(t_1, t_2) = \frac{1}{2k} e^{-k|t_1 - t_2|}$ be a free propagator with mass $k$.
Let $(A \star B)(t_1, t_2) = \int d\tau A(t_1, \tau) B(\tau, t_2)$ denote the convolution product.

Then, the connected 2-point function is:
$$\boxed{\;G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = G_0(t_1, t_2) + \frac{g}{2m} (G_0 \star G_0)(t_1, t_2) + \frac{g^2}{4m} (G_0 \star G_0^{(2m)} \star G_0)(t_1, t_2) + \frac{g^2}{4m^2} (G_0 \star G_0 \star G_0)(t_1, t_2) + O(g^3).\;}$$
