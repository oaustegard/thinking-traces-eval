## Solution

For the cubic anharmonic oscillator with U(q) = m²q²/2 - gq³/6 and Euclidean action S[q] = ∫ dt (½q̇² + m²q²/2 - gq³/6), we compute correlation functions to leading orders in g.

**Free propagator:** In Euclidean quantum mechanics with mass m,
$$G_0(t_1, t_2) = \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|}, \quad G_0(t,t) = \frac{1}{2m}.$$

### Part (i): 1-point function G₁(t) = ⟨q(t)⟩

**Order g⁰:** By Z₂ symmetry of the quadratic action (q → -q), the free correlator ⟨q(t)⟩₀ = 0. The symmetry persists at order g⁰.

**Order g¹:** The interaction -gq³/6 breaks Z₂ at the quantum level. The leading 1-point contribution comes from the tadpole diagram: a single cubic vertex with one external leg (pointing to q(t)) and two internal legs forming a self-loop.

- Vertex weight: -g
- One external leg: factor q(t) = external variable
- Self-loop: internal propagator ∫ dt_v G_0(t_v, t_v) = G_0(t,t) = 1/(2m)
- Symmetry factor: the two self-loop legs at the vertex can be paired in 1 way; the cubic vertex has 3! = 6 permutations of legs, and we select 1 external and 2 internal, giving factor 1/6 from the vertex rule. But with the self-loop, the symmetry factor is 1/2 (the loop has 2-fold symmetry). Combined: -g/(2) times the loop and external factor.

More directly: by the Feynman-rule convention and Etingof's framework, the tadpole 1-point is
$$G_1^{(1)}(t) = (-g) \cdot G_0(t,t) \cdot \int G_0(t,t') \, dt' / (\text{symmetry}).$$

The integral ∫ G_0(t,t') dt' = ∫₋∞^∞ e^{-m|t-t'|}/(2m) dt' = 1/m. So
$$G_1^{(1)}(t) = -\frac{g}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{2m} \cdot \frac{1}{m} = -\frac{g}{4m^2}.$$

**However**, the sign convention in Etingof (S = -U, with -gq³/6 already negative) means the vertex in the exponent is +g/(6ℏ). Reversing: the vertex weight is +g, so
$$G_1^{(1)}(t) = \frac{g}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{2m} \cdot \frac{1}{m} = \frac{g}{4m^2}.$$

**Answer for part (i):**
$$\boxed{G_1(t) = \frac{g}{4m^2} + O(g^2).}$$
Time-independent, as expected from translation invariance.

### Part (ii): Connected 2-point function G₂^c(t₁,t₂) modulo g³

**Order g⁰:** The free connected 2-point is
$$G_2^c(t_1, t_2)\big|_{g=0} = G_0(t_1, t_2) = \frac{e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|}}{2m}.$$

**Order g¹:** By Z₂ symmetry (the connected 2-point is an even observable), there is no odd-g contribution. Thus G₂^c = 0 at order g¹.

**Order g²:** The 1PI diagrams with two external legs include:
- **Figure-eight (tadpole on a propagator line):** one cubic vertex with 2 external legs and 2 self-loop legs. But this is 1PI only if the 2 external legs are at the same vertex. By valence 3, this is not possible (one external at one vertex leaves only 2 legs for the other). So this diagram does not arise.
- **Sunset:** two cubic vertices, 2 external legs (one per vertex), and 3 internal propagators connecting them. The 1PI condition: cutting any single internal line keeps the graph connected via the other two.

For the sunset, using 1PI self-energy analysis (as in ex_7_27 but with the cubic vertex -g):
$$\Sigma^{(2)}(t_1, t_2) = (-g)^2 \int dt_v \, dt_{v'} \, \delta(t_1 - t_v) \, [G_0(t_v, t_{v'})]^3 \, \delta(t_{v'} - t_2) / 3!,$$
where the 3! accounts for the 3 indistinguishable internal propagators.

$$\Sigma^{(2)}(t_1, t_2) = \frac{g^2}{6} [G_0(t_1, t_2)]^3 = \frac{g^2}{6} \cdot \frac{e^{-3m|t_1 - t_2|}}{8m^3} = \frac{g^2}{48 m^3} e^{-3m|t_1 - t_2|}.$$

**Answer for part (ii):**
$$\boxed{G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = \frac{e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|}}{2m} + \frac{g^2}{48 m^3} e^{-3m|t_1 - t_2|} + O(g^3).}$$

The leading term is the free propagator. The g² correction is non-local in time, reflecting the multi-propagator structure of the 2-loop sunset diagram.
