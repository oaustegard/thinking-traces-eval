Now solve: For $S(x) = x^2/2 - g(x^2 + x^3/6)$, compute the 1-loop contribution to $\log(Z/Z_0)$ where $Z = \int e^{-S(x)/\hbar} dx$ and $Z_0$ is the free partition function. Then use this to compute the number of labeled $n$-vertex 1-loop graphs with 1-valent and 3-valent vertices only.

## Reasoning

**Action and saddle point:** The action is $S(x) = x^2/2 - gx^2 - gx^3/6 = (1-2g)x^2/2 - gx^3/6$. The saddle point is at $x_c = 0$ (by parity, since we have even and odd terms only with the cubic breaking symmetry; actually, the potential is not symmetric, so let me recompute). Taking the derivative: $S'(x) = (1-2g)x - gx^2/2 = 0$ at $x = 0$ and $x = 2(1-2g)/g$. For small $g$, $x_c = 0$ is the dominant saddle. At $x_c = 0$: $S''(x) = 1-2g - gx$, so $S''(0) = 1 - 2g$.

**1-loop formula:** The 1-loop contribution to $\log(Z/Z_0)$ is
$$\log(Z/Z_0)|_{1\text{-loop}} = \frac{1}{2}\log\frac{S''(0)|_{g=0}}{S''(0)|_g} = \frac{1}{2}\log\frac{1}{1-2g} = -\frac{1}{2}\log(1-2g).$$

**Power-series expansion:**
$$-\frac{1}{2}\log(1-2g) = \frac{1}{2}\sum_{k\ge 1}\frac{(2g)^k}{k} = \sum_{k\ge 1}\frac{2^{k-1}g^k}{k} = g + 2g^2 + \frac{8g^3}{3} + \cdots$$

**Graph interpretation:** Each term at order $g^n$ corresponds to labeled 1-loop graphs with $v_1$ one-valent and $v_3$ three-valent vertices, subject to the constraint $v_1 + v_3 = n$ (total $n$ vertices) and $2E_{\text{int}} + E_{\text{ext}} = v_1 + 3v_3$ (valence sum). For a 1-loop tree (i.e., exactly 1 independent cycle), we have $E_{\text{int}} = E_{\text{ext}} = v_1 + v_3 - 1$, giving the relation $2(n-1) + n = v_1 + 3v_3$, i.e., $3n - 2 = v_1 + 3v_3$. Combined with $v_1 + v_3 = n$, we get $v_3 - v_1 = (3n-2-n)/2 = (2n-2)/2 = n-1$... this doesn't close correctly. Let me use the standard fact: a 1-loop graph has $V$ vertices and $V$ edges (since $V - E + 1 = 1$ for 1-loop). Each vertex has degree $1$ or $3$, so $\sum_i d_i = v_1 + 3v_3 = 2E = 2V = 2(v_1+v_3)$, giving $v_1 + 3v_3 = 2v_1 + 2v_3$, hence $v_3 = v_1$. So 1-loop graphs have equal numbers of 1- and 3-valent vertices.

**Coefficients and counts:** The coefficient of $g^n$ in the 1-loop $\log(Z/Z_0)$ equals (by perturbation theory) the sum of all 1-loop labeled graph weights divided by their symmetry factors. From the expansion $\sum_{k\ge 1}\frac{2^{k-1}g^k}{k}$, at order $g^n$ we have $2^{n-1}/n$. Thus the count is
$$a_n = 2^{n-1} n! / n = 2^{n-1} (n-1)!$$
for labeled $n$-vertex 1-loop graphs with $n/2$ vertices of each valence (only for even $n$).

**Final answer:** The 1-loop contribution is $\log(Z/Z_0)|_{1\text{-loop}} = \sum_{k\ge 1}\frac{2^{k-1}g^k}{k}$. The coefficient of $g^n$ gives the count of labeled 1-loop graphs with $n$ vertices (half 1-valent, half 3-valent): $\frac{2^{n-1}}{n}$ times all labeled trees on $n$ such vertices, or by direct enumeration, $2^{n-1}(n-1)!/n = 2^{n-1}(n-2)!$ for $n\ge 2$.

$\boxed{\log(Z/Z_0)|_{1\text{-loop}} = \sum_{n\ge 1}\frac{2^{n-1}g^n}{n}; \text{ graph count: } 2^{n-1}(n-2)! \text{ for even } n.}$
