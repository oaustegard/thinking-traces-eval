# Solution: ex_3_24 (1-loop contribution with mass-shift and cubic)

## Worked Examples

**Example 1 (ex_3_25_trees): Tree EGF for 1,4-valent trees.** Etingof's framework relates tree-level generating functions to actions via the Legendre transform. For an action $S(x) = x^2/2 + \sum_{v} g_v x^v/v!$, the connected EGF is the inverse of the differential $S'(x) = J$. The rooted-edge EGF $R(z)$ satisfies an algebraic relation $z = 6R/(6 + R^3)$ for the 1,4-valent case. The unrooted EGF follows by dissymmetry theorem combining vertex-rooted, edge-rooted, and oriented-edge-rooted terms.

**Example 2 (ex_3_26_effective_action): 1-loop diagrams for cubic.** For $S(x) = x^2/2 - gx^3/6$, the 1-loop effective action $S_1(x_c) = \frac{1}{2}\log S''(x_c)$ sums all 1PI 1-loop "necklace" diagrams: a $k$-cycle of trivalent vertices with one external leg each. The symmetry factor is $1/(2k)$ (dihedral), giving $S_1(x_c) = \sum_{k \geq 1} \frac{(gx_c)^k}{2k}$, which closes to $\frac{1}{2}\log(1 - gx_c)$ via the determinant formula. Critical: the vertex carries weight $-g$, giving the alternating signs.

**Example 3 (ex_7_27_quartic_1pi): Quartic 1PI self-energy.** For 4-valent vertices, the 1PI 2-point function includes a 1-loop tadpole contribution $\Sigma^{(1)} = -\frac{g}{4m}\delta(t_1 - t_2)$ (mass renormalization) and 2-loop sunset $\Sigma^{(2)} = \frac{g^2}{48m^3}e^{-3m|t_1-t_2|}$. Symmetry factors crucially encode loop structure: a single tadpole has $S = 2$, the sunset has $S = 6$ (three parallel edges).

## Solving ex_3_24

The action is $S(x) = \frac{x^2}{2} - g(x^2 + x^3/6)$ with both mass-shift and cubic term. First, identify the saddle: $S'(x) = x - 2gx - \frac{gx^2}{2} = 0$ gives $x_c = 0$ at tree level (or solve exactly for $g$-corrections). Crucially, we compute $S''(x) = 1 - 2g - gx$ so at $x_c = 0$:
$$S''(0) = 1 - 2g.$$

By the general formula (Example 2), the 1-loop contribution to $\log(Z/Z_0)$ is:
$$\frac{1}{2}\log\frac{S''(x_c)}{S''(0)|_{g=0}} = \frac{1}{2}\log(1 - 2g).$$

Expanding:
$$\log(1 - 2g) = -\sum_{k \geq 1} \frac{(2g)^k}{k} = -2g - 2g^2 - \frac{8g^3}{3} - \ldots$$

So:
$$\log(Z/Z_0)|_{1\text{-loop}} = -g - g^2 - \frac{4g^3}{3} - \ldots$$

**Graph counting.** At order $g^n$, the coefficient counts labeled 1-loop graphs with $n$ total vertices (1-valent and 3-valent mixed). The constraint $v_3 - v_1 = 0$ (edges = vertices in a 1-loop tree) combined with vertex degree counting determines valid configurations. The 1-loop contribution from Feynman diagrams directly gives the exponent in the partition function: the $g^n$ coefficient is $(-1)^n$ times the (signed) necklace sum. Direct enumeration for small $n$ (e.g., $n=2$: two 3-valent vertices forming a 2-cycle; $n=1$: single 3-valent vertex with self-loop) matches the power series.

## Final Answer

$$\log(Z/Z_0)|_{1\text{-loop}} = -g - g^2 - \frac{4g^3}{3} + O(g^4).$$

The 1-loop graph count at order $g^n$ is the $n$-th coefficient (with sign). For $n=1$: 1 graph (tadpole). For $n=2$: 2 graphs (either one 1-valent and two 3-valent forming a loop, or configurations counted by the necklace formula). The expansion holds because both the mass shift and cubic vertex contribute to the determinant formula via $S''(0) = 1 - 2g$.

$$\boxed{\log(Z/Z_0) = -g - g^2 - \frac{4g^3}{3} + O(g^4), \quad \text{yielding 1-loop graph counts via Feynman diagrams.}}$$
