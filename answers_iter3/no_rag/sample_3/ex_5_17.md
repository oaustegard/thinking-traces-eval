# Solution to ex_5_17: Free Groups and Gamma(N)

## Part (a): Showing Gamma(N) is Free for N ≥ 3 and Structure of Gamma(2)

### Gamma(N) is Free for N ≥ 3

The principal congruence subgroup $\Gamma(N)$ consists of matrices in $SL_2(\mathbb{Z})$ congruent to the identity modulo $N$.

**Claim:** For $N \geq 3$, $\Gamma(N)$ is a free group.

**Proof:** Consider the action of $\Gamma(N)$ on the upper half-plane $\mathbb{H}$. A matrix $\gamma \in \Gamma(N)$ acts as a Möbius transformation. For $\gamma$ to fix a point $z \in \mathbb{H}$, we need $\gamma z = z$, which (for a matrix not a scalar multiple of the identity) can occur only if $\gamma$ has a fixed point on the boundary of $\mathbb{H}$.

For $N \geq 3$, the only torsion elements in $SL_2(\mathbb{Z})$ that are congruent to the identity modulo $N$ are the identity itself. The torsion elements of $SL_2(\mathbb{Z})$ are:
- The identity $I$
- The matrix of order 2: $\begin{pmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$
- Elements of order 3 and 6 (in the appropriate conjugacy classes)

For $N \geq 3$, these non-identity torsion elements are NOT congruent to $I$ modulo $N$. Therefore, $\Gamma(N)$ contains no nontrivial torsion, and every nontrivial element acts freely on $\mathbb{H}$ (no fixed points in $\mathbb{H}$).

Since $\Gamma(N)$ acts freely and properly discontinuously on the contractible space $\mathbb{H}$, the quotient $\mathbb{H}/\Gamma(N)$ is a $K(\Gamma(N), 1)$ space. The fundamental group of an open Riemann surface (which $\mathbb{H}/\Gamma(N)$ is) is a free group. Thus $\Gamma(N)$ is free.

### Structure of Gamma(2)

For $N = 2$, the matrix $-I = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{pmatrix}$ is congruent to $I$ modulo 2 (since $-1 \equiv 1 \pmod{2}$).

Therefore, $-I \in \Gamma(2)$, and $-I$ has order 2 in the projective space.

$\Gamma(2)$ contains a subgroup $\Gamma_+(2)$ (the quotient by the subgroup $\{\pm I\}$) that is free. We have:
$$\Gamma(2) = \Gamma_+(2) \rtimes \langle -I \rangle$$

where $\langle -I \rangle \cong \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$ is the quotient by $-I$.

Thus:
$$\boxed{\Gamma(2) = \Gamma_+(2) \times \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}}$$

where $\Gamma_+(2)$ is the free group on 2 generators.

## Part (b): Number of Free Generators

**Formula:** The number of free generators of $\Gamma(N)$ is:
$$r = 1 - \chi(\Gamma(N))$$

where $\chi(\Gamma(N))$ is the Euler characteristic of the group.

For a torsion-free group acting on a contractible space, the Euler characteristic can be computed as:
$$\chi(\Gamma(N)) = \chi(\mathbb{H}/\Gamma(N))$$

The Euler characteristic of $\mathbb{H}/\Gamma(N)$ is related to the hyperbolic area by the Gauss-Bonnet theorem:
$$\text{Area}(\mathbb{H}/\Gamma(N)) = -2\pi \chi(\Gamma(N))$$

For the principal congruence subgroup:
$$[\SL_2(\mathbb{Z}) : \Gamma(N)] = N^3 \prod_{p | N} \left(1 - \frac{1}{p^2}\right)$$

Using the orbifold Euler characteristic of $\SL_2(\mathbb{Z})$ (which has $\chi = -1/12$) and the multiplicativity of Euler characteristic under finite-index subgroups:
$$\chi(\Gamma(N)) = \chi(\SL_2(\mathbb{Z})) \cdot [\SL_2(\mathbb{Z}) : \Gamma(N)]$$

Wait, this needs care. For torsion-free subgroups of finite index, $\chi$ is multiplicative:
$$\chi(\Gamma(N)) = -\frac{1}{12} \cdot N^3 \prod_{p|N}\left(1 - \frac{1}{p^2}\right)$$

Therefore, the number of generators is:
$$r = 1 - \chi(\Gamma(N)) = 1 + \frac{N^3}{12}\prod_{p|N}\left(1 - \frac{1}{p^2}\right)$$

**Example (N = 3):**
$$[\SL_2(\mathbb{Z}) : \Gamma(3)] = 3^3 \left(1 - \frac{1}{9}\right) = 27 \cdot \frac{8}{9} = 24$$

$$\chi(\Gamma(3)) = -\frac{24}{12} = -2$$

$$r = 1 - (-2) = 3$$

So $\Gamma(3)$ is the free group on **3 generators**.

## Final Answer

$$\boxed{\text{For } N \geq 3: \Gamma(N) \text{ is free on } r = 1 + \frac{N^3}{12}\prod_{p|N}\left(1-\frac{1}{p^2}\right) \text{ generators.}}$$

$$\boxed{\Gamma(2) = \Gamma_+(2) \times \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z} \text{ where } \Gamma_+(2) \text{ is free on 2 generators.}}$$

$$\boxed{\text{Example: } \Gamma(3) \text{ is free on 3 generators.}}$$
