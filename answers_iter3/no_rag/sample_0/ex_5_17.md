# Free Groups and Principal Congruence Subgroups

## Part (a): Freeness of Gamma(N)

### For N ≥ 3:

The principal congruence subgroup $\Gamma(N)$ consists of matrices in $SL_2(\mathbb{Z})$ congruent to the identity modulo $N$.

**Key observation:** Any torsion element in $SL_2(\mathbb{Z})$ must have finite order. The only torsion elements are:
- Order 2: $S = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$ and $-I = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{pmatrix}$
- Order 3: $ST = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix}$

For $N \geq 3$, these generators are **not** congruent to the identity modulo $N$ (e.g., $S \equiv \begin{pmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \pmod{N}$ is clearly not $\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}$).

Therefore, $\Gamma(N)$ contains **no nontrivial torsion** for $N \geq 3$.

**Action on the upper half-plane:** $\Gamma(N)$ acts on $\mathbb{H} = \{z \in \mathbb{C} : \text{Im}(z) > 0\}$ via:
$$\gamma \cdot z = \frac{az + b}{cz + d}, \quad \gamma = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix}$$

Since $\Gamma(N)$ is torsion-free, **every nontrivial element acts without fixed points** in $\mathbb{H}$ (fixed points of finite-order elements would contradict torsion-freeness).

A free group action on a contractible space (like $\mathbb{H}$) has quotient $\mathbb{H}/\Gamma(N)$ that is a $K(\Gamma(N), 1)$ space.

**Conclusion:** The fundamental group of $\mathbb{H}/\Gamma(N)$ is $\Gamma(N)$. Since this quotient is (topologically) an open Riemann surface (of finite area if $N \geq 2$), its fundamental group is **free**.

Therefore, **$\Gamma(N)$ is a free group for $N \geq 3$**.

### For Gamma(2):

The group $\Gamma(2)$ contains the element $-I$, which **does** satisfy:
$$-I \equiv \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{pmatrix} \equiv \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \pmod{2}$$

So $-I \in \Gamma(2)$, and $(-I)^2 = I$.

**Structure:** Write $\Gamma_+(2) = \{\gamma \in \Gamma(2) : \gamma \equiv I \pmod{2}, \det(\gamma) = 1\}$ (the subgroup without $-I$). This is the **torsion-free** part, acting freely on $\mathbb{H}$, hence free.

Then:
$$\Gamma(2) = \Gamma_+(2) \rtimes \{I, -I\} = \Gamma_+(2) \times \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$$

(The action of $-I$ on $\Gamma_+(2)$ is trivial since $-I$ is central.)

So $\Gamma_+(2)$ is free on **2 generators** (this can be shown explicitly using the Farey tessellation or by computing the fundamental domain), and:
$$\Gamma(2) = \Gamma_+(2) \times \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$$

## Part (b): Number of Free Generators

**Euler characteristic approach:**

For a free group $F_r$ on $r$ generators:
$$\chi(F_r) = 1 - r$$

Since $\Gamma(N)$ is free on $r$ generators, we have:
$$r = 1 - \chi(\Gamma(N))$$

**Computing $\chi(\Gamma(N))$ for $N \geq 3$:**

The orbifold Euler characteristic of $SL_2(\mathbb{Z})$ is:
$$\chi^{\text{orb}}(SL_2(\mathbb{Z})) = -\frac{1}{12}$$

For a finite-index torsion-free subgroup $\Gamma$ of $SL_2(\mathbb{Z})$:
$$\chi(\Gamma) = [SL_2(\mathbb{Z}) : \Gamma] \cdot \chi^{\text{orb}}(SL_2(\mathbb{Z})) = -\frac{[SL_2(\mathbb{Z}) : \Gamma(N)]}{12}$$

The index is:
$$[SL_2(\mathbb{Z}) : \Gamma(N)] = N^3 \prod_{p | N} \left(1 - \frac{1}{p^2}\right)$$

Therefore:
$$\chi(\Gamma(N)) = -\frac{N^3}{12} \prod_{p | N} \left(1 - \frac{1}{p^2}\right)$$

And the number of generators is:
$$r = 1 - \chi(\Gamma(N)) = 1 + \frac{N^3}{12} \prod_{p | N} \left(1 - \frac{1}{p^2}\right)$$

**Example:** For $N = 3$ (prime):
$$r = 1 + \frac{27}{12}\left(1 - \frac{1}{9}\right) = 1 + \frac{27}{12} \cdot \frac{8}{9} = 1 + 2 = 3$$

$$\boxed{r = 1 + \frac{N^3}{12} \prod_{p | N} \left(1 - \frac{1}{p^2}\right)}$$

For $N = 3$: **3 generators**. For general $N \geq 3$: the formula above.
