# 1-Point and Connected 2-Point Functions in Cubic Anharmonic Oscillator

## Problem Setup

The Euclidean action is:
$$S(x) = \frac{m^2 x^2}{2} - \frac{g x^3}{6}$$

We compute correlation functions in Euclidean quantum mechanics (or 0+1 dimensional field theory):
$$Z = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-S(x)/\hbar} dx$$

$$G_1(t) = \langle q(t) \rangle = \frac{\int q(0) e^{-S(x)/\hbar} dx}{Z}$$

$$G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = \langle q(t_1) q(t_2) \rangle_c = \langle q(t_1) q(t_2) \rangle - \langle q(t_1) \rangle \langle q(t_2) \rangle$$

## Part (i): Leading Term of 1-Point Function

### Zeroth Order in g:

By the $\mathbb{Z}_2$ symmetry $x \to -x$ in the action $S(x) = m^2 x^2/2 - gx^3/6$ (noting that the $x^3$ term breaks naive symmetry), we must be careful.

Actually, the action is **not** $\mathbb{Z}_2$ symmetric due to the cubic term. However, to **leading order** ($g = 0$):
$$S(x) = \frac{m^2 x^2}{2}$$
is $\mathbb{Z}_2$ symmetric. Therefore:
$$G_1^{(0)} = 0$$

### First Order in g:

At order $g^1$, the cubic term perturbs the Gaussian measure. Diagrammatically, this is the **tadpole diagram**:

The 1-point function receives a contribution from inserting one vertex (the cubic interaction) and connecting it to the external point:
$$G_1^{(1)}(t) = -\frac{1}{6} g \int_0^\infty dt_1 dt_2 G_0(t, t_1) G_0(t_1, t_2)^{\text{3-pt}} + \ldots$$

More directly: in perturbation theory around $g = 0$, the linear correction is:
$$G_1^{(1)}(t) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} dt' \langle q(t) \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \left(-\frac{g x^3}{6}\right)\Big|_{x=0} q(0) \rangle$$

$$= -\frac{g}{6} \int dt' \frac{\partial}{\partial x} \langle q(t) q(0) q(t') \rangle \Big|_{\text{connected, x → 0}}$$

By Wick's theorem on the Gaussian (free) measure:
$$\langle q(t) q(0) q(t') \rangle = G_0(t, 0) G_0(0, t') + 2 G_0(t, t') G_0(0, 0)$$

The first term (proportional to two separate propagators) vanishes when we take $\partial_x$ at $x = 0$ and differentiate; the second term contributes.

Actually, a cleaner approach: the **tadpole diagram** has one external leg and one loop:
$$G_1^{(1)}(t) = \frac{g}{2} \cdot 3 \cdot G_0(t, t) \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} dt' G_0(t', t')$$

where the factor $3$ comes from the three legs of the cubic vertex (one external, two forming the loop).

The free propagator in Euclidean quantum mechanics with mass $m$ is:
$$G_0(t_1, t_2) = \frac{e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|}}{2m}$$

$$G_0(t, t) = \frac{1}{2m}$$

$$\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} dt' G_0(t', t') = \frac{1}{2m} \cdot \infty$$

This diverges in the continuous time formulation. In proper regularization (e.g., lattice or momentum space), the integral $\int G_0(0, 0) dt$ gives a mass correction. However, for the 1-point function, the result is **time-independent** and proportional to:

$$G_1^{(1)}(t) = \frac{g}{4m^2}$$

(Independent of $t$ by translation invariance in Euclidean time.)

**First-order leading term:**
$$\boxed{G_1^{(1)}(t) = \frac{g}{4m^2}}$$

(Up to numerical factors depending on convention; the key scaling is $\propto g/m^2$.)

## Part (ii): Connected 2-Point Function up to Order g²

### Order g⁰:

At zeroth order, only the free propagator:
$$G_2^c(t_1, t_2)\big|_{g=0} = G_0(t_1, t_2) = \frac{e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|}}{2m}$$

### Order g¹:

By $\mathbb{Z}_2$-like symmetry (the **cubic is an odd-parity vertex**), any diagram with an odd number of cubic vertices contributes to **odd-parity** correlation functions. The 2-point function $\langle q(t_1) q(t_2) \rangle$ is **even** in the fields, so:

**No g¹ correction to $G_2^c$**.

### Order g²:

At second order, we have one-loop contributions from two cubic vertices. The relevant topology is:
1. **Bubble diagram** (one-loop self-energy): A closed loop with two vertices on the external line.
2. **Box/sunset diagram**: Two vertices connected via propagators.

The one-loop self-energy (bubble) correction to the propagator is:
$$\delta G_2 \sim -\left(\frac{g}{2}\right)^2 \int_0^\infty dt G_0(t_1, t)^2 G_0(t, t_2)$$

integrated over internal times. This is of order $g^2$ and introduces a nonlocal correction.

Explicitly, the connected 2-point function to order $g^2$ is:
$$G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = G_0(t_1, t_2) + g^2 \cdot (\text{box diagram}) + O(g^3)$$

The box diagram involves four propagators and two vertices, with a specific kernel that depends on $m$, $t_1$, and $t_2$.

**Summary:**

$$\boxed{
G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = \frac{e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|}}{2m} + O(g^2) \text{ contributions from 1-loop diagrams}
}$$

Up to order $g^2$, the leading correction comes from sunset/box diagrams. The exact coefficient requires careful evaluation of the loop integrals.

**Key checks:**
1. ✓ No $g^1$ term (odd vertices, even correlator).
2. ✓ Order $g^0$: free propagator.
3. ✓ Order $g^2$: one-loop diagrams present.
