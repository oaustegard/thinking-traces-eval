# Solution to ex_5_19: Triangle Groups and Orbifold Euler Characteristic

## Problem Setup

We have a hyperbolic triangle $\Delta$ in the upper half-plane with angles:
$$\alpha = \frac{\pi}{2}, \quad \beta = \frac{\pi}{3}, \quad \gamma = \frac{\pi}{7}$$

The group $\Gamma$ is generated by rotations $a, b, c$ around the three vertices by angles $2\alpha, 2\beta, 2\gamma$.

---

## Part (i): Topology and Orbifold Structure

### Fundamental Domain

The fundamental domain for the action of $\Gamma$ on $H$ consists of two copies of $\Delta$ glued along their boundaries:
- One copy of $\Delta$ itself
- Its mirror reflection across one edge

These two copies form a quadrilateral (or a half-disk pair) whose boundary edges are identified by the generators $a, b, c$.

**Topological identification:** The identification glues this quadrilateral/double-triangle into a **sphere** (genus 0 surface).

### Orbifold Structure

The quotient space $H / \Gamma$ is a 2-dimensional orbifold with three singular points (cone points), each with a cyclic stabilizer:

- **At vertex** corresponding to $a$: stabilizer $\langle a \rangle$ of order **2**
- **At vertex** corresponding to $b$: stabilizer $\langle b \rangle$ of order **3**
- **At vertex** corresponding to $c$: stabilizer $\langle c \rangle$ of order **7**

These are the images of the original triangle vertices under the quotient map.

---

## Part (ii): Orbifold Euler Characteristic

### Gauss-Bonnet Formula for Orbifolds

The orbifold Euler characteristic is computed as:
$$\chi^{\text{orb}}(H/\Gamma) = \chi^{\text{top}}(\text{underlying surface}) - \sum_{\text{cone points}} \left(1 - \frac{1}{n_i}\right)$$

where $n_i$ are the stabilizer orders.

**Underlying surface:** The quotient is topologically a sphere, $\chi^{\text{top}}(S^2) = 2$.

**Deficit angles:**
- Point with stabilizer of order 2: deficit $1 - 1/2 = 1/2$
- Point with stabilizer of order 3: deficit $1 - 1/3 = 2/3$
- Point with stabilizer of order 7: deficit $1 - 1/7 = 6/7$

### Calculation

$$\chi^{\text{orb}}(H/\Gamma) = 2 - \frac{1}{2} - \frac{2}{3} - \frac{6}{7}$$

Finding a common denominator (LCM of 1, 2, 3, 7 is 42):
$$= \frac{84}{42} - \frac{21}{42} - \frac{28}{42} - \frac{36}{42} = \frac{84 - 85}{42} = -\frac{1}{42}$$

Therefore:
$$\boxed{\chi(\Gamma) = -\frac{1}{42}}$$

### Verification via Hyperbolic Area

The hyperbolic area of the orbifold is:
$$\text{Area}(H/\Gamma) = -2\pi \chi^{\text{orb}} = -2\pi \cdot \left(-\frac{1}{42}\right) = \frac{\pi}{21}$$

By Gauss-Bonnet for the triangle, the area of $\Delta$ is:
$$\text{Area}(\Delta) = \pi \left(1 - \frac{\alpha}{\pi} - \frac{\beta}{\pi} - \frac{\gamma}{\pi}\right) = \pi \left(1 - \frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{3} - \frac{1}{7}\right)$$

$$= \pi \cdot \frac{42 - 21 - 14 - 6}{42} = \pi \cdot \frac{1}{42}$$

The fundamental domain is two copies of $\Delta$, so:
$$\text{Area}(H/\Gamma) = 2 \cdot \pi \cdot \frac{1}{42} = \frac{\pi}{21} \quad \checkmark$$

---

## Part (iii): Presentation of $\Gamma$

### Deriving the Relations

**Orders of generators:**
- The rotation $a$ by angle $2\alpha = \pi$ around a vertex has order 2: $a^2 = 1$
- The rotation $b$ by angle $2\beta = 2\pi/3$ around a vertex has order 3: $b^3 = 1$
- The rotation $c$ by angle $2\gamma = 2\pi/7$ around a vertex has order 7: $c^7 = 1$

**The relation $abc = 1$:**

Consider the composition $abc$. Going around all three vertices successively with the rotations adds up to a total rotation angle:
$$2\alpha + 2\beta + 2\gamma = 2(\alpha + \beta + \gamma) = 2 \cdot \frac{\pi}{2} + 2 \cdot \frac{\pi}{3} + 2 \cdot \frac{\pi}{7}$$

Actually, the key is that $a, b, c$ are the sides of a triangle, and their composition cycles around the triangle. In the orbifold quotient, the boundary of the fundamental domain is identified, so the composition of these three generators must be the identity:
$$abc = 1$$

Equivalently, in $\text{PSL}_2(\mathbb{R})$, the product of three rotations whose total angle equals $2(\alpha + \beta + \gamma) = \pi + 2\pi/3 + 2\pi/7 = \pi(1 + 2/3 + 2/7)$ fixes the center of the triangle (or is identity in PSL_2(R) acting on $H$).

### Presentation

The presentation of the $(2,3,7)$-triangle group is:
$$\boxed{\Gamma = \langle a, b, c \mid a^2 = b^3 = c^7 = abc = 1 \rangle}$$

This is the **triangle group** $\Delta(2,3,7)$, also denoted $\Gamma(2,3,7)$.

---

## Final Answer

$$\boxed{\text{(i) } H/\Gamma \text{ is homeomorphic to a sphere with three cone points of orders 2, 3, 7.}}$$

$$\boxed{\text{(ii) } \chi(\Gamma) = -\frac{1}{42}}$$

$$\boxed{\text{(iii) } \Gamma = \langle a, b, c \mid a^2, b^3, c^7, abc \rangle}$$
