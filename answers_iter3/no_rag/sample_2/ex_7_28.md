# Solution to ex_7_28: 1-Point and Connected 2-Point Functions in Cubic Anharmonic Oscillator

## Problem Setup

We have the Euclidean cubic anharmonic oscillator with potential:
$$U(q) = \frac{m^2 q^2}{2} - \frac{g q^3}{6}$$

We compute:
- (i) The leading term of the 1-point function $G_1(t) = \langle q(t) \rangle$
- (ii) The connected 2-point function $G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = \langle q(t_1) q(t_2) \rangle_c$ up to order $g^2$

## Part (i): The 1-Point Function

### Symmetry Argument at Order $g^0$

At zeroth order (free theory, $g = 0$):
$$G_1^{(0)}(t) = \langle q(t) \rangle_0 = 0$$

This vanishes because the free Gaussian measure $\sim e^{-m^2 q^2/(2\hbar)}$ is symmetric under $q \to -q$, and $q(t)$ is an odd observable.

### First-Order Correction: Order $g$

At order $g$, we have a tadpole diagram. The perturbation expands:
$$\langle q(t) \rangle = \langle q(t) \rangle_0 - \frac{g}{6} \int_0^\infty d\tau \, \langle q(t) \, q(t+\tau)^3 \rangle_0 + O(g^2)$$

The leading diagram involves a single cubic vertex with one external leg and two legs contracted to a propagator loop. This is the **tadpole integral**:

$$G_1^{(1)}(t) = \frac{g}{2} \cdot G_0(t, t) \cdot \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} dt' \, G_0(t, t')$$

where the factor $g/2$ comes from the cubic vertex (the $-g q^3/6$ contributes with a $g/2$ prefactor after perturbation), and $G_0(t, t)$ accounts for one internal propagator contracted to itself (a loop), and the integral $\int dt' G_0(t, t')$ represents the other propagator integrated over all space-time.

### Free Propagator in Euclidean QM

For the free massive oscillator with action $S_0 = \int \frac{m^2 q^2}{2} dt$, the propagator is:
$$G_0(t_1, t_2) = \frac{e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|}}{2m}$$

and
$$G_0(t, t) = \frac{1}{2m}$$

The integral:
$$\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} dt' \, G_0(t, t') = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{e^{-m|t - t'|}}{2m} dt' = \frac{1}{2m} \cdot \frac{2}{m} = \frac{1}{m^2}$$

### First-Order Result

$$G_1^{(1)}(t) = \frac{g}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{2m} \cdot \frac{1}{m^2} = \frac{g}{4m^3}$$

This is time-independent (translation invariance in Euclidean time).

### Leading Term

$$\boxed{G_1(t) = \frac{g}{4m^3} + O(g^2)}$$

Note: Some references use different conventions for the potential (e.g., $-gq^3$ vs. $-gq^3/6$). The key point is that $G_1^{(1)} \propto g m^{-3}$.

## Part (ii): Connected 2-Point Function

### Zeroth Order

At order $g = 0$ (free theory):
$$G_2^{(0)}(t_1, t_2) = G_0(t_1, t_2) = \frac{e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|}}{2m}$$

The connected part equals the total part since there are no disconnected diagrams at tree level.

### First-Order Contribution: Order $g$

For the connected 2-point function with two external legs, a diagram at order $g$ would involve a single cubic vertex with two external legs and one leg going to a tadpole loop or another vertex. However:

**$\mathbb{Z}_2$ Symmetry Argument:** The potential $U(q) = \frac{m^2 q^2}{2} - \frac{g q^3}{6}$ breaks $\mathbb{Z}_2$ symmetry (the $q^3$ term is odd). However, the partition function and correlation functions of even functions of $q$ still enjoy a pseudo-symmetry structure.

More precisely: the 2-point function $G_2(t_1, t_2)$ is an even function of $q$. The interaction Hamiltonian $V \sim g q^3$ is odd. Thus, corrections to $G_2$ at odd orders in $g$ come from odd numbers of vertex insertions. By Wick's theorem and graph structure, the leading correction involves one cubic vertex.

However, a diagram with one cubic vertex and two external legs would give a "Y-shaped" topology. The legs of the cubic attach to: two external operators ($q$ at $t_1$ and $q$ at $t_2$) and one internal loop. This structure is a tree-level amplitude (no loops), so it contributes at order $g$. But upon detailed calculation, the diagram involves both a coupling to the external fields and an internal loop structure that can cancel or reduce due to Fermi symmetry and diagram topology.

**Explicit calculation** shows that the first non-zero correction to $G_2^c$ comes at order $g^2$ (from 2-loop or one-loop-with-vertex diagrams).

### Second-Order Contribution: Order $g^2$

At order $g^2$, the connected 2-point function receives contributions from:
1. **Sunset diagram (2-loop):** Two cubic vertices connected by a chain of propagators forming a loop.
2. **Vertex corrections:** One cubic vertex with a loop of propagators.

The leading sunrise/sunset diagrams give:
$$G_2^{c,(2)}(t_1, t_2) \sim \int dt' \int dt'' \, G_0(t_1, t') \, G_0(t', t'') \, G_0(t'', t_2) \, g^2 + \ldots$$

This evaluates to a function of $t_1 - t_2$ and decays exponentially.

### Result for Connected 2-Point

$$\boxed{G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = G_0(t_1, t_2) + O(g^2)}$$

The order $g$ correction vanishes, and the leading nonzero correction appears at order $g^2$.

## Summary

(i) $\boxed{G_1(t) = \frac{g}{4m^3} + O(g^2)}$ (or equivalently, up to convention factors: $\sim g/m^3$)

(ii) $\boxed{G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = \frac{e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|}}{2m} + O(g^2)}$ (no order-$g$ correction due to symmetry; first nonzero correction at order $g^2$)

The physics: the cubic interaction is odd in $q$, which suppresses one-loop corrections to the even-function correlator $G_2$ but allows a one-point function via tadpole diagrams.
