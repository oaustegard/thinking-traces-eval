# Solution to ex_5_17: Free Groups and Generators of Gamma(N)

## Problem Overview

We examine the principal congruence subgroup $\Gamma(N)$ of $\text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$ (matrices congruent to the identity modulo $N$) and determine when it is free and how many generators it has.

## Part (a): Freeness of $\Gamma(N)$ for $N \geq 3$

### Claim: $\Gamma(N)$ acts freely on the upper half-plane $\mathbb{H}$ for $N \geq 3$.

**Proof:** A free action means no nontrivial element of $\Gamma(N)$ fixes any point in $\mathbb{H}$.

Suppose $\gamma \in \Gamma(N)$ with $\gamma \neq I$ fixes a point $z \in \mathbb{H}$. Then:
$$\gamma(z) = \frac{az + b}{cz + d} = z$$

where $\gamma = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix}$, so $az + b = cz + d$, giving $(a - d)z = d - b$ or $cz^2 + (d - a)z - b = 0$.

For an element of $\text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$ to fix a point, it must have a real fixed point or be elliptic (with $|a + d| < 2$). For $\gamma \in \Gamma(N)$ with $\gamma \equiv I \pmod{N}$:
$$\gamma = I + N \cdot A$$

for some matrix $A$. The characteristic polynomial is:
$$\det(\lambda I - \gamma) = (\lambda - a)(\lambda - d) - bc$$

For small $N$, elements in $\Gamma(N)$ congruent to the identity generate torsion-free actions. Specifically, torsion elements in $\text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$ are:
- Order 2: $S = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$ (not in $\Gamma(N)$ for $N \geq 3$)
- Order 3: $ST = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix}$ (not in $\Gamma(N)$ for $N \geq 3$)

For $N \geq 3$, no nontrivial element of $\Gamma(N)$ is congruent to a torsion element mod $N$. Thus, $\Gamma(N)$ has no torsion, and acts freely on $\mathbb{H}$.

### Fundamental Domain and Freeness:

Since $\Gamma(N)$ acts freely on the contractible space $\mathbb{H}$, the quotient $\mathbb{H}/\Gamma(N)$ is a $K(\Gamma(N), 1)$ space (an Eilenberg-MacLane space). The quotient is a Riemann surface (or orbifold) of genus $\geq 1$, hence has fundamental group that is free.

**Therefore, $\Gamma(N)$ is a free group for $N \geq 3$.**

### Gamma(2): $\Gamma_+(2) \times \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$

For $N = 2$, elements congruent to $I$ mod $2$ include both $I$ and $-I$:
$$-I \equiv I \pmod{2}$$

So $\Gamma(2)$ contains $\{I, -I\}$, the center of $\text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$. The quotient by this central subgroup is:
$$\Gamma_+(2) = \Gamma(2) / \{\pm I\}$$

which is the orientation-preserving subgroup with no central inversion. Thus:
$$\Gamma(2) \cong \Gamma_+(2) \times \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$$

$\Gamma_+(2)$ is generated by two elements (for example, $T^2$ and $ST^2S$, where $T$ and $S$ are the standard generators of $\text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$), making it a free group on 2 generators.

## Part (b): Number of Free Generators for $\Gamma(N)$, $N \geq 3$

We use the Euler characteristic formula. For a discrete group $\Gamma$ acting freely on a contractible space, the fundamental domain has Euler characteristic:
$$\chi(\mathbb{H}/\Gamma) = \frac{\chi(\text{fund. domain})}{\text{area}} \cdot \text{volume}$$

More directly, for a free group on $r$ generators:
$$\chi(\Gamma) = 1 - r$$

where $\chi(\Gamma)$ is computed from the orbifold Euler characteristic of $\mathbb{H}/\Gamma$.

### Orbifold Euler Characteristic:

By Gauss-Bonnet for hyperbolic surfaces and multiplicativity of Euler characteristics:
$$\chi(\Gamma(N)) = \frac{\chi(\text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}))}{\text{index}} = \frac{-1/12}{[\text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}) : \Gamma(N)]}$$

The index is:
$$[\text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}) : \Gamma(N)] = N^3 \prod_{p | N} \left(1 - \frac{1}{p^2}\right)$$

Therefore:
$$\chi(\Gamma(N)) = -\frac{N^3 \prod_p(1 - 1/p^2)}{12}$$

and the number of free generators is:
$$r = 1 - \chi(\Gamma(N)) = 1 + \frac{N^3 \prod_p(1 - 1/p^2)}{12}$$

### Examples:

- **$N = 3$:** Index $= 3^3 (1 - 1/9) = 27 \cdot 8/9 = 24$. So $\chi(\Gamma(3)) = -24/12 = -2$, and $r = 1 - (-2) = 3$ generators.
  
- **$N = 2$:** This case is special (has torsion). The formula is different, but $\Gamma_+(2)$ on its own has 2 generators.

## Final Answer

$$\boxed{\text{(a) } \Gamma(N) \text{ is free for } N \geq 3 \text{ due to torsion-free action on } \mathbb{H}. \Gamma(2) \cong \Gamma_+(2) \times \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}}$$

$$\boxed{\text{(b) Number of generators } = 1 + \frac{N^3 \prod_{p|N}(1 - p^{-2})}{12} \text{ for } N \geq 3}$$

For $N = 3$: **3 generators**. For $N = 2$: **2 generators** (in the free part $\Gamma_+(2)$).
