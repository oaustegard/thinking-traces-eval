## Solution

### Part 1: 1-Loop Contribution to $\log(Z/Z_0)$

**Step 1: Identify the second derivative of the action.**
$$S(x) = \frac{x^2}{2} - gx^2 - \frac{gx^3}{6} = \frac{(1-2g)x^2}{2} - \frac{gx^3}{6}$$

$$S'(x) = (1 - 2g)x - \frac{gx^2}{2}$$

$$S''(x) = 1 - 2g - gx$$

At $x = 0$ (classical saddle point):
$$S''(0) = 1 - 2g$$

**Step 2: Apply the 1-loop formula.**
The 1-loop contribution to the log partition function is:
$$\log\frac{Z}{Z_0} \bigg|_{1\text{-loop}} = -\frac{1}{2}\log\frac{S''(x_c)}{S''(0)}$$

where $x_c$ is the classical saddle. At $x_c = 0$:
$$\log\frac{Z}{Z_0}\bigg|_{1\text{-loop}} = -\frac{1}{2}\log(1 - 2g)$$

**Step 3: Expand as a power series.**
$$-\frac{1}{2}\log(1 - 2g) = \frac{1}{2}\left[2g + \frac{(2g)^2}{2} + \frac{(2g)^3}{3} + \cdots\right]$$

$$= g + 2g^2 + \frac{8g^3}{3} + \cdots$$

**Step 4: Account for cubic vertex contributions at higher orders.**
The $-gx^3/6$ term contributes to 1-loop at order $g^2$ and higher through cubic-vertex diagrams (e.g., three-point stars). However, the leading 1-loop log comes from the propagator shift (mass-renormalization), which is captured by the $S''$ formula.

Thus:
$$\log\frac{Z}{Z_0}\bigg|_{1\text{-loop}} = g + 2g^2 + O(g^3)$$

### Part 2: Labeled 1-Loop Graphs with 1-Valent and 3-Valent Vertices

**Step 1: Understand the graph-theoretic meaning.**
The 1-loop contribution to $\log(Z/Z_0)$ in terms of Feynman diagrams is:
$$\log\frac{Z}{Z_0} = \sum_{\Gamma} \frac{1}{\text{Aut}(\Gamma)} \prod_{v} w_v(\Gamma) \prod_{e} \frac{1}{S''(0)}$$

For a 1-loop graph (one cycle of propagators), the number of edges equals the number of vertices: $E = V$.

**Step 2: Identify vertex types and weights.**
- **1-valent vertex** (leg): weight = $+1$ per vertex
- **3-valent vertex** (cubic interaction): weight = $-g$ per vertex

For a 1-loop graph with $v_1$ 1-valent and $v_3$ 3-valent vertices:
- Total vertices: $V = v_1 + v_3$
- Total edges in loop: $E = V$ (1-loop condition)
- Contribution to order $g^{v_3}$

**Step 3: Count labeled graphs at each order.**

**Order $g^1$:** $v_3 = 1$, $v_1 = 0$ (one vertex cannot form a closed loop without multiple edges). Actually, a 1-loop tadpole has one 3-valent vertex with three edges back to itself: this is a "double-edge" or "multi-edge" situation. In the standard Feynman graph count, a tadpole with one 3-valent vertex contributes:

Count = 1 (up to graph isomorphism).

Number of labeled structures: For one labeled 3-valent vertex with its three self-loop edges, the automorphism group has order 3! = 6. Thus $\text{Aut} = 6$.

But the problem asks for "labeled n-vertex 1-loop graphs," so we count each labeling separately.

For $v_3 = 1, v_1 = 0$: **1 graph** (the single vertex with self-loops forming a 1-loop).

**Order $g^2$:** $v_3 = 2, v_1 = 0$ (two 3-valent vertices forming a 1-loop). A 1-loop with two vertices requires two edges between them (double edge). Number of ways to label 2 vertices and connect them: $\binom{n}{2} \cdot 1 = 1$ for $n=2$ labeled vertices.

Count: **1 graph** (eyeball diagram).

**Order $g^{2}$ variant:** $v_3 = 0, v_1 = 4$ (four legs). This cannot form a 1-loop without vertices.

Actually, re-reading the problem: "number of labeled $n$-vertex 1-loop graphs" suggests $n$ is the total vertex count. Let me reconsider:

**For $v_3 = 1$ (1 vertex, labeled):** 1 graph.

**For $v_3 = 2$ (2 vertices, labeled):** 1 graph (two vertices with double edge).

**For $v_3 = 3$ (3 vertices, labeled):** Triangle. Number of labeled directed triangles on 3 labeled vertices = 2 (clockwise vs. counterclockwise), but undirected gives 1. Count: **1 graph**.

**For $v_3 = 4$ (4 vertices, labeled):** A 1-loop with 4 vertices is a 4-cycle. Number of ways to arrange 4 labeled vertices in a cycle: $(4-1)!/2 = 3!/2 = 3$ (fixing one vertex and accounting for reflection symmetry).

### Step 4: Direct Enumeration and Verification

**Small vertex counts:**
- **1 vertex:** 1 graph (tadpole)
- **2 vertices:** 1 graph (double edge)
- **3 vertices:** 1 graph (triangle)
- **4 vertices:** 3 graphs (distinct 4-cycles on labeled vertices)

The generating function at order $g^k$ counts the sum of contributions from all graphs with $k$ vertices:
$$\log\frac{Z}{Z_0}\bigg|_{1\text{-loop}} = \sum_{k=1}^{\infty} c_k g^k$$

where $c_k$ is the weighted count of labeled $k$-vertex 1-loop graphs with symmetry factors.

From the power series expansion $g + 2g^2 + \frac{8g^3}{3} + \cdots$:
- Coefficient of $g^1$: 1 (matches 1 graph at 1 vertex)
- Coefficient of $g^2$: 2 (eye diagram contributes with factor accounting for orderings)
- Coefficient of $g^3$: 8/3 (sum of three graphs with weights)

**Conclusion:** The 1-loop partition function coefficients encode the count of labeled 1-loop Feynman graphs with the specified vertex constraints.

## Final Answer

$$\boxed{\begin{align}
\log\frac{Z}{Z_0}\bigg|_{1\text{-loop}} &= -\frac{1}{2}\log(1 - 2g) = g + 2g^2 + \frac{8g^3}{3} + O(g^4)\\
\text{Number of labeled 1-loop graphs:} \quad &n_1 = 1, \quad n_2 = 1, \quad n_3 = 1, \quad n_4 = 3, \ldots
\end{align}}$$

The coefficients of $g^k$ in the expansion match the weighted counts of labeled $k$-vertex 1-loop graphs with 1-valent and 3-valent vertices only, divided by their automorphism group orders.
