## Solution

### Part (a): Freeness of $\Gamma(N)$ for $N \geq 3$

**Step 1: Show $\Gamma(N)$ acts freely on the upper half-plane $\mathbb{H}$.**

Recall that $\Gamma(N)$ consists of matrices in $SL_2(\mathbb{Z})$ congruent to the identity modulo $N$:
$$\Gamma(N) = \left\{\begin{pmatrix}a & b \\ c & d\end{pmatrix} \in SL_2(\mathbb{Z}) : \begin{pmatrix}a & b \\ c & d\end{pmatrix} \equiv \begin{pmatrix}1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1\end{pmatrix} \pmod{N}\right\}$$

A nontrivial element of $\gamma \in \Gamma(N)$ fixes a point $z \in \mathbb{H}$ if and only if $\gamma$ is **elliptic** (conjugate over $\mathbb{C}$ to a rotation).

The torsion elements in $SL_2(\mathbb{Z})$ are:
- **Order 2:** the matrix $S = \begin{pmatrix}0 & -1 \\ 1 & 0\end{pmatrix}$ (and conjugates)
- **Order 3:** the matrix $ST = \begin{pmatrix}-1 & -1 \\ 1 & 0\end{pmatrix}$ (and conjugates)
- **Order 6:** products like $TST^{-1}S$

**For $N \geq 3$:**
The matrix $S \equiv \begin{pmatrix}0 & -1 \\ 1 & 0\end{pmatrix} \not\equiv I \pmod{N}$ (the $(1,2)$ entry is $-1$, not congruent to $0$ mod $N \geq 3$).

Similarly, order-3 torsion elements are not congruent to the identity modulo $N \geq 3$.

**Conclusion:** No nontrivial element of $\Gamma(N)$ is a torsion element, so $\Gamma(N)$ contains no elliptic elements. Thus, the action of $\Gamma(N)$ on $\mathbb{H}$ is **free** (no nontrivial element fixes any point).

**Step 2: Deduce $\Gamma(N)$ is a free group.**

A free action of $\Gamma(N)$ on the contractible space $\mathbb{H}$ means that $\mathbb{H}/\Gamma(N)$ is a $K(\Gamma(N), 1)$ (Eilenberg-MacLane space).

The quotient $\mathbb{H}/\Gamma(N)$ is a non-compact Riemann surface (with cusps). Its fundamental group is $\Gamma(N)$.

A non-compact Riemann surface with $g$ handles and $n$ cusps deformation-retracts to a one-dimensional CW complex (a graph). The fundamental group of a graph is free.

**Conclusion:** $\Gamma(N)$ is a **free group** for $N \geq 3$.

### Part (a): $\Gamma(2)$ as a Direct Product

**Step 1: Identify the quotient by $\pm I$.**

Recall that $\Gamma(2)$ is defined modulo 2:
$$\Gamma(2) = \left\{\begin{pmatrix}a & b \\ c & d\end{pmatrix} \in SL_2(\mathbb{Z}) : \begin{pmatrix}a & b \\ c & d\end{pmatrix} \equiv I \pmod{2}\right\}$$

The matrix $-I = \begin{pmatrix}-1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1\end{pmatrix}$ satisfies:
$$-I \equiv \begin{pmatrix}1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1\end{pmatrix} \pmod{2}$$

(since $-1 \equiv 1 \pmod{2}$). Thus $-I \in \Gamma(2)$.

**Step 2: Factor out $\{I, -I\}$.**

Define:
$$\Gamma_+(2) := \Gamma(2) / \{\pm I\}$$

This is the quotient by the central subgroup generated by $-I$. Since $-I$ is central and of order 2:
$$\Gamma(2) = \Gamma_+(2) \times \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$$

(direct product).

**Step 3: Show $\Gamma_+(2)$ is free.**

The quotient $PSL_2(\mathbb{Z}) / PSL_2(\mathbb{Z}) \cap \Gamma_+(2)$ ... [alternatively: $\Gamma_+(2)$ acts freely on $\mathbb{H}$ as $-I$ is the only element that acts trivially when projected.] 

By the same argument as before, $\Gamma_+(2)$ is a free group. Standard SL_2(\mathbb{Z}) generators $T, S$ satisfy:
- $T = \begin{pmatrix}1 & 1 \\ 0 & 1\end{pmatrix}$ has order $\infty$ (parabolic, not in torsion subgroup)
- $S$ has order 2 (but $S \not\in \Gamma(2)$)

The generators of $\Gamma_+(2)$ are, e.g., $\alpha = T^2$ and $\beta = ST^2S$ (both have infinite order and generate freely).

**Conclusion:** $\Gamma(2) = \Gamma_+(2) \times \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$ where $\Gamma_+(2)$ is a **free group on 2 generators**.

### Part (b): Number of Free Generators of $\Gamma(N)$ for $N \geq 3$

**Step 1: Apply the rank formula $r = 1 - \chi(\Gamma(N))$.**

For a torsion-free group $\Gamma(N)$, the Euler characteristic of its classifying space $K(\Gamma(N), 1)$ is:
$$\chi(\Gamma(N)) = 1 - r$$

where $r$ is the rank of $\Gamma(N)$ as a free group (number of free generators).

Thus:
$$r = 1 - \chi(\Gamma(N))$$

**Step 2: Compute $\chi(\Gamma(N))$ using the index formula.**

We use the multiplicativity of $\chi$ for finite-index subgroups of $SL_2(\mathbb{Z})$:
$$\chi(\Gamma(N)) = \frac{\chi(SL_2(\mathbb{Z}))}{[\SL_2(\mathbb{Z}) : \Gamma(N)]}$$

The Euler characteristic of $SL_2(\mathbb{Z})$ (as a torsion group) is computed via orbifold Euler characteristic:
$$\chi^{\text{orb}}(SL_2(\mathbb{Z})) = \chi(\mathbb{H}/SL_2(\mathbb{Z})) = -\frac{1}{12}$$

(The orbifold quotient $\mathbb{H}/SL_2(\mathbb{Z})$ has singular points of orders 2 and 3, contributing deficits. The formula uses the genus and cone-point formula.)

**Step 3: Compute the index $[SL_2(\mathbb{Z}) : \Gamma(N)]$.**

By reduction modulo $N$:
$$[SL_2(\mathbb{Z}) : \Gamma(N)] = |SL_2(\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z})|$$

The order of $SL_2(\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z})$ is:
$$|SL_2(\mathbb{Z}/N\mathbb{Z})| = N^3 \prod_{p | N} \left(1 - \frac{1}{p^2}\right)$$

For example:
- $N = 3$: $|SL_2(\mathbb{Z}/3\mathbb{Z})| = 27 \cdot (1 - 1/9) = 24$
- $N = 2$: $|SL_2(\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z})| = 8 \cdot (1 - 1/4) = 6$

**Step 4: Apply the formula.**

$$\chi(\Gamma(N)) = \frac{-1/12}{N^3 \prod_{p|N}(1 - 1/p^2)} = -\frac{1}{12N^3 \prod_{p|N}(1 - 1/p^2)}$$

Thus, the number of free generators is:
$$r = 1 - \chi(\Gamma(N)) = 1 + \frac{1}{12N^3 \prod_{p|N}(1 - 1/p^2)}$$

**Step 5: Sample calculation for $N = 3$.**
$$r = 1 + \frac{1}{12 \cdot 27 \cdot (8/9)} = 1 + \frac{1}{12 \cdot 24} = 1 + \frac{1}{288}$$

Wait, this doesn't give an integer. Let me recalculate: $|SL_2(\mathbb{Z}/3)| = 24$, so:
$$\chi(\Gamma(3)) = \frac{-1/12}{24} = -\frac{1}{288}$$

$$r = 1 - (-1/288) = 1 + \frac{1}{288}$$

This still isn't an integer. **Correction:** For torsion-free subgroups, use:
$$r = 1 - \chi(\Gamma(N)) = 1 - \left(-\frac{1}{12[\SL_2(\mathbb{Z}) : \Gamma(N)]}\right) = 1 + \frac{1}{12 \cdot 24} = 1 + \frac{1}{288}$$

Actually, the **correct formula** (avoiding the orbifold issue) is:
$$r = 1 + \frac{[\SL_2(\mathbb{Z}) : \Gamma(N)]}{12}$$

**For $N = 3$:** $r = 1 + 24/12 = 1 + 2 = 3$ ✓

**General formula:**
$$\boxed{r = 1 + \frac{N^3}{12}\prod_{p|N}\left(1 - \frac{1}{p^2}\right)}$$

$$\boxed{\begin{align}

&\quad\quad \Gamma(2) = \Gamma_+(2) \times \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}\\
&\quad\text{where } \Gamma_+(2) \text{ is free on 2 generators.}\\
&\\
&\text{(b)} \quad \text{Number of free generators of } \Gamma(N), N \geq 3:\\
&\quad\quad r = 1 + \frac{N^3}{12}\prod_{p|N}\left(1 - \frac{1}{p^2}\right)\\
&\text{Example: } N=3 \Rightarrow r = 1 + \frac{27 \cdot (8/9)}{12} = 3
\end{align}}$$
