## Solution

### Part (a): Showing Gamma(N) is Free for N >= 3

**Step 1: Free action on the upper half-plane**

For $N \geq 3$, the principal congruence subgroup $\Gamma(N)$ consists of matrices $\begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix} \in \text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$ with $a \equiv d \equiv 1 \pmod{N}$ and $b \equiv c \equiv 0 \pmod{N}$.

Any such matrix, when viewed as an element of $\text{PSL}_2(\mathbb{R})$ (projective action on the upper half-plane $H$), acts by the Möbius transformation:
$$z \mapsto \frac{az + b}{cz + d}$$

A fixed point would satisfy $z = \frac{az + b}{cz + d}$, i.e., $cz^2 + (d-a)z - b = 0$.

For a nontrivial element $\gamma \in \Gamma(N)$ to fix a point in $H$, the matrix must have a conjugate pair of eigenvalues (giving an elliptic element). Elliptic elements in $\text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$ are generated by roots of unity, and specifically:
- Order 2: $\begin{pmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$
- Order 3: $\begin{pmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{pmatrix}$
- Order 4: powers of $\begin{pmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}^2$
- Order 6: products

For $N \geq 3$, none of these elliptic elements are congruent to the identity modulo $N$ (since their off-diagonal entries are nonzero and noncongruent to 0 mod $N$).

Therefore, $\Gamma(N)$ acts freely on $H$ (no nontrivial element fixes any point in $H$).

**Step 2: Fundamental group is free**

By a classical theorem, if a discrete group acts freely on a contractible space (like $H$), then the quotient $H/\Gamma(N)$ is a $K(\Gamma(N), 1)$ space.

For a surface, the fundamental group of an open Riemann surface (which $H/\Gamma(N)$ is, for $N \geq 3$) is always a free group.

Therefore, **$\Gamma(N)$ is a free group for $N \geq 3$**.

**Step 3: Gamma(2) structure**

For $\Gamma(2)$, the torsion element $-I = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{pmatrix}$ satisfies $-I \equiv \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \pmod{2}$ (since $2 | (-1 - 1)$), so $-I \in \Gamma(2)$.

The subgroup $\Gamma_+(2)$ of $\Gamma(2)$ consisting of matrices with positive $(1,1)$-entry is a free group on 2 generators (e.g., $T^2 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}$ and $ST^2S = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 2 \\ -2 & 3 \end{pmatrix}$ in SL_2(Z) generators).

Since $\Gamma(2)$ contains both $\Gamma_+(2)$ and the central element $-I$ (which generates $\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$), and these generate $\Gamma(2)$ with no additional relations:

$$\boxed{\Gamma(2) = \Gamma_+(2) \times \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}}$$

### Part (b): Number of Free Generators for N >= 3

**Step 1: Construct K(Gamma(N), 1)**

Since $\Gamma(N)$ is torsion-free, the upper half-plane $H$ with its fundamental domain under $\Gamma(N)$ action gives a $K(\Gamma(N), 1)$ space. The quotient $H/\Gamma(N)$ is an open surface of finite genus $g(\Gamma(N))$.

**Step 2: Compute Euler characteristic**

The Euler characteristic is related to the fundamental group rank $r$ (number of generators) by:
$$r = 1 - \chi(\Gamma(N))$$

where $\chi(\Gamma(N))$ is the Euler characteristic of any $K(\Gamma(N), 1)$ space.

For a torsion-free subgroup of finite index in $\text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$:
$$\chi(\Gamma(N)) = -\frac{[\text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}) : \Gamma(N)]}{12}$$

This follows from the orbifold Euler characteristic of $\text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$ being $-1/12$.

**Step 3: Compute the index**

The index of $\Gamma(N)$ in $\text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$ is:
$$[\text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}) : \Gamma(N)] = N^3 \prod_{p | N} \left(1 - \frac{1}{p^2}\right)$$

**Step 4: Apply rank formula**

The number of generators is:
$$r(\Gamma(N)) = 1 - \chi(\Gamma(N)) = 1 + \frac{[\text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}) : \Gamma(N)]}{12} = 1 + \frac{N^3}{12}\prod_{p|N}\left(1-\frac{1}{p^2}\right)$$

**Example: $N = 3$**
$$[\text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}) : \Gamma(3)] = 27 \cdot \left(1 - \frac{1}{9}\right) = 27 \cdot \frac{8}{9} = 24$$

$$r(\Gamma(3)) = 1 + \frac{24}{12} = 1 + 2 = 3$$

## Final Answer

**(a)** For $N \geq 3$: $\Gamma(N)$ acts freely on the upper half-plane (no torsion), making it a free group. For $N = 2$: $\Gamma(2) = \Gamma_+(2) \times \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$ where $\Gamma_+(2)$ is free on 2 generators.

$$\boxed{\Gamma(N) \text{ is free for } N \geq 3; \quad \Gamma(2) = F_2 \times \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}}$$

**(b)** The number of free generators of $\Gamma(N)$ for $N \geq 3$ is:

$$\boxed{r(\Gamma(N)) = 1 + \frac{N^3}{12}\prod_{p|N}\left(1-\frac{1}{p^2}\right)}$$

For $N = 3$: $r(\Gamma(3)) = 3$ generators.
