**Key difference:** ex_2_14_wallis handles steepest descent on a *real* domain with boundary extrema; ex_2_15 requires steepest descent on a *periodic* domain $[0, 2\pi]$ with interior critical point(s). The Bessel integral has *period*, not a boundary.

---

## Solution

### Part 1: Asymptotic regime as $a \to +\infty$

**Setup:**
$$I_0(a) = \frac{1}{2\pi}\int_0^{2\pi} e^{a\cos\theta}\, d\theta$$

The phase is $f(\theta) = \cos\theta$. Its maximum on $[0, 2\pi]$ occurs at $\theta = 0$ (and by periodicity also at $\theta = 2\pi$) with $f(0) = 1$.

**Steepest descent:**
Near $\theta = 0$, expand:
$$\cos\theta = 1 - \frac{\theta^2}{2} + \frac{\theta^4}{24} - \cdots$$

The exponent is:
$$a\cos\theta = a - \frac{a\theta^2}{2} + \frac{a\theta^4}{24} - \cdots$$

**Leading term (Gaussian approximation):**
$$I_0(a) \sim \frac{1}{2\pi}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{a - a\theta^2/2}\, d\theta = \frac{e^a}{2\pi}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-a\theta^2/2}\, d\theta$$

Using $\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-\alpha\theta^2}\, d\theta = \sqrt{\pi/\alpha}$ with $\alpha = a/2$:
$$I_0(a) \sim \frac{e^a}{2\pi}\sqrt{\frac{\pi \cdot 2}{a}} = \frac{e^a}{\sqrt{2\pi a}}$$

**Second-order correction:**
Include the next term in the expansion $\cos\theta \approx 1 - \theta^2/2 + \theta^4/24$.

The integral becomes:
$$\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-a\theta^2/2 + a\theta^4/24}\, d\theta$$

Using the formula for Gaussian fluctuations, the correction to the exponential is $+\frac{1}{8a}$ in the logarithm, giving a multiplicative factor of $e^{1/(8a)} \approx 1 + \frac{1}{8a}$.

**Result for $a \to +\infty$:**
$$I_0(a) \sim \frac{e^a}{\sqrt{2\pi a}}\left(1 + \frac{1}{8a} + O(a^{-2})\right)$$

---

### Part 2: Asymptotic regime as $a \to i\infty$ (i.e., $a = ib$ with $b \to +\infty$)

**Setup:**
$$I_0(ib) = \frac{1}{2\pi}\int_0^{2\pi} e^{ib\cos\theta}\, d\theta$$

The phase is now $f(\theta) = \cos\theta$ (real) multiplied by pure imaginary $ib$. The exponent oscillates: $ib\cos\theta$ ranges from $-ib$ to $+ib$ as $\theta$ varies.

**Stationary phase points:**
The phase $\phi(\theta) = b\cos\theta$ has critical points where $d\phi/d\theta = -b\sin\theta = 0$, i.e., at $\theta = 0$ and $\theta = \pi$.

At $\theta = 0$: $\phi(0) = b$, $\phi''(0) = -b$.
At $\theta = \pi$: $\phi(\pi) = -b$, $\phi''(\pi) = +b$.

**Stationary phase formula:**
Near $\theta = 0$: $\cos\theta \approx 1 - \theta^2/2$ gives
$$e^{ib\cos\theta} \approx e^{ib}e^{-ib\theta^2/2}$$

The contribution is:
$$\frac{e^{ib}}{2\pi}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-ib\theta^2/2}\, d\theta = \frac{e^{ib}}{2\pi}\sqrt{\frac{\pi \cdot 2}{ib}} = \frac{e^{ib}}{\sqrt{2\pi ib}}$$

Near $\theta = \pi$ (write $\theta = \pi + \phi$): $\cos\theta \approx -1 + \phi^2/2$, giving
$$e^{ib\cos\theta} \approx e^{-ib}e^{ib\phi^2/2}$$

The contribution is:
$$\frac{e^{-ib}}{2\pi}\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{ib\phi^2/2}\, d\phi = \frac{e^{-ib}}{2\pi}\sqrt{\frac{\pi \cdot 2}{-ib}} = \frac{e^{-ib}}{\sqrt{2\pi \cdot (-ib)}}$$

**Combining:**
$$I_0(ib) \sim \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi ib}}\left(e^{ib} + e^{-ib}\right) = \frac{2\cos b}{\sqrt{2\pi ib}}$$

The factor $\sqrt{ib} = \sqrt{b} \cdot e^{i\pi/4}$, so:
$$I_0(ib) \sim \frac{2\cos b}{\sqrt{2\pi b}}e^{-i\pi/4} = \sqrt{\frac{2}{\pi b}}\cos\left(b - \frac{\pi}{4}\right)$$

---

## Final Answer

**As $a \to +\infty$:**
$$\boxed{I_0(a) \sim \frac{e^a}{\sqrt{2\pi a}}\left(1 + \frac{1}{8a} + O(a^{-2})\right)}$$

**As $a = ib$ with $b \to +\infty$:**
$$\boxed{I_0(ib) \sim \sqrt{\frac{2}{\pi b}}\cos\left(b - \frac{\pi}{4}\right)}$$
