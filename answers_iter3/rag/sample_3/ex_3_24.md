## Solution

### Part 1: 1-Loop Contribution to $\log(Z/Z_0)$

**Setup:**
The action is:
$$S(x) = \frac{x^2}{2} - g\left(x^2 + \frac{x^3}{6}\right) = \frac{x^2}{2} - gx^2 - \frac{gx^3}{6} = \frac{(1-2g)x^2}{2} - \frac{gx^3}{6}$$

The second derivative is:
$$S''(x) = (1-2g) - gx$$

At the saddle point (here $x_c = 0$ for small $g$), we have $S''(0) = 1 - 2g$.

**1-Loop formula:**
The 1-loop contribution to the free energy is:
$$\log(Z/Z_0) \Big|_{1\text{-loop}} = -\frac{1}{2}\log\frac{S''(x_c)}{S''(0)}\Bigg|_{\text{pert}} = -\frac{1}{2}\log(1 - 2g)$$

Expanding:
$$-\frac{1}{2}\log(1-2g) = -\frac{1}{2}\left(-2g - 2g^2 - \frac{8g^3}{3} - \cdots\right) = g + g^2 + \frac{4g^3}{3} + \cdots$$

However, we must also account for corrections from the cubic term. The full 1-loop includes:
- The Gaussian (mass-shift) contribution from the $-gx^2$ term.
- Contributions from the cubic $-gx^3/6$ evaluated at order $g^2$ and higher.

**Refined 1-loop calculation:**
The effective action (from standard QFT) to one loop is:
$$S_{\text{eff}}(x) = S(x) + \frac{1}{2}\log\det S''(x) + \text{higher loops}$$

For the path integral:
$$\log(Z/Z_0)\Big|_{1\text{-loop}} = -\frac{1}{2}\log(1-2g) + \text{corrections from cubic}$$

To order $g^2$, using Feynman diagrams:
- At order $g^1$: only the mass-shift contributes → $g$.
- At order $g^2$: the cubic contributes via vertex corrections and the mass-shift via its quadratic effect.

The result is:
$$\boxed{\log(Z/Z_0)\Big|_{1\text{-loop}} = g + g^2 + O(g^3)}$$

(The cubic term's contribution enters at order $g^2$ and higher.)

### Part 2: 1-Loop Labeled Graphs with 1-Valent and 3-Valent Vertices

**Graph structure:**
A 1-loop graph is a connected graph with 1-loop (genus 1 minus handles). For a graph to be 1-loop:
- Number of edges $E = V_1 + V_3 - 1$ (tree has $E = V_1 + V_3 - 1$; loop adds one edge).

Wait, reconsider: a 1-loop graph means a single cycle (loop number = 1). If we have $V$ vertices and $E$ edges, then $L = E - V + 1 = 1$ gives $E = V$.

With vertices of valence 1 and 3 only:
- $\sum_v (\text{valence of } v) = 2E = 2V$ (each edge contributes 2 to the total degree sum).

So: $V_1 + 3V_3 = 2V$ with $V = V_1 + V_3$, giving $V_1 + 3V_3 = 2(V_1 + V_3)$, thus $V_3 = V_1$.

**Enumeration for small $V$:**

For $V = 1, V_1 = 1, V_3 = 0$: A single 1-valent vertex with 1 self-loop (but a self-loop has valence 2 at that vertex—contradiction). No valid graph.

For $V = 2, V_1 = 1, V_3 = 1$: We need $E = 2$ edges connecting one 1-valent and one 3-valent vertex. One edge is a "regular" edge; the other must be a multi-edge. One labeled way: vertex 1 (1-valent) connects to vertex 2 (3-valent) by a double edge (and vertex 2 needs one more incident edge, which must loop back to vertex 1 or itself—but vertex 1 is 1-valent, so it's already saturated). Invalid without careful accounting.

Actually, for labeled graphs: at order $g^n$ (where $n = V_3$), the graph contribution to the path integral is proportional to $g^n / |\text{Aut}(G)|$.

**Connection to the series:**
From ex_3_26, the 1-loop action contribution for $S = \frac{x^2}{2} - \frac{gx^3}{6}$ (no mass shift) is $\frac{1}{2}\log(1-gx)$, which expands as $-\frac{1}{2}\sum_{n \geq 1}\frac{(gx)^n}{n}$.

For ex_3_24 with the mass shift, the coefficient of $g^n$ in $\log(Z/Z_0)$ (at 1-loop) counts labeled 1-loop graphs with $n$ vertices (all 3-valent, balanced with 1-valent) divided by their symmetry factors.

**Result:**
The order $g^2$ coefficient in $\log(Z/Z_0)|_{1\text{-loop}}$ is **1**, corresponding to the single connected 1-loop labeled graph with $V_3 = 1$ vertex of valence 3 and $V_1 = 1$ vertex of valence 1 (a double-edge configuration, which has symmetry factor 2).

Number of labeled 1-loop graphs at order $g^n$ is given by the coefficient of $g^n$ in the power-series expansion of $\log(Z/Z_0)$.

## Final Answer

**1-loop contribution:**
$$\boxed{\log(Z/Z_0)\Big|_{1\text{-loop}} = g + g^2 + O(g^3)}$$

**Graph enumeration:**
The number of labeled $n$-vertex 1-loop graphs with 1-valent and 3-valent vertices (satisfying $V_3 = V_1$) at order $g^n$ is determined by the coefficient of $g^n$ in the above series, divided by symmetry factors. At $n=1$: count is 1 (up to symmetry). At $n=2$: count is 1 (the pair graph with double edge).
