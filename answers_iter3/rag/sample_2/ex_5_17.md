## Solution

### Part (a): Freeness of $\Gamma(N)$ for $N \geq 3$

**Claim:** $\Gamma(N)$ is a free group for $N \geq 3$.

**Proof via torsion-free action on $\mathbb{H}$:**

The group $\Gamma(N)$ acts on the upper half-plane $\mathbb{H}$ by Möbius transformations. A matrix $\gamma = \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix} \in \text{PSL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$ has a fixed point in $\mathbb{H}$ only if it is elliptic (has finite order) or parabolic (has order $\infty$ but a fixed point on the boundary).

**Key fact:** The elliptic elements in $\text{PSL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$ are generated by matrices of finite order, specifically:
- Order 2: $S = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}$
- Order 3: $ST = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix}$

For $N \geq 3$, any element congruent to the identity modulo $N$ cannot be an elliptic element of order $> 1$ because:
- $S \equiv \begin{pmatrix} 0 & -1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix} \not\equiv I \pmod{N}$ for $N \geq 3$ (the $(0,-1)$ and $(1,0)$ entries prevent congruence to $I$).
- Similarly, $ST$ and other finite-order generators are not congruent to $I$ modulo $N$ for $N \geq 3$.

Thus, **$\Gamma(N)$ has no nontrivial torsion for $N \geq 3$**, i.e., it is torsion-free.

A torsion-free discrete group acting freely on a contractible space (like $\mathbb{H}$) has a classifying space $K(\Gamma(N), 1) = \mathbb{H}/\Gamma(N)$ that is an open Riemann surface (a hyperbolic orbifold without singular points, since the action is free).

**Key topological fact:** The fundamental group of an open Riemann surface is free. Therefore, $\Gamma(N)$ is free.

**Freeness of $\Gamma(2)$:**

The group $\Gamma(2) = \{\gamma \in \text{PSL}_2(\mathbb{Z}) : \gamma \equiv I \pmod{2}\}$ has a special structure:
- It contains $-I = \begin{pmatrix} -1 & 0 \\ 0 & -1 \end{pmatrix}$, which is congruent to $I$ modulo 2 (since $-1 \equiv 1 \pmod{2}$).
- The center of $\text{PSL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$ is trivial in $\text{PSL}_2$ (we work with projective matrices), but the element $-I$ generates a subgroup of order 2.

Thus, $\Gamma(2)$ is a central extension:
$$1 \to \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z} \to \Gamma(2) \to \Gamma_+(2) \to 1$$

where $\Gamma_+(2)$ is the "oriented" subgroup (index 2 in $\Gamma(2)$). Since $\Gamma_+(2)$ is torsion-free and acts freely on $\mathbb{H}$, it is free. Thus, $\Gamma(2) = \Gamma_+(2) \times \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$ is the direct product of a free group and $\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$.

Explicitly, $\Gamma_+(2)$ is generated by $T^2 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix}$ and $ST^2S = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ -2 & 1 \end{pmatrix}$ (or equivalent standard generators), and these are free generators.

### Part (b): Number of Free Generators of $\Gamma(N)$ for $N \geq 3$

**Apply Euler characteristic method:**

For a torsion-free group $\Gamma$, the number of free generators $r$ is related to the Euler characteristic by:
$$r = 1 - \chi(\Gamma)$$

**Compute $\chi(\Gamma(N))$ using the index formula:**

The Euler characteristic is multiplicative under finite-index subgroups:
$$\chi(\Gamma(N)) = \frac{\chi(\text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}))}{[\text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}) : \Gamma(N)]}$$

More precisely, since $\text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})$ has torsion (elements of order 2 and 3), we use the orbifold Euler characteristic:
$$\chi^{\text{orb}}(\text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z})) = -\frac{1}{12}$$

For $N \geq 3$, the index is:
$$[\text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}) : \Gamma(N)] = N^3 \prod_{p | N} \left(1 - \frac{1}{p^2}\right)$$

Thus:
$$\chi(\Gamma(N)) = -\frac{1}{12} \cdot \left[\text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}) : \Gamma(N)\right] = -\frac{1}{12} N^3 \prod_{p|N}\left(1 - \frac{1}{p^2}\right)$$

**Number of free generators:**
$$r = 1 - \chi(\Gamma(N)) = 1 + \frac{1}{12} N^3 \prod_{p|N}\left(1 - \frac{1}{p^2}\right)$$

**Sample calculations:**

For $N = 3$ (prime):
$$r = 1 + \frac{1}{12} \cdot 27 \cdot \left(1 - \frac{1}{9}\right) = 1 + \frac{27}{12} \cdot \frac{8}{9} = 1 + \frac{2 \cdot 27}{12} = 1 + \frac{54}{12} = 1 + \frac{9}{2}$$

Wait, let me recalculate: 
$$[\text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}) : \Gamma(3)] = 3^3 \cdot \left(1 - \frac{1}{9}\right) = 27 \cdot \frac{8}{9} = 24$$

$$\chi(\Gamma(3)) = -\frac{24}{12} = -2$$

$$r = 1 - (-2) = 3$$

For $N = 2$:
$$[\text{SL}_2(\mathbb{Z}) : \Gamma(2)] = 2^3 \cdot \left(1 - \frac{1}{4}\right) = 8 \cdot \frac{3}{4} = 6$$

$$\chi(\Gamma(2)) = -\frac{6}{12} = -\frac{1}{2}$$

But $\Gamma(2)$ has torsion, so the formula $r = 1 - \chi$ needs adjustment. For $\Gamma(2) = \Gamma_+(2) \times \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$:
$$\chi(\Gamma(2)) = \chi(\Gamma_+(2)) \cdot \chi(\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}) = \chi(\Gamma_+(2)) \cdot 0 = 0$$

Thus, $\Gamma_+(2)$ (the free part) has $\chi(\Gamma_+(2)) = 1 - 2 = -1$ (using $[\text{SL}_2 : \Gamma_+(2)] = 12$), so $r_+ = 1 - (-1) = 2$.

## Final Answer

**(a)** For $N \geq 3$, $\Gamma(N)$ acts freely and torsion-freely on $\mathbb{H}$, so $\mathbb{H}/\Gamma(N)$ is an open Riemann surface with fundamental group $\Gamma(N)$. Since fundamental groups of open surfaces are free, **$\Gamma(N)$ is a free group**.

For $N = 2$, **$\Gamma(2) = \Gamma_+(2) \times \mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$** where $\Gamma_+(2)$ is a free group on 2 generators and $\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z}$ is generated by $-I$.

**(b)** The number of free generators of $\Gamma(N)$ for $N \geq 3$ is:

$$\boxed{r = 1 + \frac{N^3}{12}\prod_{p|N}\left(1 - \frac{1}{p^2}\right)}$$

Examples:
- $N = 3$: $r = 1 + \frac{27 \cdot 8/9}{12} = 1 + 2 = 3$
- $N = 5$: $r = 1 + \frac{125 \cdot 24/25}{12} = 1 + 10 = 11$

$$\boxed{\text{For } \Gamma(2): \text{ } \Gamma_+(2) \text{ has } 2 \text{ free generators}}$$
