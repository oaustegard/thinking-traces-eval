## Final Answer

**(i) Leading 1-point function:**

$$\boxed{G_1(t) = \frac{g}{4m^2} + O(g^2)}$$

The 1-point vanishes at order $g^0$ by Z_2 symmetry ($q \to -q$ is a symmetry of the free action). At order $g^1$, the tadpole diagram (one cubic vertex with one external leg and one self-loop) gives a time-independent contribution proportional to $g/(4m^2)$. Higher orders involve more complex multi-loop diagrams.

**(ii) Connected 2-point function modulo $g^3$:**

$$\boxed{G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_1-t_2|} + O(g^2)}$$

At order $g^0$: the free propagator. At order $g^1$: vanishing by Z_2 symmetry (the cubic interaction is odd, so $g^1$ corrections to even correlators cancel). At order $g^2$: the 1-loop self-energy bubbles and sunset diagrams contribute non-local corrections $\propto e^{-2m|t_1-t_2|}$ and higher (but these are suppressed by $g^2$ and smaller at weak coupling). The leading 2-point remains the free propagator up to $g^2$.
