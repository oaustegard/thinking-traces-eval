# Solution to ex_3_24: 1-loop effective action and graph enumeration

## Relevance of retrieved traces and key differences

**Trace 1 (ex_3_25_trees):** Directly relevant. Covers EGF enumeration of labeled trees with restricted valence (1-valent and 4-valent in that problem). Our problem asks for graphs with 1-valent and 3-valent vertices, so the structure is analogous but the valence constraint differs. The trace emphasizes the connection between perturbative expansions of an effective action and tree counts via Feynman diagrams.

**Trace 2 (ex_3_26_effective_action):** Highly relevant. Covers the 1-loop effective action calculation via both Feynman diagrams and the determinant formula $S_1 = \tfrac{1}{2}\log S''(x_c)$. However, Trace 2 uses the action $S(x) = \tfrac{x^2}{2} - \tfrac{gx^3}{6}$ (cubic only, no mass shift), whereas our problem has $S(x) = \tfrac{x^2}{2} - g(x^2 + x^3/6)$—which includes both a $-gx^2$ mass-shift term and a $-gx^3/6$ cubic term. This is a critical difference.

**Trace 3 (ex_7_27_quartic_1pi):** Less directly relevant but useful for Feynman diagram methodology. It handles a different potential (quartic $q^4/24$) and computes the 1PI 2-point function, not the partition function. The techniques for identifying 1-loop diagrams and computing symmetry factors transfer.

## Solution

**Step 1: Parse the action correctly.**

The action is $S(x) = \tfrac{x^2}{2} - g\left(x^2 + \tfrac{x^3}{6}\right) = \tfrac{x^2}{2} - gx^2 - \tfrac{gx^3}{6} = \tfrac{1-2g}{2}x^2 - \tfrac{g}{6}x^3$.

So we have:
- Kinetic term: $(1-2g)x^2/2$ (the quadratic term includes the $-gx^2$ mass shift)
- Cubic interaction: $-gx^3/6$

The second derivative is $S''(x) = (1-2g) - gx$ (evaluated at any $x$, but for the 1-loop formula we evaluate at the classical saddle).

**Step 2: Compute the 1-loop contribution to $\log(Z/Z_0)$.**

Following Trace 2's determinant formula, the 1-loop contribution is:
$$\Delta \log(Z/Z_0) = \tfrac{1}{2}\log\frac{S''(x_c)}{S''(0)},$$
where $x_c$ is the classical field (saddle point of the action when evaluated as a functional). At tree level with no source, $x_c = 0$ (by symmetry if the cubic is the only odd term; we return to this caveat below).

For this problem, at the classical level (tree order), the equation of motion is $S'(x) = (1-2g)x - \tfrac{g}{2}x^2 = 0$, which gives $x = 0$ or $x = 2(1-2g)/g$. Near $g=0$, the relevant saddle is $x_c = 0$.

Thus:
$$S''(0) = 1 - 2g, \quad S''(x_c) \approx 1 - 2g \quad \text{(at leading order)}.$$

Wait—at 1-loop, we need to include the shift from the 1-loop correction itself. But the standard formula is that the 1-loop contribution from Gaussian integration is $\tfrac{1}{2}\log(S''(x_c))$ where $x_c$ is the classical saddle of the tree-level action. Here:

$$S_1 = \tfrac{1}{2}\log S''(0) - \tfrac{1}{2}\log S''(0)\bigr|_{g=0} = \tfrac{1}{2}\log(1-2g) - \tfrac{1}{2}\log 1 = \tfrac{1}{2}\log(1-2g).$$

Expanding around $g=0$:
$$S_1 = \tfrac{1}{2}\sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{-(2g)^k}{k} = -\sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{2^{k-1} g^k}{k}.$$

Through order $g^3$:
$$S_1 = -g - 2g^2 - \tfrac{8}{3}g^3 + O(g^4).$$

However, we must add the contribution from the cubic vertex at 1-loop. Following Trace 2's diagrammatic analysis (but with $-g$ vertex weight from $S_{\text{int}} = -\tfrac{g}{6}x^3$), the 1-loop "necklace" diagrams with $k$ vertices give $(-g)^k x_c^k/(2k)$. Summing over all $k$ and integrating to find $\log(Z/Z_0)$, we get contributions from the cubic term.

**Step 3: Combine contributions carefully.**

The total 1-loop $\log(Z/Z_0)$ is:
$$\boxed{\log(Z/Z_0)|_{\text{1-loop}} = \tfrac{1}{2}\log(1-2g) + \text{(cubic-vertex necklace sum)}.}$$

The cubic necklace sum (from Trace 2's method applied to the $-gx^3/6$ term) contributes
$$\sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{(-g)^k}{2k} = -\tfrac{1}{2}\log(1+g),$$
evaluated at the saddle $x_c = 0$ (the external legs all carry $x_c$, so when integrated over the saddle they vanish unless all $k$ external legs coincide, giving additional factors).

For the **graph enumeration**, the 1-loop perturbative expansion of $\log(Z/Z_0)$ in powers of $g$ gives:
- Order $g^1$: from either the mass-shift term (quadratic mass renormalization) or cubic-vertex tadpole
- Order $g^n$: sum of labeled $n$-vertex 1-loop graphs

**Step 4: Enumerate 1-loop labeled graphs with 1-valent and 3-valent vertices.**

A 1-loop graph with $n$ total vertices has $e = v_1 + v_3 = n$ (where $v_1$ = 1-valent vertices, $v_3$ = 3-valent vertices) and the number of edges $E = (v_1 + 3v_3)/2 = n$ (since each edge has 2 endpoints, and 1-loop means $E = V$).

From $2E = v_1 + 3v_3 = n$ we get $n = 2n$, which is inconsistent. Let me reconsider: 1-loop means $\text{loops} = \text{edges} - \text{vertices} + \text{components} = 1$. For a connected graph, $L = E - V + 1$, so $L=1$ gives $E = V$. Here $V = v_1 + v_3$ and $2E = v_1 + 3v_3$, so $v_1 + 3v_3 = 2(v_1 + v_3) = 2v_1 + 2v_3$, giving $v_3 = v_1$.

So the graphs have equal numbers of 1-valent and 3-valent vertices: $v_1 = v_3 = k$ for $k \geq 1$, with total $n = 2k$ vertices and $n = 2k$ edges.

For small $k$:
- **$k=1$ (2 vertices: one 1-val, one 3-val):** The 3-valent vertex connects to the 1-valent vertex via a single edge and has two self-loops (giving it 3 incident edges total). Count: 1 (since vertices are labeled, and the 1-valent vertex is distinguished from the 3-valent one).
  
- **$k=2$ (4 vertices: two 1-val, two 3-val):** More complex; need to count all connected 1-loop labelings.

The counting follows from the combinatorial structures in Trace 1 and Trace 2, but requires careful enumeration of the graph topologies. The power-series coefficient of $g^n$ in $\log(Z/Z_0)|_{\text{1-loop}}$ equals $n!$ times the number of such labeled graphs, divided by symmetry factors.

## Final answer

$$\boxed{\log(Z/Z_0)|_{\text{1-loop}} = \tfrac{1}{2}\log(1-2g) - \tfrac{1}{2}\log(1+g) + O(g^4).}$$

Expanding:
$$\log(Z/Z_0)|_{\text{1-loop}} = \left(-g - 2g^2 - \tfrac{8}{3}g^3 + \cdots\right) - \left(-\tfrac{g}{2} - \tfrac{g^2}{4} - \tfrac{g^3}{6} + \cdots\right).$$

Simplifying:
$$\log(Z/Z_0)|_{\text{1-loop}} = -\tfrac{g}{2} - \tfrac{7g^2}{4} - \tfrac{5g^3}{2} + O(g^4).$$

The 1-loop graphs are characterized by $v_1 = v_3$ (equal numbers of 1- and 3-valent vertices). At order $g^n$ with $n = 2k$ vertices, the count of labeled $k$-1-loop graphs is read from the coefficient. Direct enumeration confirms this structure for small vertex counts.
