## Solution

### Setting up the 1-loop formula

The partition function is $Z = \int e^{-S(x)/\hbar} dx$ with $S(x) = x^2/2 - g(x^2 + x^3/6)$. The effective action is $S_{\text{eff}}(x_c) = S(x_c) + \hbar S_1(x_c) + O(\hbar^2)$.

At one-loop (from Trace 2's methodology, the Coleman-Weinberg formula):
$$S_1(x_c) = \frac{1}{2} \log S''(x_c).$$

**Compute $S''(x)$:**
$$S'(x) = x - 2gx - \frac{g x^2}{2} = x(1 - 2g - gx/2),$$
$$S''(x) = 1 - 2g - gx.$$

At the saddle point $x_c$ (which, for zero external source, corresponds to $x_c = 0$ in the vacuum), we have:
$$S''(0) = 1 - 2g.$$

Thus:
$$\log(Z/Z_0)|_{1\text{-loop}} = S_1(0) = \frac{1}{2} \log(1 - 2g).$$

### Expansion to order $g^2$

Expand $\log(1 - 2g)$ as a power series:
$$\log(1 - 2g) = -\sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{(2g)^k}{k} = -2g - 2g^2 - \frac{8}{3}g^3 - \cdots$$

Therefore:
$$S_1(0) = \frac{1}{2}\left(-2g - 2g^2 - \frac{8}{3}g^3 - \cdots\right) = -g - g^2 - \frac{4}{3}g^3 - \cdots$$

**To order $g^2$ (inclusive):**
$$\log(Z/Z_0)|_{1\text{-loop}} = -g - g^2 + O(g^3).$$

### Interpretation as graph counts

The 1-loop contribution arises from 1PI Feynman diagrams. Following Trace 2's approach, the action has a cubic vertex (from $-gx^3/6$) and a mass-shift term (from $-gx^2$). The mass shift modifies the propagator structure.

The effective coupling felt by diagram terms comes from two sources:
1. The cubic vertex directly contributes diagrams with 3-valent vertices (like Trace 2's necklaces).
2. The mass-shift term $-gx^2$ can be viewed as a perturbation that shifts the propagator denominator or appears as a vertex in an alternative diagram language.

At order $g^n$, the coefficient of $g^n$ in $\log(Z/Z_0)|_{1\text{-loop}}$ counts (with appropriate signs and symmetry factors) labeled 1-loop Feynman graphs with $n$ vertices and internal edges equal to vertices (the defining 1-loop property). The presence of both the mass and cubic terms expands the repertoire: some diagrams come purely from the cubic, while others mix mass and cubic contributions.

### Graph enumeration check (order $g^1, g^2$)

**Order $g^1$:** Only the 1-vertex tadpole (one 3-valent vertex with a self-loop and one external line carrying the mass shift). This is consistent with the coefficient $-1$ in $-g - g^2 - \cdots$.

**Order $g^2$:** Contributions include:
- Two-vertex diagrams from the cubic alone (e.g., two 3-valent vertices connected by 2 edges, as in Trace 2's "eye" or higher structures).
- Mixed diagrams involving the mass-shift term and the cubic in tandem.

The coefficient $-1$ at order $g^2$ reflects the combination of all such 1PI topologies weighted by symmetry factors.

For completeness, one could enumerate small-vertex 1-loop labeled graphs directly:
- $n=1$ vertex: tadpole (1 graph).
- $n=2$ vertices: edge diagrams with 2 edges forming cycles (2 non-isomorphic unrooted structures; labeled count is $2 \times 2 = 4$ or similar, adjusted for symmetry).

The exact count depends on how the mass and cubic terms are encoded in the vertex set, but the coefficient $-g^2$ in the log expansion is the canonical answer from the effective-action formula.

## Final answer

$$\boxed{\log(Z/Z_0)|_{1\text{-loop}} = -g - g^2 + O(g^3).}$$

At order $g^n$, the coefficient is the negative of the contribution from all labeled 1-loop Feynman graphs with $v_1 + v_3 = n$ vertices (1-valent and 3-valent) satisfying the 1-loop constraint $E = V$. The presence of the mass-shift term $-gx^2$ in the action modifies which diagrams contribute at each order relative to the cubic-only case (Trace 2), but the overall 1-loop structure — 1PI necklace and loop diagrams — remains the organizing principle.
