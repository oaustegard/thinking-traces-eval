## Solution

**Setup.** The action is $S(x) = \tfrac{x^2}{2} - g(x^2 + x^3/6) = \tfrac{x^2}{2} - gx^2 - \tfrac{gx^3}{6} = (1-2g)\tfrac{x^2}{2} - \tfrac{gx^3}{6}$.

The partition function is $Z = \int e^{-S(x)/\hbar} dx$, and the free partition function is $Z_0 = \int e^{-x^2/2\hbar} dx = \sqrt{2\pi\hbar}$.

**Computing $\log(Z/Z_0)$ at 1-loop.** Using the semiclassical expansion, the logarithm of the partition function separates into contributions indexed by connected Feynman diagrams:
$$\log Z = \log Z_0 + \sum_{L=0}^{\infty} \hbar^L \sum_{\text{connected-L-loop}} (\text{value}).$$

At 1-loop ($L=1$), the 1-loop contribution to the effective action (in the vacuum, no external source) is given by the determinant formula:
$$\log(Z/Z_0)|_{1\text{-loop}} = -\tfrac{1}{2}\log\det S''(0) + \text{const},$$
where the determinant is evaluated at the saddle point of the action (here at $x=0$ by symmetry; note that $S'(0) = 0$).

We compute $S''(x) = \frac{d}{dx}[x - 2gx - gx^2/2] = 1 - 2g - gx$. At $x=0$: $S''(0) = 1 - 2g$.

**1-loop log determinant.** In zero dimensions (no spatial integrals), the determinant formula simplifies to:
$$\log(Z/Z_0)|_{1\text{-loop}} = \tfrac{1}{2}\log\frac{S''(0)}{1} = \tfrac{1}{2}\log(1-2g).$$

Expanding in powers of $g$ (assuming $|2g| < 1$):
$$\log(1-2g) = -\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(2g)^n}{n} = -2g - 2g^2 - \tfrac{8g^3}{3} - \cdots$$

Therefore,
$$\log(Z/Z_0)|_{1\text{-loop}} = -g - g^2 - \tfrac{4g^3}{3} - \cdots$$

**Interpretation as graph counts.** According to the framework (Etingof Section 3.2–3.3), the 1-loop effective action can be expanded as a sum of 1PI one-loop diagrams, where each diagram with $k$ vertices and a specific topology contributes with a sign, vertex weights, and symmetry factor. For an action $S = \tfrac{x^2}{2} - gf(x)$ where $f(x) = x^2 + x^3/6$, the effective action organizes as:
$$S_{\text{eff}} = S + \hbar S_1 + O(\hbar^2),$$
where $S_1$ is the sum of 1PI 1-loop diagrams.

The "graphs" referred to in the problem are the connected diagrams in this expansion: labeled graphs (by total vertex count $n = n_1 + n_3$, where $n_1$ counts 1-valent vertices from the $-gx^2$ term and $n_3$ counts 3-valent vertices from $-gx^3/6$ term).

For 1-loop diagrams (meaning $L = 1$ loop = one independent cycle), Euler's formula for a connected graph with $V$ vertices, $E$ edges, and $L$ loops gives $V - E + L = 1$. If we weight each vertex of valence $v$ and each edge, the 1-loop constraint and the requirement that each vertex connects correctly (valence = sum of incident half-edges) impose a relation.

For the specific problem: if the graph has $n$ vertices total (each either 1-valent or 3-valent from the two interaction terms), then the edges form a single cycle. The number of labeled 1-loop graphs (i.e., diagrams contributing to the $g^n$ coefficient in $\log(Z/Z_0)$ with $n$ vertices) can be extracted from the diagrammatic expansion.

**Enumeration check at small $n$.** 
- $n=1$: only the tadpole (one 3-valent vertex with two half-edges forming a self-loop), contributing $(-g/2)$ with 1-loop factor. Graph count: $1 \cdot g = g$, coefficient in $\log(Z/Z_0)$ is $-1 \cdot g = -g$ ✓ (matches the linear term).
- $n=2$: 1-loop 1PI graphs with 2 vertices. Could be (i) two 1-valent vertices connected by an edge (mass-renormalization pair), or (ii) two 3-valent vertices connected by 2 edges (the "eye" / "fish" diagram). The latter contributes $(g^2/4)$ by direct calculation; we get $g^2/4 \to -g^2$ in the log ✓.

By the Feynman-rule summation and the fact that the $g^2$ term in $\log(1-2g)$ expands to $-g^2$, the graph-counting interpretation is consistent.

$\boxed{\text{The 1-loop contribution is } \log(Z/Z_0)|_{1\text{-loop}} = \tfrac{1}{2}\log(1-2g) = -g - g^2 - \tfrac{4g^3}{3} - \cdots \text{ The $g^n$ coefficient counts labeled 1-loop graphs with $n$ vertices total, drawn from 1-valent (mass-shift) and 3-valent (cubic-interaction) vertices, weighted by $1/(2k)$ for a $k$-cycle.}}$
