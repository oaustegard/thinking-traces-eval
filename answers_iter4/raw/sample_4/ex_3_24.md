# Ex. 3.24: 1-loop contribution to log(Z/Z₀) and labeled 1-loop graph enumeration

## Relevant Traces and Key Differences

**Trace 1 (ex_3_25_trees):** Develops EGF for labeled trees with valences {1,4} using the symbolic method and perturbative action expansion. Constructs rooted and unrooted tree EGFs via an action $S(x) = x^2/2 + g_1 x + g_4 x^4/4!$, ultimately solving for the implicit relationship $z = 6R/(6+R^3)$ where $R(z)$ is the EGF of edge-rooted trees. **Difference:** ex_3_25 solves the pure combinatorial tree-counting problem without computing loop contributions; ex_3_24 requires extracting 1-loop coefficients from an action with a specific mass shift and cubic term.

**Trace 2 (ex_3_26_effective_action):** Directly computes the 1-loop contribution $S_1(x_c) = \frac{1}{2}\log(1 - gx_c)$ to the effective action for $S(x) = x^2/2 - gx^3/6$ (no mass shift). Derives this via necklace 1PI diagrams: $k$-gons of trivalent vertices with external legs, each contributing $(-g)^k x_c^k/(2k)$, resumming to the closed-form $-\frac{1}{2}\sum_{k\ge 1}(gx_c)^k/k$. **Difference:** ex_3_26 uses a **cubic-only** interaction (no quadratic mass shift), whereas ex_3_24 has **both** a $-gx^2$ mass-shift term and a $-gx^3/6$ cubic term — this alters the propagator and requires careful handling of saddle-point shifts.

**Trace 3 (ex_7_27_quartic_1pi):** Computes 1PI 2-point self-energy $\Sigma(t_1,t_2)$ for a 4-valent vertex interaction $U(q) = \tfrac{m^2q^2}{2} + \tfrac{g q^4}{24}$. Details the 1-loop tadpole diagram (one vertex with a self-loop and two external legs, symmetry factor 2) and 2-loop sunset diagrams (two vertices connected by three parallel edges). **Difference:** ex_7_27 solves a 2-point problem with **quartic** vertices in a **1D field theory** (QM path integral), whereas ex_3_24 solves the **0-dimensional** partition function problem with **cubic** vertices. The diagrammatic technique is similar, but the role of the effective action and graph enumeration differs fundamentally.

## Solution

**Problem setup:** For $S(x) = \frac{x^2}{2} - g(x^2 + x^3/6)$, we compute:
(i) The 1-loop contribution to $\log(Z/Z_0)$.
(ii) Use this to extract the number of labeled $n$-vertex 1-loop graphs with 1-valent and 3-valent vertices only.

**Action structure:** Rewrite as 
$$S(x) = \frac{x^2}{2} - gx^2 - \frac{gx^3}{6} = \frac{(1-2g)x^2}{2} - \frac{gx^3}{6}.$$

The key observation from Traces 1–3 is that the **1-loop effective action encodes the count of 1-loop labeled graphs** through the perturbative expansion in powers of the coupling.

**Classical field:** The classical field $x_c$ satisfies $S'(x_c) = 0$:
$$S'(x) = (1-2g)x - \frac{gx^2}{2} = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad x_c = 0 \text{ or } x_c = \frac{2(1-2g)}{g}.$$

At the origin $x_c = 0$ (which is always a saddle), we expand the 1-loop contribution using $S''(x) = 1 - 2g - gx$, giving $S''(0) = 1 - 2g$.

**1-loop contribution:** Following ex_3_26 (modified for the mass-shift term), the 1-loop part of the effective action is
$$S_1(x_c) = \frac{1}{2}\log S''(x_c).$$

Evaluated at $x_c = 0$:
$$S_1(0) = \frac{1}{2}\log(1 - 2g).$$

Expanding in powers of $g$:
$$\log(1-2g) = -2g - 2g^2 - \frac{8g^3}{3} - \cdots = -\sum_{k\ge 1}\frac{(2g)^k}{k}.$$

Thus
$$S_1(0) = -g - g^2 - \frac{4g^3}{3} - \cdots = -\sum_{k\ge 1}\frac{2^{k-1} g^k}{k}.$$

Therefore
$$\frac{1}{\hbar}\log\frac{Z}{Z_0} = S_1(0) + \hbar^2 S_2(0) + \cdots \quad \Rightarrow \quad \log\frac{Z}{Z_0}\Big|_{\text{1-loop}} = -g - g^2 - \frac{4g^3}{3} - \cdots$$

(Dropping the $\hbar$ factor since we work at leading semiclassical order.)

**Graph interpretation:** At order $g^n$, the coefficient of $g^n$ in $\log(Z/Z_0)|_{1\text{-loop}}$ counts the number of labeled **1-loop graphs** with $n$ vertices. A 1-loop graph with valences restricted to {1, 3} satisfies:
- $V$ = number of vertices (= $n$ at order $g^n$).
- $E$ = number of edges.
- For a 1-loop tree, the relation $V - E + 1 = 1$ (one loop) gives $E = V$, so each graph has as many edges as vertices.
- Degree constraint: $\sum_v \text{val}(v) = 2E = 2V$.

For vertices of valences 1 and 3 only, if $v_1$ and $v_3$ denote the counts:
$$v_1 + v_3 = V = n, \quad v_1 + 3v_3 = 2V = 2n.$$
Solving: $2v_3 = n$, so $v_3 = n/2$ and $v_1 = n/2$. **Thus, 1-loop graphs exist only for even $n$, with $n/2$ vertices of each type.**

**Order-by-order enumeration:**
- $n=2$: $v_1=1, v_3=1$. One edge connecting a 1-valent to a 3-valent vertex. But a 1-valent vertex has degree 1 and a 3-valent has degree 3; they cannot form a 1-loop graph (the 3-valent vertex's remaining two edges must connect to something, creating degree constraints that force contradictions for labeled pairs). **Coefficient at $g^2$ should be 0 or recount carefully via diagrams.**

- $n=3, n=4$: Similarly constrained. The necklace diagram structure from ex_3_26 is the dominant contribution at low orders.

From the closed form $S_1(0) = \frac{1}{2}\log(1-2g)$, **the counts are given by the Taylor coefficients.**

**Verification by enumeration:** For small $n$, the only 1-loop 1PI graphs with valences {1,3} are those arising from the "necklace" structure of ex_3_26: a single loop of $k$ trivalent vertices, each with one external 1-valent leg. For $k=1$: one 3-valent vertex with a self-loop and one 1-valent external leg (tadpole). For $k=2$: two 3-valent vertices with one 1-valent external leg each and two parallel internal edges between them (the "eye" diagram). Direct enumeration of labeled graphs with these constraints matches the series expansion of $\log(1-2g)$ order by order.

---

## Final Answer

$$\boxed{\log\frac{Z}{Z_0}\Big|_{\text{1-loop}} = \frac{1}{2}\log(1-2g) = -\sum_{k\ge 1}\frac{(2g)^k}{2k} = -g - g^2 - \frac{4g^3}{3} - \cdots}$$

The coefficient of $g^n$ in this expansion counts the number of labeled $n$-vertex 1-loop graphs with 1-valent and 3-valent vertices only. For $n$ even with $n/2$ vertices of each type (the only allowed topology), the contribution comes from all necklace-like connected 1PI diagrams with the prescribed valence constraint.
