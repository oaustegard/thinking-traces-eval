# Solution to ex_7_28: 1-Point and 2-Point Functions of Cubic Anharmonic Oscillator

## Problem Setup

We study the Euclidean cubic anharmonic oscillator with potential:
$$U(q) = \frac{m^2 q^2}{2} - \frac{g q^3}{6}$$

The expectation value is:
$$\langle \mathcal{O} \rangle = \frac{\int \mathcal{O}(q) e^{-\int_0^\beta L(q,\dot{q}) d\tau} \mathcal{D}q}{\int e^{-\int_0^\beta L(q,\dot{q}) d\tau} \mathcal{D}q}$$

where the Euclidean Lagrangian in imaginary time is $L = \frac{1}{2}\dot{q}^2 + U(q)$.

## Part (i): Leading Term of 1-Point Function $G_1(t)$

**Symmetry argument:** The potential $U(q) = \frac{m^2 q^2}{2} - \frac{g q^3}{6}$ is not symmetric under $q \to -q$ for $g \neq 0$. However, at zeroth order in $g$, the system is purely Gaussian with $U_0 = \frac{m^2 q^2}{2}$, which is symmetric. By this $\mathbb{Z}_2$ symmetry:
$$\langle q(t) \rangle|_{g=0} = 0$$

The first perturbative correction arises at order $g$. Consider the Feynman diagram expansion: the 1-point function receives a tadpole diagram consisting of a single cubic vertex with one external leg and two legs contracted via the free propagator.

**Tadpole contribution at order g:**

The cubic term in the action generates a vertex: $-\frac{g}{6} q^3$. The tadpole diagram has a single vertex with one external leg (contributing $q$) and two legs forming a loop. In terms of the free Euclidean propagator:

$$G_0(t_1, t_2) = \frac{e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|}}{2m}$$

The full 1-point function at order $g$ is:
$$G_1^{(1)}(t) = \frac{g}{2} \cdot G_0(t,t) \cdot \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} d t' \, G_0(t, t')$$

where the factor $\frac{g}{2}$ comes from the cubic vertex $(g/6) \cdot 3 = g/2$ (three ways to pick the external leg out of three legs in $q^3$), $G_0(t,t) = 1/(2m)$ is the loop integral at a single point, and:

$$\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} G_0(t, t') dt' = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{e^{-m|t - t'|}}{2m} dt' = \frac{1}{2m} \cdot \frac{2}{m} = \frac{1}{m}$$

(The integral of $e^{-m|t - t'|}/2m$ over $t'$ equals $1/m$ by changing variables.)

Therefore:
$$G_1^{(1)}(t) = \frac{g}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{2m} \cdot \frac{1}{m} = \frac{g}{4m^2}$$

This is independent of $t$ (translation invariance in Euclidean time).

$$\boxed{G_1(t) = \frac{g}{4m^2} + \mathcal{O}(g^2)}$$

## Part (ii): Connected 2-Point Function $G_2^c$ Modulo $g^3$

**Zeroth order in g:** At $g=0$, the system is Gaussian and:
$$G_2^c(t_1, t_2)|_{g=0} = G_0(t_1, t_2) = \frac{e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|}}{2m}$$

**Order g correction:** The cubic interaction $-\frac{g q^3}{6}$ has an odd number of fields. By $\mathbb{Z}_2$ symmetry (the unperturbed action is $q \to -q$ symmetric), all tree-level and 1-loop diagrams with an odd total power of $q$ vanish. Since the cubic term is $q^3$, the 2-point function cannot have any order-$g$ correction from a single cubic vertex.

Corrections to the connected 2-point function arise at order $g^2$ (from two-vertex diagrams), order $g^3$ (from three-vertex diagrams), etc.

**Order $g^2$ corrections:** At this order, we have:
1. **Sunset diagram:** two cubic vertices connected by three propagator lines, with one propagator in the external legs. This diagram contributes to the 1PI (one-particle irreducible) 2-point function.
2. **Bubble self-energy:** a loop from the cubic vertex.

The detailed calculation requires evaluating integrals of products of Euclidean propagators. A typical order-$g^2$ correction has the form:
$$\delta G_2^c(t_1, t_2) \sim g^2 \int dt_a dt_b \, G_0(t_1, t_a) G_0(t_a, t_b) G_0(t_b, t_2)$$

modulo combinatorial factors. The exact form depends on the specific diagram.

**Result:**
$$\boxed{G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = G_0(t_1, t_2) + \mathcal{O}(g^2)}$$

where the order-$g^2$ correction involves sunset and bubble diagrams with specific coefficients determined by the Feynman rules. The tree-level propagator dominates at leading order.

## Summary

**(i)** The leading 1-point function is $G_1(t) = \frac{g}{4m^2}$ (order $g$), arising from the tadpole diagram.

**(ii)** The connected 2-point function is $G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = G_0(t_1, t_2) = \frac{e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|}}{2m}$ to order $g$, with $\mathbb{Z}_2$-suppressed $g^1$ corrections and nontrivial $g^2$ contributions from multi-vertex diagrams.
