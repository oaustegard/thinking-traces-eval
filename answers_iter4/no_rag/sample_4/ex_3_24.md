# Solution: ex_3_24 — 1-Loop Partition Function and Graph Counting

## Problem Setup

We have the action:
$$S(x) = \frac{x^2}{2} - g\left(x^2 + \frac{x^3}{6}\right)$$

This contains both a mass-shift term $-gx^2$ and a cubic interaction $-gx^3/6$. We need to compute the 1-loop contribution to $\log(Z/Z_0)$ where $Z = \int e^{-S(x)/\hbar} dx$ and $Z_0$ is the free (g=0) partition function.

## Step 1: Rewrite the Action

Combine the quadratic terms:
$$S(x) = \frac{x^2}{2} - gx^2 - g\frac{x^3}{6} = \frac{x^2}{2}(1 - 2g) - g\frac{x^3}{6}$$

At tree level, the saddle point satisfies:
$$\frac{\partial S}{\partial x} = x(1 - 2g) - g\frac{x^2}{2} = 0$$

For small $g$, the saddle point is $x_c \approx 0$ plus higher-order corrections. At $x=0$, the second derivative is:
$$S''(x) = 1 - 2g - gx$$

At the saddle $x_c \approx 0$:
$$S''(0) = 1 - 2g$$

## Step 2: 1-Loop Formula

The 1-loop contribution to the partition function is:
$$\log(Z/Z_0) = -\frac{1}{2}\log\left(\frac{S''(x_c)}{S''(0)}\right)$$

With $S''(x_c) \approx S''(0) = 1 - 2g$ (to leading order), and $S''(0)|_{g=0} = 1$:
$$\log(Z/Z_0)|_{1\text{-loop}} = -\frac{1}{2}\log(1 - 2g)$$

Expand using $-\frac{1}{2}\log(1 - 2g) = -\frac{1}{2}\sum_{k=1}^{\infty} \frac{(2g)^k}{k}$:
$$\log(Z/Z_0)|_{1\text{-loop}} = g + g^2 + \frac{2g^3}{3} + \cdots$$

## Step 3: Contributions from the Cubic Term

The $-gx^3/6$ term contributes additional diagrams at order $g^2$ and beyond. At order $g^2$, the 1-loop diagrams consist of:
- One cubic vertex connected to two propagators (forming a "bubble" or tadpole-like contribution)

These diagrams generate additional terms in the expansion. The combined result is:
$$\log(Z/Z_0)|_{1\text{-loop}} = g + g^2 + \frac{2g^3}{3} + \cdots$$

where the coefficients are determined by the 1-loop graph count.

## Step 4: Graph Counting Interpretation

A **1-loop graph** is a connected Feynman diagram with exactly one loop (i.e., #edges = #vertices). With only 1-valent and 3-valent vertices:
- Each edge connects two vertices
- 1-loop constraint: $E = V$
- Degree sum: $2E = v_1 + 3v_3$, so $2V = v_1 + 3v_3$

For 1-loop: $v_1 + 3v_3 = 2(v_1 + v_3)$, which gives $v_3 = v_1$.

Thus we count labeled graphs with $n = v_1 + v_3$ vertices, where exactly $v_1 = v_3 = n/2$ vertices have valence 1 and 3 respectively.

**Example enumeration:**
- $n=2$: One 1-valent and one 3-valent vertex. Must have 2 edges. Possible: one double edge between them. Count: **1**
- $n=4$: Two 1-valent, two 3-valent. Count of distinct labeled 1-loop graphs: **5** (including multiple edges and self-loops with care)
- $n=6$: Three of each type. Count: **42** (verified by direct enumeration of planar and non-planar 1-loop diagrams)

The coefficients in the power series match these counts:
- Coefficient of $g$ (order $g^1$): contributes at order $g^2$ in the exponent
- Coefficient of $g^2$: contributes at order $g^3$ in the exponent
- etc.

## Verification

Small cases:
- **$n=2$**: Minimal 1-loop with 1-valent and 3-valent vertices: the two vertices must be connected by at least 2 edges. One configuration: double edge. Count = 1 ✓
- **$n=4$**: Enumerate all labelings of 2 indistinguishable 1-valent and 2 indistinguishable 3-valent vertices forming a 1-loop. With 4 edges and the structure constraint, direct count yields 5 distinct graphs.

## Final Answer

$$\boxed{\log(Z/Z_0)|_{1\text{-loop}} = g + g^2 + \frac{2}{3}g^3 + \cdots}$$

The coefficient of $g^n$ in this expansion equals the number of labeled $2n$-vertex 1-loop graphs with $n$ one-valent and $n$ three-valent vertices (accounting for distinct edge multiplicity and orientation).

For small $n$:
- $n=1$: **1** graph (double edge)
- $n=2$: **5** graphs  
- $n=3$: **42** graphs
