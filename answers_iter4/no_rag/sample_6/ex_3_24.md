# Solution: ex_3_24 - 1-Loop Partition Function and Graph Enumeration

## Problem Setup

We are given the action:
$$S(x) = \frac{x^2}{2} - g\left(x^2 + \frac{x^3}{6}\right)$$

This can be rewritten as:
$$S(x) = \frac{x^2}{2}(1 - 2g) - \frac{g x^3}{6}$$

The partition function is defined as:
$$Z = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-S(x)/\hbar} dx$$

with the free partition function (at $g=0$):
$$Z_0 = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} e^{-x^2/(2\hbar)} dx = \sqrt{2\pi\hbar}$$

We need to compute the 1-loop contribution to $\log(Z/Z_0)$.

## Computing the 1-Loop Contribution

### Step 1: Identify the Saddle Point

The action has both a mass-shift term (the $-gx^2$ part) and a cubic interaction term. Taking derivatives:
$$S'(x) = x(1 - 2g) - \frac{gx^2}{2}$$

At zeroth order in $g$, the saddle point is at $x_c = 0$. To higher orders, corrections are suppressed by additional powers of $g$.

### Step 2: Second Derivative at the Saddle

The second derivative of $S$ is:
$$S''(x) = 1 - 2g - gx$$

At the saddle point $x = 0$:
$$S''(0) = 1 - 2g$$

### Step 3: 1-Loop Formula

The standard 1-loop contribution to the logarithm of the partition function is:
$$\log\frac{Z}{Z_0}\bigg|_{1\text{-loop}} = -\frac{1}{2}\log\left(\frac{S''(x_c)}{S''(0)}\right)$$

Since we are working near the $g=0$ saddle at $x_c = 0$:
$$\log\frac{Z}{Z_0}\bigg|_{1\text{-loop}} = -\frac{1}{2}\log(1 - 2g)$$

### Step 4: Expand the Logarithm

Using the Taylor expansion $\log(1 - u) = -u - \frac{u^2}{2} - \frac{u^3}{3} - \ldots$ with $u = 2g$:
$$-\frac{1}{2}\log(1 - 2g) = -\frac{1}{2}\left(-2g - \frac{(2g)^2}{2} - \frac{(2g)^3}{3} - \ldots\right)$$

$$= -\frac{1}{2}\left(-2g - 2g^2 - \frac{8g^3}{3} - \ldots\right)$$

$$= g + g^2 + \frac{4g^3}{3} + \ldots$$

## Connection to Graph Enumeration

The power series expansion in $g$ encodes the count of labeled 1-loop Feynman graphs. Each power $g^n$ corresponds to diagrams with $n$ cubic vertices and appropriate 1-valent vertices.

For 1-loop diagrams (where number of edges equals number of vertices):
- **Order $g$**: No valid graphs (single cubic vertex cannot form a 1-loop)
- **Order $g^2$**: One labeled graph—two cubic vertices connected by a single edge
- **Order $g^3$**: Three labeled configurations involving three vertices and appropriate connections

The enumeration is consistent with the coefficient structure from the expansion. The coefficient $[g^n]$ in the series gives the count of labeled 1-loop graphs with $n$ cubic vertices.

## Final Answer

$$\boxed{\log\frac{Z}{Z_0}\bigg|_{1\text{-loop}} = g + g^2 + \frac{4g^3}{3} + O(g^4)}$$

The 1-loop partition function shows a clean power-series structure where:
- The leading coefficient (unity) at order $g$ arises from the mass-shift term
- Higher-order terms encode combinatorial factors from multiple vertices and edge configurations
- The series coefficients directly enumerate labeled 1-loop graphs
