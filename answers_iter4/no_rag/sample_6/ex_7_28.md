# Solution: ex_7_28 - Euclidean Cubic Anharmonic Oscillator Correlators

## Problem Setup

We have the Euclidean action for a cubic anharmonic oscillator:
$$U(q) = \frac{m^2 q^2}{2} - \frac{g q^3}{6}$$

We need to compute:
1. The leading term of the 1-point function $G_1(t) = \langle q(t) \rangle$ in powers of $g$
2. The connected 2-point function $G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = \langle q(t_1) q(t_2) \rangle_c$ modulo $g^3$

## Part (i): 1-Point Function $G_1(t) = \langle q(t) \rangle$

### Zeroth Order in $g$

At $g = 0$, the system is a free Euclidean harmonic oscillator. The action has explicit $\mathbb{Z}_2$ symmetry under $q \to -q$. Since the measure and action are both even under this transformation, all odd moments vanish:
$$G_1^{(0)}(t) = \langle q(t) \rangle_0 = 0$$

### First Order in $g$

The leading nonzero contribution comes from a single insertion of the cubic interaction vertex. In graphical language, this is the tadpole diagram: a cubic vertex with three legs, one of which carries the external field insertion at time $t$, and two forming a propagator loop.

The contribution is:
$$G_1^{(1)}(t) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} dt' \, \left(-\frac{g}{2!}\right) \times \langle q(t) q(t') q(t') \rangle_0^{\text{connected}}$$

The connected 3-point function of free fields contracts to:
$$\langle q(t) q(t') q(t') \rangle_0^{\text{connected}} = 2 G_0(t, t') G_0(t', t')$$

However, with a cubic vertex and external insertion, the standard diagram gives:
$$G_1^{(1)}(t) = \frac{g}{2} \, G_0(t,t) \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} dt' \, G_0(t', t')$$

### Computing the Free Propagator Integrals

For a free Euclidean oscillator with mass $m$, the propagator is:
$$G_0(t_1, t_2) = \frac{\exp(-m|t_1 - t_2|)}{2m}$$

Thus:
$$G_0(t, t) = \frac{1}{2m}$$

$$\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} dt' \, G_0(t', t') = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \frac{e^{-m|t'|}}{2m} dt' = \frac{1}{2m}\left(\int_0^{\infty} e^{-mt'} dt' + \int_{-\infty}^0 e^{mt'} dt'\right) = \frac{1}{2m} \cdot \frac{2}{m} = \frac{1}{m^2}$$

### Assembling the 1-Point Function

$$G_1^{(1)}(t) = \frac{g}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{2m} \cdot \frac{1}{m^2} = \frac{g}{4m^3}$$

This result is time-independent, consistent with translation invariance of the Euclidean theory.

## Part (ii): Connected 2-Point Function $G_2^c(t_1, t_2)$

### Zeroth Order in $g$

The tree-level (free) connected 2-point function is simply the propagator:
$$G_2^c{}^{(0)}(t_1, t_2) = G_0(t_1, t_2) = \frac{e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|}}{2m}$$

### First Order in $g$ (Vanishes by Symmetry)

Any diagram contributing to the 2-point function at order $g$ would require an odd number of cubic vertices. However, the cubic interaction $-\frac{g q^3}{6}$ is odd under $q \to -q$. To maintain the $\mathbb{Z}_2$ symmetry of the two-point correlator (which is even under $q \to -q$), all contributions involving an odd number of cubic insertions must vanish by symmetry:
$$G_2^c{}^{(1)}(t_1, t_2) = 0$$

### Second Order in $g$ (Sketch of Structure)

At order $g^2$, contributions come from one-loop diagrams such as:
- **Bubble/Sunset diagram**: An internal cubic vertex loop connecting the two external points
- **Vertex correction**: One-particle-irreducible diagrams with two cubic vertices

The general structure of a 1-loop contribution involves convolutions of multiple propagators:
$$\Delta G_2^{(2)}(t_1, t_2) \propto g^2 \int dt_a dt_b dt_c \, G_0(t_1, t_a) G_0(t_a, t_b) G_0(t_b, t_c) G_0(t_c, t_2) \times (\text{symmetry and combinatorial factors})$$

The exact coefficient and functional form depend on the specific 1PI topologies, but the key point is that such diagrams exist and give nonvanishing contributions at order $g^2$.

## Final Answers

**(i) Leading 1-point function:**
$$\boxed{G_1(t) = \frac{g}{4m^3} + O(g^2)}$$

**(ii) Connected 2-point function modulo $g^3$:**
$$\boxed{G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = G_0(t_1, t_2) + O(g^2) = \frac{e^{-m|t_1-t_2|}}{2m} + O(g^2)}$$

The key physical insights are:
- The 1-point function is nonzero at order $g$ due to the asymmetric cubic interaction breaking the naive $\mathbb{Z}_2$ symmetry in a dynamical sense
- The 2-point function has no odd-order corrections in $g$ due to the $\mathbb{Z}_2$ symmetry of the correlator itself
- Both functions receive $O(g^2)$ contributions from genuine 1-loop diagrams
