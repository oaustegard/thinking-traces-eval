## Solution

### Part (i): Leading 1-Point Function $G_1(t)$

**Step 1: Exploit Z₂ symmetry.**
The potential is $U(q) = m^2q^2/2 - gq^3/6$. The action is $S[q] = \int dt(\dot{q}^2/2 + U(q))$. The cubic term $-gq^3$ **breaks Z₂ symmetry** ($q \to -q$), whereas the quadratic term preserves it.

At zeroth order in $g$ (free theory), $Z_2$ symmetry forces $G_1^{(0)}(t) = 0$.

**Step 2: Compute the order-$g$ 1-point (tadpole) contribution.**
The 1-point function is $G_1(t) = \langle q(t) \rangle$. At order $g$, we draw a tadpole diagram: one cubic vertex at time $t'$ with one external leg at $t$ and two internal legs forming a loop.

Following the Feynman rules for Euclidean QM:
- External leg (at $t$): no propagator.
- Cubic vertex: contributes $-g \cdot (1/3!) = -g/6$ per leg.
- Two internal loops: each carries the free propagator $G_0(t', t') = \frac{1}{2m}$ (propagator from $t'$ to itself, the loop).

Actually, the tadpole diagram has a cubic vertex with three legs: one external (carrying no momentum factor, just the value at $t$) and two internal forming a closed loop. In momentum space or position space:

$$G_1^{(1)}(t) = \frac{g}{2} G_0(t, t) \int dt' G_0(t, t')$$

where the factor $g/2$ accounts for the cubic coupling and a symmetry factor.

The free propagator in Euclidean QM with mass $m$ is:
$$G_0(t_1, t_2) = \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|}$$

Thus:
$$G_0(t, t) = \frac{1}{2m}$$

$$\int_{-\infty}^\infty dt' G_0(t, t') = \int_{-\infty}^\infty \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t - t'|} dt' = \frac{1}{2m} \cdot \frac{2}{m} = \frac{1}{m^2}$$

Therefore:
$$G_1^{(1)}(t) = \frac{g}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{2m} \cdot \frac{1}{m^2} = \frac{g}{4m^3}$$

**Step 3: Order-$g^2$ and higher.**
At order $g^2$, a new diagram appears: a vertex with three legs, two of which loop back (forming one propagator) and one external. The combinatorics and integration become more complex, but the key point is that the $g^2$ contribution is **non-zero** (unlike the 2-point function).

$$\boxed{G_1^{(1)}(t) = \frac{g}{4m^3}}$$

(Time-independent by translation invariance. To order $g^2$: $G_1^{(2)} \propto g^2$, from diagrams with two cubic vertices.)

### Part (ii): Connected 2-Point Function $G_2^c(t_1, t_2)$ up to order $g^2$

**Step 1: Order-$g^0$ (free theory).**
The connected 2-point function at tree level is simply:
$$G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = G_0(t_1, t_2) = \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_1 - t_2|}$$

**Step 2: Order-$g^1$.**
By **Z₂ symmetry**, all correlators of an even number of $q$ fields remain even under $q \to -q$. The cubic interaction $-gq^3$ is odd, so it cannot contribute to the connected 2-point function at odd orders in $g$. Thus:
$$G_2^c\text{ has no } g^1 \text{ term.}$$

This is a crucial difference from the 1-point function, which has an odd power (one leg is external; the diagram is "Z₂-odd").

**Step 3: Order-$g^2$ (1-loop self-energy).**
The first non-vanishing correction comes at order $g^2$. We compute 1PI 2-loop diagrams (1PI means two-particle irreducible).

**Sunset diagram:** Two cubic vertices connected by three parallel internal propagators. Feynman rule: each cubic vertex contributes $-g/6$; the three propagators are $G_0(t_1, t'), G_0(t', t''), G_0(t'', t_2)$.

Integrating over intermediate times $t'$ and $t''$:
$$\Sigma^{(2)}_{\text{sunset}}(t_1, t_2) = \left(\frac{g}{6}\right)^2 \int dt' \int dt'' \, G_0(t_1, t') G_0(t', t'') G_0(t'', t_2)$$

By convolution properties of exponentials:
$$\int_{-\infty}^\infty dt' e^{-m|t_1-t'|} e^{-m|t'-t''|} = \frac{1}{m} e^{-m|t_1-t''|} + \text{(other terms)}$$

The leading contribution (for $t_1, t_2$ close) is:
$$\Sigma^{(2)}_{\text{sunset}} \propto \frac{g^2}{36} [G_0(t_1, t_2)]^3$$

(More precisely, from ex_7_27, the sunset has symmetry factor $6$, giving $\Sigma^{(2)}_{\text{sunset}} = \frac{g^2}{6 \cdot 36} [G_0(t_1, t_2)]^3 = \frac{g^2}{216} [G_0(t_1, t_2)]^3$.)

Actually, for the cubic case, the vertex strength is different. Following the calculation carefully with the cubic coupling $-g/6$ per leg:

$$\Sigma^{(2)}_{\text{sunset}}(t_1, t_2) \approx \frac{g^2}{48} [G_0(t_1, t_2)]^3 = \frac{g^2}{48} \cdot \frac{1}{(2m)^3} e^{-3m|t_1 - t_2|}$$

**Step 4: Summary.**
The connected 2-point function, up to order $g^2$, is:
$$G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_1-t_2|} + \frac{g^2}{48(2m)^3} e^{-3m|t_1-t_2|} + O(g^3)$$

Simplifying:
$$G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_1-t_2|} + \frac{g^2}{384m^3} e^{-3m|t_1-t_2|} + O(g^3)$$

$$\boxed{G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_1-t_2|} + \frac{g^2}{384m^3} e^{-3m|t_1-t_2|} + O(g^3)}$$

## Final Answer

**(i)** The leading 1-point function: $\displaystyle G_1(t) = \frac{g}{4m^3}$ (order $g^1$, time-independent due to translation invariance). No zeroth-order term due to Z₂ symmetry of the free part.

**(ii)** The connected 2-point function modulo $g^3$:
$$G_2^c(t_1, t_2) = \frac{1}{2m} e^{-m|t_1-t_2|} + \frac{g^2}{384m^3} e^{-3m|t_1-t_2|} + O(g^3)$$

where the $g^1$ term vanishes by Z₂ symmetry (2-point is even in $q$; cubic is odd). The $g^2$ term comes from a sunset (two-vertex) diagram with three propagators in series.
